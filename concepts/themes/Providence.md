# Providence

God's continuous governance, preservation, and direction of all creation toward His purposes. Providence means that history is not random but guided — the sovereign God works "all things after the counsel of His will" (Eph 1:11).

## Preservation

God sustains what He creates:
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Colossians/Colossians 1#v17|Col 1:17]] — "In Him all things hold together"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 1#v3|Heb 1:3]] — "He upholds all things by the word of His power"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 104#v27|Ps 104:27-30]] — All creatures look to God for provision
- Neh 9:6 — "You preserve all of them"

## Governance

God directs history toward His purposes:
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 8#v28|Rom 8:28]] — "God causes all things to work together for good"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ephesians/Ephesians 1#v11|Eph 1:11]] — "Predestined according to His purpose"
- Prov 16:9 — "The mind of man plans his way, but the LORD directs his steps"
- Gen 50:20 — "You meant evil against me, but God meant it for good"

## Providence in Biblical Narrative

| Event | Human action | Divine purpose |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Joseph's slavery | Brothers' jealousy | Preservation of Israel (Gen 50:20) |
| Pharaoh's hardness | Royal stubbornness | Display of God's power (Exod 9:16) |
| Assyria's invasion | Imperial aggression | Rod of God's anger (Isa 10:5-7) |
| Cross of Christ | Human injustice | Salvation of the world (Acts 2:23) |

## The Cross as Supreme Providence

[[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 2#v23|Acts 2:23]] — "This Man, delivered over by the predetermined plan and foreknowledge of God, you nailed to a cross by the hands of godless men." The greatest human evil becomes the greatest divine good — the pattern of all providence.

## Canonical Arc

God provides (Gen 22:14) → God rules through kings and empires → God works through exile and restoration → God accomplishes salvation through suffering → God brings all things to consummation (Rev 21-22).
