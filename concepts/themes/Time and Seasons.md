# Time and Seasons

God creates time (Gen 1:3-5) and fills it with meaning through sacred rhythms, appointed festivals, and prophetic markers. Biblical time is not cyclical (as in ancient Near Eastern mythology) but linear and purposeful — moving from creation to consummation.

## The Creation of Time

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v3|Gen 1:3-5]] — Day and night: the first temporal [[concepts/hebrew/H914 - badal|separation]]
- Gen 1:14-19 — Sun, moon, and stars for "[[concepts/hebrew/H226 - ot|signs]] and for [[concepts/hebrew/H4150 - moed|seasons]] and for [[concepts/hebrew/H3117 - yom|days]] and years"
- The seven-day week culminates in Sabbath (Gen 2:1-3) — time is structured by rest

## Appointed Times (Moedim)

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H4150 - moed|מוֹעֵד]] (*moed*, "appointed time") designates the liturgical calendar:

| Festival | Time | Significance |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Passover | Spring (Nisan 14) | Redemption from Egypt |
| Weeks/Pentecost | 50 days after Passover | Firstfruits; the Spirit poured out |
| Trumpets | Fall (Tishri 1) | Assembly; the coming king |
| Day of Atonement | Tishri 10 | National cleansing |
| Tabernacles | Tishri 15-21 | God dwelling with His people |

## Kairos and Chronos

The NT distinguishes between chronological time and significant time:
- χρόνος (*chronos*) — sequential, measured time
- καιρός (*kairos*) — the decisive moment, the right time
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Mark/Mark 1#v15|Mark 1:15]] — "The time (kairos) is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Galatians/Galatians 4#v4|Gal 4:4]] — "When the fullness of the time came, God sent forth His Son"

## Eternity

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H5769 - olam|עוֹלָם]] (*olam*) stretches in both directions — the "everlasting" God (Gen 21:33), the "everlasting" covenant (Gen 17:7). The NT anticipates the end of temporal time and the beginning of unending life with God (Rev 22:5).

## Canonical Arc

Time created (Gen 1:3-5) → Sabbath established (Gen 2:1-3) → Liturgical calendar (Lev 23) → Prophetic time markers (Dan 9:24-27) → Fullness of time (Gal 4:4) → The age to come (Rev 21-22).
