# Theology Proper

The doctrine of God — His existence, attributes, and works. Scripture does not argue for God's existence; it assumes it (Gen 1:1). The biblical God reveals Himself through His names, His acts in history, and supremely through His Son (Heb 1:1-3).

## Divine Attributes

### Incommunicable (unique to God)
- **Aseity**: God is self-existent — [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 3#v14|Exod 3:14]] ("I AM WHO I AM")
- **Sovereignty**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 103#v19|Ps 103:19]] — "His sovereignty rules over all"
- **Immutability**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Malachi/Malachi 3#v6|Mal 3:6]] — "I, the LORD, do not change"
- **Eternality**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 90#v2|Ps 90:2]] — "From everlasting to everlasting, You are God"

### Communicable (shared with creatures in limited measure)
- **Holiness**: [[concepts/hebrew/H6944 - qodesh|קֹדֶשׁ]] / [[concepts/greek/G40 - hagios|ἅγιος]] — Isa 6:3; Rev 4:8
- **Love**: [[concepts/hebrew/H157 - ahav|אָהַב]] / [[concepts/greek/G26 - agape|ἀγάπη]] — 1 John 4:8
- **Righteousness**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 89#v14|Ps 89:14]] — "Righteousness and justice are the foundation of Your throne"
- **Faithfulness**: [[concepts/hebrew/H571 - emeth|אֱמֶת]] / [[concepts/greek/G225 - aletheia|ἀλήθεια]] — Lam 3:23

## The Trinity

The one God exists eternally as three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. The OT lays groundwork (the plural of [[concepts/hebrew/H430 - Elohim]], the Angel of YHWH, the Spirit of God), while the NT makes it explicit:
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Matthew/Matthew 28#v19|Matt 28:19]] — The trinitarian baptismal formula
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/2 Corinthians/2 Corinthians 13#v14|2 Cor 13:14]] — The trinitarian benediction
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ephesians/Ephesians 1#v3|Eph 1:3-14]] — Father elects, Son redeems, Spirit seals

## Canonical Arc

God reveals Himself progressively: by name ([[concepts/themes/Divine Names]]), through creation ([[concepts/themes/Creation]]), in covenant ([[concepts/themes/Covenant]]), through the prophets, and finally in His Son (Heb 1:1-2). The fullness of God's self-revelation is Jesus Christ — "He who has seen Me has seen the Father" ([[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 14#v9|John 14:9]]).
