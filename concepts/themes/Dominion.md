# Dominion

The divine mandate for humanity to rule over creation as God's vicegerents — image-bearers exercising delegated authority. This mandate, given in Genesis 1:28, is lost through sin, fulfilled in Christ, and will be restored in the new creation.

## The Creation Mandate

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v28|Gen 1:28]] — "Be fruitful and multiply, and fill the [[concepts/hebrew/H776 - erets|earth]], and subdue it; and rule over… every living thing"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 2#v15|Gen 2:15]] — "The LORD God took the man and put him into the garden to cultivate it and keep it"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 8#v6|Ps 8:6-8]] — "You make him to rule over the works of Your hands"

## Dominion Lost

The Fall disrupts the human dominion mandate:
- The ground is cursed (Gen 3:17-18)
- Animals become hostile (Gen 9:2)
- Human rulers become tyrants (Gen 10-11)
- Creation groans (Rom 8:19-22)

## Dominion Restored in Christ

[[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 2#v6|Heb 2:6-9]] quotes Psalm 8 and applies it to Jesus: "We do not yet see all things subjected to him. But we do see… Jesus." What Adam lost, the last Adam (1 Cor 15:45) restores. Christ is the true human, exercising perfect dominion.

## Eschatological Fulfillment

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Daniel/Daniel 7#v14|Dan 7:14]] — The Son of Man receives everlasting dominion
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 5#v10|Rev 5:10]] — "They will reign upon the earth"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 22#v5|Rev 22:5]] — "They will reign forever and ever"

## Canonical Arc

Mandate given (Gen 1:28) → Dominion lost (Gen 3) → Kings as partial stewards (1 Sam-2 Kgs) → The Son of Man receives dominion (Dan 7) → Christ exercises perfect dominion (Heb 2) → Saints reign with Christ (Rev 22:5).
