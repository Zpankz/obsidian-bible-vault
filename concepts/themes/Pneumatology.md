# Pneumatology

The doctrine of the Holy Spirit — the third person of the Trinity, who creates, empowers, indwells, and transforms. The Spirit's work spans the entire canon: hovering over creation (Gen 1:2), empowering the prophets, anointing the Messiah, and indwelling the church.

## The Spirit in the OT

- **Creation**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v2|Gen 1:2]] — "The Spirit of God was hovering over the surface of the waters"
- **Empowerment**: Judg 6:34 — "The Spirit of the LORD clothed Gideon"
- **Prophetic inspiration**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 2#v2|Ezek 2:2]] — "The Spirit entered me"
- **Promise of outpouring**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Joel/Joel 2#v28|Joel 2:28]] — "I will pour out My Spirit on all flesh"
- **New covenant**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 36#v26|Ezek 36:26-27]] — "I will put My Spirit within you"

## The Spirit in the NT

### Pentecost
[[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 2#v1|Acts 2:1-4]] fulfills Joel 2:28 — the Spirit is poured out on all flesh, not just prophets, priests, and kings. This democratizes the Spirit's presence.

### The Paraclete
Jesus promises "another [[concepts/greek/G3875 - parakletos|Paraclete]]" (John 14:16) — the Spirit continues Christ's ministry on earth:
- Teaching and reminding (John 14:26)
- Testifying about Christ (John 15:26)
- Convicting the world (John 16:8-11)
- Guiding into all truth (John 16:13)

### Life in the Spirit
- **Indwelling**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 8#v9|Rom 8:9]] — "If the Spirit of God dwells in you"
- **Fruit**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Galatians/Galatians 5#v22|Gal 5:22-23]] — Love, joy, peace, patience...
- **Gifts**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/1 Corinthians/1 Corinthians 12#v4|1 Cor 12:4-11]] — Varieties of gifts, one Spirit
- **Sealing**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ephesians/Ephesians 1#v13|Eph 1:13-14]] — The Spirit as down payment of inheritance

## Lexical Spine

[[concepts/hebrew/H7307 - ruach|רוּחַ]] (*ruach*) → [[concepts/greek/G4151 - pneuma|πνεῦμα]] (*pneuma*): both mean spirit/wind/breath. The physical metaphor persists — the Spirit blows where He wills (John 3:8), breathes life into the church (Acts 2:2), and cannot be controlled or contained.
