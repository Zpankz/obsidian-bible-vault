# New Creation

God's ultimate purpose is not merely the rescue of souls from a doomed world but the renewal of all things. The new creation is the restoration and transformation of the original creation, purged of sin and death, perfected in glory.

## OT Hope

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 65#v17|Isa 65:17]] — "I create new heavens and a new earth"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 11#v6|Isa 11:6-9]] — The peaceable kingdom: wolf with lamb, child with serpent
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 47#v1|Ezek 47:1-12]] — The river from the temple bringing life to the Dead Sea

## Christ as the Beginning of the New Creation

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/2 Corinthians/2 Corinthians 5#v17|2 Cor 5:17]] — "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creature; the old things passed away"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Colossians/Colossians 1#v18|Col 1:18]] — Christ the "firstborn from the dead" = the [[concepts/greek/G746 - arche|beginning]] of new creation
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 8#v29|Rom 8:29]] — Believers conformed to the image of the firstborn

## Creation Groaning and Hoping

[[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 8#v19|Rom 8:19-23]] — Creation itself "waits eagerly" for the revealing of the sons of God. The redemption of believers includes the liberation of the whole created order from its "slavery to corruption."

## The Vision of Revelation

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 21#v1|Rev 21:1]] — "I saw a new heaven and a new earth"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 21#v3|Rev 21:3]] — "The tabernacle of God is among men"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 21#v4|Rev 21:4]] — No more death, mourning, crying, or pain
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 21#v5|Rev 21:5]] — "Behold, I am making all things new"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 22#v2|Rev 22:2]] — The tree of life reappears, healing the nations

## Canonical Arc

Creation (Gen 1-2) → Fall and curse (Gen 3) → Covenant restoration promises (the Prophets) → Christ inaugurates new creation (2 Cor 5:17) → Spirit as firstfruits (Rom 8:23) → All things made new (Rev 21:5). The end surpasses the beginning: not a garden but a city; not two people but a multitude from every nation.
