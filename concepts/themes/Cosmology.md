# Cosmology

The biblical understanding of the structure, origin, and purpose of the universe. Biblical cosmology is not a scientific treatise but a theological statement: the cosmos exists because God willed it, is sustained by His word, and moves toward His purposes.

## The Structure of Genesis 1

The six days of creation form a literary framework of forming and filling:

| Day (Forming) | Day (Filling) | Domain |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Day 1: Light / darkness | Day 4: Sun, moon, stars | Luminaries |
| Day 2: Sky / waters | Day 5: Birds, fish | Inhabitants of sky and sea |
| Day 3: Land / vegetation | Day 6: Animals, humanity | Land dwellers |

Day 7: Sabbath — God rests, not from exhaustion but in satisfaction; the cosmos is a temple and Sabbath is its consecration.

## The Three-Tiered Cosmos

The biblical authors describe the world as experienced:
- [[concepts/hebrew/H8064 - shamayim|שָׁמַיִם]] (*shamayim*) — Heavens (sky, firmament, God's dwelling)
- [[concepts/hebrew/H776 - erets|אֶרֶץ]] (*erets*) — Earth (dry land, the habitable world)
- תְּהוֹם (*tehom*) — The deep (waters below, the abyss)

## Creation by Word

[[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v3|Gen 1:3]] — "God said… and it was." Creation happens through divine speech. The NT identifies this creative Word as the [[concepts/greek/G3056 - logos|Logos]]: "All things came into being through Him" ([[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 1#v3|John 1:3]]).

## Creation by Separation

The verb [[concepts/hebrew/H914 - badal|בָּדַל]] (*badal*, "to separate") structures Genesis 1:
- Light from darkness (1:4)
- Waters above from waters below (1:6-7)
- Land from sea (1:9-10)

God creates by ordering, distinguishing, and naming — the opposite of chaos.

## The Cosmos as Temple

The parallels between Genesis 1 and tabernacle/temple construction suggest the cosmos itself is God's temple:
- Seven days of creation ↔ Seven speeches for tabernacle instructions (Exod 25-31)
- God "rests" ↔ God's presence "rests" in the temple
- Garden as the Holy of Holies ↔ Eden as sacred space

## Canonical Arc

Cosmos created (Gen 1) → Cosmos cursed (Gen 3:17) → Cosmos sustained (Col 1:17) → Cosmos groaning (Rom 8:22) → Cosmos renewed (Rev 21:1). The destiny of the physical creation is not destruction but transformation.
