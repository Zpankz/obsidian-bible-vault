# Covenant

The organizing principle of biblical theology — God relates to His creation through binding, oath-sealed commitments. The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H1285 - berith|בְּרִית]] (*berith*) and Greek [[concepts/greek/G1242 - diatheke|διαθήκη]] (*diatheke*) describe God's sovereign disposition of grace toward His people.

## The Major Biblical Covenants

| Covenant | Recipient | Sign | Key Text |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Noahic | All creation | Rainbow | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 9#v9\|Gen 9:9-17]] |
| Abrahamic | Abraham and seed | Circumcision | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 15#v18\|Gen 15:18]]; Gen 17:1-14 |
| Mosaic/Sinai | Israel | Sabbath | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 19#v5\|Exod 19:5-6]]; Exod 24:3-8 |
| Davidic | David and dynasty | Throne | 2 Sam 7:12-16 |
| New | All who believe | The Spirit | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 31#v31\|Jer 31:31-34]]; Luke 22:20 |

## Covenant Structure

Biblical covenants follow ancient Near Eastern treaty patterns:
1. **Preamble** — Identification of the suzerain (Exod 20:2a)
2. **Historical prologue** — What the suzerain has done (Exod 20:2b)
3. **Stipulations** — Obligations (Exod 20:3-17)
4. **Sanctions** — Blessings and curses (Deut 28)
5. **Witnesses** — Heaven and earth (Deut 30:19)

## The New Covenant

[[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 31#v31|Jer 31:31-34]] promises a covenant that writes the law on hearts, not tablets. [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 8#v8|Heb 8:8-12]] declares this fulfilled in Christ, whose [[concepts/greek/G129 - haima|blood]] ratifies the new covenant (Luke 22:20).

## Canonical Arc

The covenant thread runs from Noah (preservation) → Abraham (promise) → Sinai (law) → David (kingdom) → New Covenant (fulfillment in Christ). Each covenant builds on the previous without abolishing it.
