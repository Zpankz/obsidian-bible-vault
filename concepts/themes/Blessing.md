# Blessing

God's gracious bestowal of good upon His creation. The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H1288 - barak|בָּרַךְ]] (*barak*) appears first in Genesis 1:22 — God blesses the sea creatures. Blessing is a creation-word before it is a redemption-word.

## The Abrahamic Blessing

[[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 12#v1|Gen 12:1-3]] is the axis of the OT:
1. "I will make you a great nation"
2. "I will bless you"
3. "I will make your name great"
4. "You shall be a blessing"
5. "In you all the families of the earth will be blessed"

The final clause makes Abraham's blessing missional — it is not for hoarding but for distribution to all nations.

## Blessing and Curse

| Blessing | Curse | Text |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Fertile land | Thorns and thistles | Gen 3:17-18 |
| Covenant prosperity | Covenant exile | Deut 28 |
| Priestly benediction | Removal of God's face | Num 6:24-26 |
| Life | Death | Deut 30:19 |

## NT Fulfillment

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Galatians/Galatians 3#v14|Gal 3:14]] — "The blessing of Abraham might come to the Gentiles in Christ Jesus"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Ephesians/Ephesians 1#v3|Eph 1:3]] — "Blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places in Christ"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Matthew/Matthew 5#v3|Matt 5:3-12]] — The Beatitudes redefine blessing: the poor, the mourning, the meek are blessed

## The Beatitudes as Counter-Blessings

Jesus inverts the expected categories: blessing belongs not to the powerful but to the poor in spirit, the persecuted, the peacemakers. This is the kingdom's logic — the first shall be last.

## Canonical Arc

Creation blessing (Gen 1:22, 28) → Abrahamic blessing (Gen 12:1-3) → Priestly blessing (Num 6:24-26) → Covenant blessings and curses (Deut 28) → Christ becomes a curse to bring blessing (Gal 3:13-14) → Every spiritual blessing in Christ (Eph 1:3).
