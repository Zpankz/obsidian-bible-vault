# Eschatology

The doctrine of last things — the consummation of God's purposes in history. Biblical eschatology is not merely about the end of the world but about the breaking-in of God's future into the present. The kingdom is "already" inaugurated and "not yet" consummated.

## The Already / Not Yet

| Already (inaugurated) | Not Yet (consummated) |
|:---|:---|
| Christ has risen (1 Cor 15:20) | The general resurrection awaits (1 Cor 15:23) |
| The kingdom has come (Luke 17:21) | The kingdom will be fully revealed (Matt 25:34) |
| Eternal life is a present possession (John 5:24) | The fullness of life awaits (Rev 21:4) |
| Satan is defeated (Col 2:15) | Satan will be destroyed (Rev 20:10) |

## Key Eschatological Events

- **Christ's return**: [[bibles/NASB-IL/Acts/Acts 1#v11|Acts 1:11]]; 1 Thess 4:16-17
- **Resurrection of the dead**: [[bibles/NASB-IL/1 Corinthians/1 Corinthians 15#v42|1 Cor 15:42-44]]; Dan 12:2
- **Final judgment**: [[bibles/NASB-IL/Revelation/Revelation 20#v11|Rev 20:11-15]]; Matt 25:31-46
- **New heavens and new earth**: [[bibles/NASB-IL/Revelation/Revelation 21#v1|Rev 21:1-5]]; Isa 65:17

## OT Eschatological Hope

- The Day of the LORD: [[bibles/NASB-IL/Joel/Joel 2#v31|Joel 2:31]]; Amos 5:18-20
- The Son of Man: Dan 7:13-14 → [[bibles/NASB-IL/Mark/Mark 14#v62|Mark 14:62]]
- The age to come ([[concepts/hebrew/H5769 - olam|עוֹלָם]]): Isa 65:17-25

## Canonical Arc

Creation (Gen 1-2) → Fall (Gen 3) → Redemptive history → Cross and Resurrection → Church age → Return of Christ → Final judgment → [[concepts/themes/New Creation]] (Rev 21-22). The end recapitulates the beginning: the tree of life reappears (Rev 22:2), death is abolished (Rev 21:4), and God dwells with His people (Rev 21:3).
