# Hamartiology

The doctrine of sin — its origin, nature, effects, and remedy. Sin in Scripture is not merely moral failure but rebellion against God, a cosmic power that enslaves, and a condition from which only divine grace can deliver.

## The Origin of Sin

- **The Fall**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 3#v1|Gen 3:1-7]] — Temptation, disobedience, and consequences
- **Universal spread**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 5#v12|Rom 5:12]] — "Through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin"
- **The heart's condition**: Gen 6:5; Jer 17:9 — "The heart is more deceitful than all else"

## Sin as Power

Paul's distinctive contribution is the personification of sin in Romans 5-8:
- Sin "entered the world" ([[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 5#v12|Rom 5:12]])
- Sin "reigned in death" (Rom 5:21)
- Sin enslaves (Rom 6:6, 17)
- Sin pays wages — [[concepts/greek/G2288 - thanatos|death]] ([[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 6#v23|Rom 6:23]])
- Sin dwells within (Rom 7:17, 20)

## The Remedy

Sin's remedy is not moral improvement but divine intervention:
- **Atonement**: [[concepts/hebrew/H3722 - kaphar|כָּפַר]] — covering/propitiation through sacrifice
- **Repentance**: [[concepts/greek/G3340 - metanoeo|μετανοέω]] — a complete turning
- **Justification**: [[concepts/greek/G1343 - dikaiosyne|δικαιοσύνη]] — declared righteous by faith
- **New creation**: [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/2 Corinthians/2 Corinthians 5#v17|2 Cor 5:17]] — "The old things passed away"

## Canonical Arc

Prohibition (Gen 2:17) → Disobedience (Gen 3) → Spread (Gen 4-11) → Sacrificial system (Leviticus) → Prophetic diagnosis (Isaiah, Jeremiah) → The Lamb of God (John 1:29) → Sin defeated (Rom 6-8) → Sin abolished (Rev 21:27).
