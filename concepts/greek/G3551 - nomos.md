# νόμος — Nomos

Law — the LXX translation of [[concepts/hebrew/H8451 - torah|Torah]]. In Paul's letters, *nomos* is used with shifting referents: the Mosaic law, the Pentateuch, a general principle, or the "law" of sin and death. Paul's relationship with *nomos* is complex: the law is holy (Rom 7:12) yet cannot save (Gal 2:16), reveals sin (Rom 3:20) yet is fulfilled in love (Rom 13:10).

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Matthew/Matthew 5#v17|Matt 5:17]] — "I did not come to abolish the Law (*nomos*) but to fulfill"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 3#v20|Rom 3:20]] — "By the works of the Law (*nomos*) no flesh will be justified"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 7#v12|Rom 7:12]] — "The Law (*nomos*) is holy, and the commandment is holy and righteous and good"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Galatians/Galatians 3#v24|Gal 3:24]] — "The Law (*nomos*) has become our tutor to lead us to Christ"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 10#v4|Rom 10:4]] — "Christ is the end (*telos*) of the Law (*nomos*)"

## Theological Significance

Paul's theology of *nomos* is often misread as anti-law. In fact, Paul affirms the law's divine origin and holiness but insists it was never intended to be the means of justification. The law diagnoses sin (Rom 3:20), restrains evil (1 Tim 1:9), and tutors Israel toward Christ (Gal 3:24), but it cannot transform the human heart.

Romans 10:4 — "Christ is the *telos* of the law" — is the key. *Telos* means both "end" and "goal": Christ is both the termination of law-keeping as a path to righteousness and the fulfillment of everything the law aimed at. The new covenant writes Torah on the heart (Jer 31:33), so that the Spirit accomplishes what the law commanded but could not empower (Rom 8:3-4).
