# εἰρήνη — Eirene

Peace — not merely the absence of conflict but the comprehensive well-being inherited from the Hebrew *shalom*. In the NT, peace is first vertical (reconciliation with God through Christ) and then horizontal (reconciliation among human beings).

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Peace with God | Reconciliation through Christ | [[bibles/NASB-IL/Romans/Romans 5#v1\|Rom 5:1]] |
| Inner peace | Tranquility of soul | [[bibles/NASB-IL/Philippians/Philippians 4#v7\|Phil 4:7]] |
| Interpersonal peace | Reconciliation among people | [[bibles/NASB-IL/Ephesians/Ephesians 2#v14\|Eph 2:14]] |
| Messianic peace | Eschatological wholeness | Luke 2:14; John 14:27 |

## Theological Significance

Paul declares "we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ" (Rom 5:1) — the objective result of justification. This is not a subjective feeling but a changed status: the enmity between God and humanity has been ended at the cross.

Ephesians 2:14 names Christ Himself as "our peace" — He is not merely the peacemaker but the peace itself, breaking down the dividing wall between Jew and Gentile and creating "one new man."

## OT Background

The Hebrew שָׁלוֹם (*shalom*) means wholeness, completeness, welfare — far richer than the Greek or English "peace." It includes material prosperity, health, right relationships, and covenant blessing (Num 6:26). The LXX renders *shalom* with εἰρήνη, importing the full semantic range into the NT.
