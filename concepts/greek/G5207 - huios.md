# υἱός — Huios

Son — the supreme christological title in the NT, expressing Jesus' unique relationship to the Father. "Son of God" (*huios tou theou*) denotes divine origin and nature; "Son of Man" (*huios tou anthropou*) invokes Daniel 7:13's heavenly figure who receives universal dominion. Both titles converge in Jesus.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 3#v16|John 3:16]] — "He gave His only begotten Son (*huios*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Matthew/Matthew 16#v16|Matt 16:16]] — "You are the Christ, the Son (*huios*) of the living God"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 1#v2|Heb 1:2]] — "In these last days has spoken to us in His Son (*huios*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 8#v14|Rom 8:14]] — "All who are being led by the Spirit… are sons (*huioi*) of God"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Mark/Mark 14#v62|Mark 14:62]] — "You shall see the Son (*huios*) of Man sitting at the right hand of Power"

## Theological Significance

"Son of God" in the NT is not merely a messianic title (though it includes that) but an ontological claim: the Son shares the Father's nature (John 5:18; 10:30). Hebrews 1:3 says the Son is "the radiance of His glory and the exact representation of His nature." Sonship here means eternal equality, not inferiority.

Through Christ, believers receive the "adoption as sons" (*huiothesia*, Gal 4:5) — not equality with the eternal Son but participation in His relationship with the Father. The distinction matters: Christ is Son by nature; believers are sons by grace. Yet the reality is staggering: "you are all sons of God through faith in Christ Jesus" (Gal 3:26).
