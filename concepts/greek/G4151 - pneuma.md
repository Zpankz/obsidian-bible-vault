# πνεῦμα — Pneuma

Spirit, wind, breath — the direct Greek equivalent of Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H7307 - ruach|ruach]]. In the NT, *pneuma* refers primarily to the Holy Spirit (*to pneuma to hagion*), but also to the human spirit, angelic/demonic spirits, and wind. The Spirit is the third person of the Trinity, the agent of regeneration, sanctification, and empowerment.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 3#v8|John 3:8]] — "The wind (*pneuma*) blows where it wishes… so is everyone born of the Spirit (*pneuma*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 2#v4|Acts 2:4]] — "They were all filled with the Holy Spirit (*pneuma*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 8#v11|Rom 8:11]] — "The Spirit (*pneuma*) of Him who raised Jesus from the dead"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Galatians/Galatians 5#v22|Gal 5:22]] — "The fruit of the Spirit (*pneuma*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ephesians/Ephesians 1#v13|Eph 1:13]] — "Sealed with the Holy Spirit (*pneuma*) of promise"

## Theological Significance

The NT *pneuma* continues the OT *ruach*: the Spirit who hovered over creation (Gen 1:2) now indwells believers (1 Cor 6:19). Pentecost (Acts 2) fulfills Joel 2:28 — the Spirit poured out on all flesh, not just select leaders. This democratization of the Spirit transforms the people of God from a national entity into a Spirit-indwelt community.

Paul's pneumatology in Romans 8 is the theological high point: the Spirit gives life (8:11), leads (8:14), testifies to adoption (8:16), intercedes (8:26), and guarantees future glory (8:23). The Christian life from beginning to end is Spirit-animated — the *pneuma* is not an add-on to salvation but its very power and presence.
