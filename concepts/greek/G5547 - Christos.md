# Χριστός — Christos

Christ, the Anointed One — the Greek translation of Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H4899 - mashiach|Mashiach]] (Messiah). Initially a title ("Jesus the Christ"), it quickly became a virtual second name ("Jesus Christ" or "Christ Jesus"). The title claims that Jesus is the long-awaited anointed king, priest, and prophet of Israel, the fulfillment of OT promise.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Matthew/Matthew 16#v16|Matt 16:16]] — "You are the Christ (*Christos*), the Son of the living God"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 2#v36|Acts 2:36]] — "God has made Him both Lord and Christ (*Christos*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 5#v8|Rom 5:8]] — "While we were yet sinners, Christ (*Christos*) died for us"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/1 Corinthians/1 Corinthians 15#v3|1 Cor 15:3]] — "Christ (*Christos*) died for our sins according to the Scriptures"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Philippians/Philippians 2#v11|Phil 2:11]] — "Jesus Christ (*Christos*) is Lord"

## Theological Significance

The early Christian confession "Jesus is the Christ" (Acts 2:36; 18:5) was the claim that Jesus of Nazareth fulfilled Israel's messianic hope. But Jesus redefined the concept: the Christ was not a military conqueror but a suffering servant who would die for sins and rise again (1 Cor 15:3-4). The cross was not the failure of messiahship but its deepest expression.

Paul's formula "in Christ" (*en Christo*) appears over 80 times and denotes the believer's union with the Anointed One. To be "in Christ" is to participate in His death and resurrection, to be incorporated into His body (the church), and to share His inheritance. *Christos* is thus not only a title for Jesus but a sphere of existence for believers — the new humanity is defined by its location "in Christ."
