# μαθητής — Mathetes

A learner, pupil, or follower — one who attaches to a teacher and is shaped by the teacher's life and instruction. In the Gospels, it refers to the followers of Jesus, both the Twelve and the wider circle. In Acts, it becomes the primary term for Christians before the name "Christian" (Acts 11:26).

## Theological Significance

Jesus redefines discipleship beyond the rabbinic model. A rabbi's student aimed to become a rabbi himself; Jesus' disciples are called to permanent dependence: "A disciple is not above his teacher" (Matt 10:24). The cost is absolute — "If anyone comes to Me, and does not hate his own father and mother… he cannot be My disciple" (Luke 14:26).

The Great Commission (Matt 28:19) makes disciple-making the mission of the church: "Go… make disciples of all the nations."

## OT Background

The teacher-student relationship appears in the OT prophetic schools (1 Sam 19:20; 2 Kgs 2:3) and in wisdom literature's master-apprentice pattern (Prov 1:8). Isaiah 8:16 speaks of "binding up the testimony" among "my disciples" (לִמֻּדָי, *limmudai*). Jesus inhabits this prophetic-wisdom tradition but radicalizes it: discipleship is not a phase but a lifelong calling.
