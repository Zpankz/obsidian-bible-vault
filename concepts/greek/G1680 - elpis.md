# ἐλπίς — Elpis

Hope in the NT is not wishful thinking but confident expectation grounded in God's past acts — especially the resurrection of Christ. Paul places hope in the triad with faith and love (1 Cor 13:13) as one of the three enduring theological virtues.

## Theological Significance

Romans 5:5 — "hope does not disappoint, because the love of God has been poured out within our hearts through the Holy Spirit." Christian hope is not uncertain aspiration but Spirit-grounded confidence in the God who has already acted decisively in Christ's resurrection.

The eschatological dimension is central: we hope for what we do not yet see (Rom 8:24-25). But this hope is "an anchor of the soul, both sure and steadfast" (Heb 6:19) precisely because it rests on God's faithfulness, not human optimism.

## OT Background

The Hebrew תִּקְוָה (*tiqvah*, "hope") and related terms express the posture of waiting on YHWH (Ps 33:20; 130:5). Israel's hope was anchored in God's covenant promises — land, descendants, blessing, and ultimately the messianic age. The NT declares these hopes fulfilled and surpassed in Christ.
