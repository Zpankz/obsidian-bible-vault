# παράκλητος — Parakletos

One called alongside to help — a legal advocate, intercessor, or counselor. Used exclusively by John in the NT (John 14-16; 1 John 2:1), it designates both the Holy Spirit (the "another Paraclete" of John 14:16) and Christ Himself (the advocate with the Father, 1 John 2:1).

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| The Holy Spirit | The Helper sent by the Father | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 14#v16\|John 14:16]] |
| Spirit of truth | The one who guides into truth | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 15#v26\|John 15:26]] |
| Jesus as advocate | Intercessor with the Father | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/1 John/1 John 2#v1\|1 John 2:1]] |

## Theological Significance

Jesus' promise of "another Paraclete" (ἄλλον παράκλητον, John 14:16) implies He Himself was the first Paraclete. The Spirit continues Christ's ministry on earth while Christ advocates for believers before the Father in heaven. This creates a dual intercession: Christ at the Father's right hand (Rom 8:34; 1 John 2:1) and the Spirit within believers (Rom 8:26-27).

The Paraclete's ministry includes: teaching and reminding (John 14:26), testifying about Christ (John 15:26), convicting the world (John 16:8-11), and guiding into all truth (John 16:13).

## OT Background

The concept draws on the OT advocate/intercessor figure — Moses interceding for Israel (Exod 32:11-14), the prophets pleading God's case to the people. The Hebrew נָחַם (*nacham*, "to comfort/console") provides background for the Paraclete as "Comforter," linking to Isaiah's "Comfort, O comfort My people" (Isa 40:1).
