# δύναμις — Dynamis

Power in action — the ability to accomplish what is beyond human capacity. The English words "dynamic" and "dynamite" derive from this root, though neither captures the theological depth. In the NT, *dynamis* is the power of God operative in creation, salvation, resurrection, and the Spirit's work.

## Theological Significance

Paul's supreme statement is Romans 1:16 — "the gospel is the *dynamis* of God for salvation." The gospel is not merely *about* power; it *is* power. Similarly, "the word of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the *dynamis* of God" (1 Cor 1:18).

The paradox of divine power is stated in 2 Corinthians 12:9 — "My power is perfected in weakness." God's *dynamis* works through human insufficiency, not despite it.

## OT Background

God's power is displayed in the Exodus (Deut 4:37), creation (Jer 10:12), and the sustaining of history (Ps 147:5). The Hebrew גְּבוּרָה (*gevurah*, "might") is one of the divine attributes celebrated in the Psalms (Ps 21:13; 54:1). The LXX renders God's mighty acts as δυνάμεις.
