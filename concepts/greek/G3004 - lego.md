# λέγω — Lego

To say, speak, tell — the root verb behind [[concepts/greek/G3056 - logos|logos]] ("word"). The most frequent speech verb in the NT, used to introduce divine speech ("God said"), prophetic oracles, and Jesus' authoritative pronouncements ("Truly I say to you"). The act of speaking is the primary mode of divine revelation.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Matthew/Matthew 5#v22|Matt 5:22]] — "But I say (*lego*) to you" — Jesus' authoritative formula
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 1#v29|John 1:29]] — "He saw Jesus… and said (*lego*), 'Behold, the Lamb of God'"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 1#v5|Heb 1:5]] — "For to which of the angels did He ever say (*lego*)?"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Revelation/Revelation 21#v5|Rev 21:5]] — "He who sits on the throne said (*lego*), 'Behold, I am making all things new'"

## Theological Significance

Jesus' Sermon on the Mount formula — "You have heard that it was said… but I *lego* to you" (Matt 5:21-22) — places His speech on the same authority level as Torah. He does not cite authorities; He *is* the authority. This "I say to you" pattern reveals a speaker who claims to be the definitive voice of God.

The verb connects creation ("God said," Gen 1) with new creation ("He said, 'Behold, I am making all things new,'" Rev 21:5). Divine speech opens and closes the biblical narrative. The *logos* (noun) proceeds from the act of *lego* (verb) — what God says becomes reality, and the Word-made-flesh is the ultimate divine utterance.
