# σῴζω — Sozo

To save, deliver, heal, make whole — a comprehensive verb covering physical healing, spiritual rescue, and eschatological deliverance. In the Gospels, *sozo* is used for both physical healings ("your faith has saved/healed you," Mark 5:34) and eternal salvation ("He will save His people from their sins," Matt 1:21). The overlap is theologically intentional.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Matthew/Matthew 1#v21|Matt 1:21]] — "He will save (*sozo*) His people from their sins"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Mark/Mark 5#v34|Mark 5:34]] — "Your faith has made you well (*sozo*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 2#v21|Acts 2:21]] — "Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved (*sozo*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 10#v9|Rom 10:9]] — "You will be saved (*sozo*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ephesians/Ephesians 2#v8|Eph 2:8]] — "By grace you have been saved (*sozo*)"

## Theological Significance

The breadth of *sozo* reveals that biblical salvation is holistic — not just the soul's escape from hell but the rescue of the whole person from sin, death, and every form of brokenness. Jesus' healings are not interruptions to His "real" mission of spiritual salvation; they are signs of the comprehensive restoration He brings. To be *saved* is to be made wholly sound.

Paul uses *sozo* in past, present, and future tenses: "you have been saved" (Eph 2:8), "we are being saved" (1 Cor 1:18), and "we will be saved" (Rom 5:9-10). Salvation is a completed act, an ongoing process, and a future hope — justification, sanctification, and glorification are all contained in this single verb.
