# προφήτης — Prophetes

Prophet, one who speaks forth — not primarily a predictor of the future but a spokesman for God, one who speaks God's word into the present situation. The NT recognizes both OT prophets (Matt 5:12) and NT prophets as Spirit-gifted members of the church (1 Cor 12:28; Eph 4:11).

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Luke/Luke 24#v27|Luke 24:27]] — "Beginning with Moses and all the prophets (*prophetai*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 3#v22|Acts 3:22]] — "Moses said, 'The Lord God will raise up for you a prophet (*prophetes*)'"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ephesians/Ephesians 2#v20|Eph 2:20]] — "Built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets (*prophetai*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 1#v1|Heb 1:1]] — "God… spoke long ago to the fathers in the prophets (*prophetai*)"

## Theological Significance

Hebrews 1:1-2 sets up the decisive contrast: God spoke "in the prophets" partially and progressively, but "in these last days has spoken to us in His Son." Jesus is the prophet to end all prophets — not merely a bearer of God's word but the Word Himself. The prophetic office reaches its *telos* in the incarnation.

The NT church also has *prophetai* (1 Cor 12:28; Eph 4:11), but their role is subordinate to apostolic teaching and must be tested (1 Thess 5:20-21; 1 Cor 14:29). Prophecy in the church is the Spirit's ongoing application of the apostolic gospel, not new revelation on par with Scripture. The prophetic impulse — speaking God's word into the present — continues, but its foundation has been laid once for all.
