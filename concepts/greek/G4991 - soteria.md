# σωτηρία — Soteria

Salvation, deliverance — the noun form denoting the comprehensive rescue God accomplishes through Christ. Rooted in the OT [[concepts/hebrew/H3444 - yeshuah|yeshuah]], *soteria* encompasses rescue from sin's guilt and power, reconciliation with God, restoration of the divine image, and ultimate glorification. It is both a present possession and a future hope.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Luke/Luke 1#v77|Luke 1:77]] — "To give to His people the knowledge of salvation (*soteria*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 4#v12|Acts 4:12]] — "There is salvation (*soteria*) in no one else"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 1#v16|Rom 1:16]] — "The gospel… is the power of God for salvation (*soteria*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Philippians/Philippians 2#v12|Phil 2:12]] — "Work out your salvation (*soteria*) with fear and trembling"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 2#v3|Heb 2:3]] — "How will we escape if we neglect so great a salvation (*soteria*)?"

## Theological Significance

*Soteria* in the NT is scandalously exclusive and breathtakingly inclusive simultaneously. It is exclusive: "there is salvation in no one else" (Acts 4:12). It is inclusive: offered to "everyone who believes" (Rom 1:16), irrespective of ethnicity, status, or merit. The gospel is the power of God *for soteria* — salvation is not merely a doctrine but a divine power at work.

Philippians 2:12-13 captures the paradox: "work out your *soteria* with fear and trembling, for it is God who is at work in you." Salvation is entirely God's gift *and* requires human response. The believer works *out* what God has worked *in*. This is not synergism but the outworking of grace — God's power producing human obedience.
