# διαθήκη — Diatheke

The LXX's rendering of the Hebrew *berith* ("covenant"), deliberately chosen over the Greek συνθήκη (*syntheke*, "mutual agreement") because God's covenant is not a negotiation between equals but a sovereign disposition — God sets the terms.

## Theological Significance

Hebrews 8-10 makes the new/old covenant contrast the backbone of its argument. The new covenant, prophesied in Jeremiah 31:31-34, is "better" (Heb 8:6) because it provides what the old could not: internal transformation ("I will put My laws into their minds"), universal knowledge of God, and decisive forgiveness ("I will remember their sins no more").

Jesus' words at the Last Supper — "This cup is the new covenant in My blood" (Luke 22:20) — declare that the new covenant is inaugurated through His sacrificial death.

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H1285 - berith|בְּרִית]] (*berith*) structures the entire OT narrative: Noahic (Gen 9), Abrahamic (Gen 15, 17), Mosaic/Sinai (Exod 19-24), Davidic (2 Sam 7), and New (Jer 31:31-34). Each covenant builds toward the "new covenant" inaugurated by Christ.
