# κόσμος — Kosmos

World, universe, created order — but also the human world system alienated from God. *Kosmos* carries a double valence in the NT: God loves the world (John 3:16) and yet "the whole world lies in the power of the evil one" (1 John 5:19). The world is both the object of God's love and the arena of opposition to God.

## Theological Significance

John's Gospel uses *kosmos* 78 times, with shifting connotations: the world as God's good creation (1:10), the world as the theater of salvation (3:16), and the world as a hostile system that rejects the light (1:10b; 15:18). These are not contradictions but perspectives — the same created order that God loves has become the domain of darkness.

The Christian stance toward the *kosmos* is dialectical: engagement without conformity. Believers are "in the world" but "not of the world" (John 17:11, 16). God's plan is not the destruction of the *kosmos* but its redemption and renewal — the *kosmos* that was made through the Logos will be reclaimed by Him.
