# ποιέω — Poieo

To do, make, produce — the general-purpose Greek verb for action and production, equivalent to Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H6213 - asah|asah]]. It covers both divine creation ("all things were made through Him," John 1:3) and human activity ("do the will of My Father," Matt 7:21). The English word "poetry" derives from this root — making as creative art.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/John/John 1#v3|John 1:3]] — "All things came into being (*ginomai*) through Him" (cf. *poieo* in Heb 1:2)
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Matthew/Matthew 7#v21|Matt 7:21]] — "He who does (*poieo*) the will of My Father"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Ephesians/Ephesians 2#v10|Eph 2:10]] — "We are His workmanship (*poiema*), created in Christ Jesus for good works"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Revelation/Revelation 21#v5|Rev 21:5]] — "Behold, I am making (*poieo*) all things new"

## Theological Significance

Ephesians 2:10 uses the noun form *poiema* ("workmanship," literally "poem"): believers are God's masterwork, His creative production. This stands in immediate contrast with 2:9 ("not of works") — we are not saved *by* works but *for* works. God's making (*poieo*) produces people who then do (*poieo*) good works, reflecting His creative character.

The bookend use of *poieo* — creation in John 1:3 and new creation in Revelation 21:5 — confirms that God's making is not finished. The same creative power that brought the cosmos into being is now "making all things new." Salvation is not repair but re-creation, and the verb is the same: God *makes*.
