# ἀνάστασις — Anastasis

Literally "a standing up" — the bodily resurrection of the dead. The NT treats Christ's resurrection not as a metaphor but as the foundational event of the new creation, the "firstfruits" (1 Cor 15:20) guaranteeing the future resurrection of all believers.

| Context | Meaning | Example |

## Theological Significance

Paul makes resurrection the non-negotiable center of Christian faith: "If Christ has not been raised, your faith is worthless" (1 Cor 15:17). The resurrection is not merely survival after death but bodily transformation — the perishable puts on the imperishable (1 Cor 15:53).

Christ's resurrection inaugurates the "new creation" (2 Cor 5:17). He is the "firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep" (1 Cor 15:20) — the first harvest guaranteeing the full harvest to come.

## OT Background

The OT concept of resurrection develops from hints (Ps 16:10; Isa 26:19) to explicit affirmation (Dan 12:2 — "Many of those who sleep in the dust of the ground will awake"). The NT sees Christ's resurrection as the fulfillment of these promises and the decisive defeat of death.
