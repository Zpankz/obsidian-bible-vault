# βασιλεία — Basileia

The dynamic reign of God — not primarily a territory but the active exercise of God's sovereign rule. "The kingdom of God/heaven" is the central theme of Jesus' preaching, announced as both present reality and future consummation.

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| God's active reign | The present breaking-in of God's rule | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Luke/Luke 17#v21\|Luke 17:21]] |

| Kingdom of heaven | Matthew's reverential synonym | Matt 4:17 |
| Earthly kingdoms | Human political domains | Matt 4:8; Rev 11:15 |

## Theological Significance

The "already/not yet" tension defines NT kingdom theology. The kingdom is *already* present in Jesus' ministry — "if I cast out demons by the Spirit of God, then the kingdom of God has come upon you" (Matt 12:28). But it is *not yet* consummated — "Your kingdom come" (Matt 6:10) remains a prayer. The parables of the kingdom (Matt 13) describe this tension: the mustard seed is growing, the wheat and tares coexist until harvest.

## OT Background

God's kingship is proclaimed throughout the Psalms (Ps 93:1; 97:1 — "YHWH reigns"). Daniel 2:44 and 7:14 envision an everlasting kingdom given to "one like a son of man" — the text Jesus draws upon when proclaiming the kingdom and identifying Himself as the Son of Man.
