# ἀλήθεια — Aletheia

Literally "un-hiddenness" — truth as that which is revealed, unconcealed. In the NT, truth is not abstract correspondence but personal reality embodied in Christ ("I am the truth," John 14:6) and actualized by the Spirit ("the Spirit of truth," John 16:13).

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Christ as truth | Personal embodiment of reality | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 14#v6\|John 14:6]] |
| The gospel | The truth of the message | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Galatians/Galatians 2#v5\|Gal 2:5]] |
| Moral integrity | Truthfulness, sincerity | Eph 4:25; 1 John 3:18 |
| Divine faithfulness | God's reliability | Rom 3:7; Rom 15:8 |

## Theological Significance

John's Gospel uses ἀλήθεια 25 times, making it a Johannine keyword. The decisive move is John 14:6 — truth is not a system but a person. To "know the truth" (John 8:32) is to know Christ; the truth that "sets free" is not philosophical enlightenment but personal encounter with the Son.

The "Spirit of truth" (John 14:17; 15:26; 16:13) continues Christ's revelatory work, guiding believers into "all the truth."

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H571 - emeth|אֱמֶת]] (*emeth*) means firmness, reliability, faithfulness — truth as something you can lean on. The LXX renders it with ἀλήθεια, fusing Greek "unconcealment" with Hebrew "trustworthiness." The NT inherits both dimensions: truth is what God reveals *and* what God keeps.
