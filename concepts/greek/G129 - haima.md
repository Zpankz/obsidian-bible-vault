# αἷμα — Haima

Blood in the NT carries immense sacrificial and covenantal weight, representing life poured out (Lev 17:11) and the basis of atonement. The "blood of Christ" becomes the central metaphor for His atoning death.

## Theological Significance

The NT theology of blood rests on the OT principle that "the life of the flesh is in the blood" (Lev 17:11). Christ's blood is not merely a metaphor for death but a sacrificial reality that accomplishes what the OT sacrificial system prefigured: actual cleansing from sin (Heb 9:22), covenant ratification (Luke 22:20), and purchased redemption (1 Pet 1:18-19).

Hebrews 9:11-14 explicitly contrasts the blood of goats and bulls with the blood of Christ, who offered Himself "through the eternal Spirit."

## OT Background

The sacrificial system of Leviticus presupposes that blood — representing life — can be given in substitution. The Passover blood (Exod 12:13), the Day of Atonement (Lev 16), and the covenant blood sprinkled by Moses (Exod 24:8) all prefigure the "blood of the new covenant" inaugurated by Christ.
