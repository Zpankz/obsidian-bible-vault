# Ἰησοῦς — Iesous

The Greek form of the Hebrew name יְהוֹשֻׁעַ (*Yehoshua* / Joshua), meaning "YHWH saves" or "YHWH is salvation." The angel explains the name's significance: "you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins" (Matt 1:21). The human name of the incarnate Son carries the divine program of salvation within it.

## Theological Significance

The name "Jesus" anchors Christology in history — this is a real person, born in Bethlehem, crucified under Pontius Pilate. But the name itself is theological: it embeds YHWH's saving action into a human identity. Philippians 2:9-11 declares that God "bestowed on Him the name which is above every name," and at this name "every knee will bow" — language drawn from Isaiah 45:23, where YHWH claims exclusive worship.

## OT Background

Joshua son of Nun (יְהוֹשֻׁעַ) led Israel into the Promised Land — a type of the greater Joshua/Jesus who leads God's people into the true rest (Heb 4:8-10). The name itself derives from [[concepts/hebrew/H3444 - yeshuah|יְשׁוּעָה]] (*yeshuah*, "salvation"), embedding the salvation theology of the OT into the personal name of the Messiah.
