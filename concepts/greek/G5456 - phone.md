# φωνή — Phone

Voice, sound, call — the medium of divine self-disclosure. God's *phone* is heard at Jesus' baptism ("This is My beloved Son," Matt 3:17), at the transfiguration (Matt 17:5), and as thunder from heaven (John 12:28). The voice conveys authority, identity, and command. Jesus describes Himself as the shepherd whose *phone* the sheep know and follow (John 10:27).

## Theological Significance

The divine *phone* at Jesus' baptism and transfiguration publicly identifies and commissions the Son. These moments recall the OT theophanies where God's voice shook Sinai (Exod 19:19) and called prophets by name (1 Sam 3:4). The same voice that spoke creation into being now declares Jesus' identity.

John's Gospel develops the shepherd metaphor: the sheep recognize the shepherd's *phone* and follow (John 10:4, 27). This is not merely auditory but relational — recognition through intimate familiarity. Jesus further claims that His voice will raise the dead (John 5:25, 28) — the same creative power that said "Let there be light" will call the dead from their graves.
