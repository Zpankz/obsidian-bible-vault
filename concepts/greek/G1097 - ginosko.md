# γινώσκω — Ginosko

To know by experience, perception, or relationship — not merely intellectual awareness but participatory knowledge. In Johannine theology, to "know" God is to have eternal life (John 17:3).

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Relational knowing | Intimate knowledge of God/Christ | [[bibles/NASB-IL/John/John 17#v3\|John 17:3]] |
| Perception | Coming to understand | [[bibles/NASB-IL/John/John 8#v32\|John 8:32]] |
| Recognition | Identifying someone/something | Matt 7:23; Luke 24:31 |
| Sexual knowledge | Euphemism for intercourse | Matt 1:25 (following LXX usage) |

## Theological Significance

John equates knowledge of God with eternal life itself: "This is eternal life, that they may know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent" (John 17:3). This is not gnosis in the later Gnostic sense — detached, elitist, speculative — but covenantal knowing rooted in the Hebrew *yada*: personal, relational, transformative.

Paul counters "knowledge puffs up, but love builds up" (1 Cor 8:1), warning against knowledge detached from ἀγάπη.

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H3045 - yada|יָדַע]] (*yada*) is fundamentally relational — God "knows" Israel (Amos 3:2), Adam "knew" Eve (Gen 4:1), Israel is called to "know" YHWH (Jer 31:34). The LXX renders *yada* with γινώσκω, preserving this relational depth against the Greek tendency toward abstract knowledge.
