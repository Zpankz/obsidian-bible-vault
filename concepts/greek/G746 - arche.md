# ἀρχή — Arche

A word carrying dual force: temporal origin ("beginning") and positional authority ("ruler/sovereignty"). In Christological usage, both senses converge — Christ is both the origin of creation and the supreme authority over it.

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Temporal origin | The beginning of time/creation | [[bibles/NASB-IL/John/John 1#v1\|John 1:1]] |
| Christ as origin | Source and firstborn of creation | [[bibles/NASB-IL/Colossians/Colossians 1#v18\|Col 1:18]] |
| Spiritual authorities | Rulers, principalities | Eph 3:10; Col 2:15 |
| First principles | Elementary teachings | Heb 5:12; 6:1 |

## Theological Significance

John 1:1 echoes Genesis 1:1 with "Ἐν ἀρχῇ" ("In the beginning"), deliberately linking the Logos to the moment of creation. But John pushes further — the Word was not created *at* the beginning; He already *was* (ἦν, imperfect tense) in the beginning.

Colossians 1:18 names Christ "the ἀρχή, the firstborn from the dead" — He is not only the beginning of creation but the beginning of the new creation through resurrection.

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H7225 - reshith|רֵאשִׁית]] (*reshith*, "beginning/firstfruits") opens Scripture in Genesis 1:1. The LXX renders it with ἀρχή. Proverbs 8:22 uses *reshith* for Wisdom's origin — a text early Christians read christologically.
