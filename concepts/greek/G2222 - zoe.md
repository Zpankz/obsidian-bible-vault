# ζωή — Zoe

Life in its fullest, most qualitative sense — not merely biological existence (βίος, *bios*) but the divine life that God is and gives. John uses ζωή 36 times, making it a signature Johannine term. "Eternal life" (ζωὴ αἰώνιος) is not primarily about duration but about quality — the life of the age to come, experienced now.

| Context | Meaning | Example |

## Theological Significance

John 1:4 — "In Him was life" — locates the origin of all life in the Logos. This is then specified christologically: "I am the way, the truth, and the life" (John 14:6). Eternal life is not an abstract reward but personal union with Christ — "He who has the Son has the life" (1 John 5:12).

The Johannine "already/not yet" of life parallels the kingdom: eternal life is a present possession (John 5:24 — "has passed out of death into life") with a future consummation (John 6:40 — "I will raise him up on the last day").

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H2416 - chay|חַי]] (*chay*) appears first in the tree of life (Gen 2:9) and in God's self-identification as "the living God" (Deut 5:26). Life is God's gift, breathed into humanity (Gen 2:7). The LXX consistently uses ζωή, and John's theology of life is a sustained meditation on Genesis 1-3: the life lost in the garden is restored in Christ.
