# κύριος — Kyrios

Lord, master — the most theologically explosive title applied to Jesus in the NT. The LXX used *kyrios* to translate the divine name [[concepts/hebrew/H3068 - YHWH|YHWH]]. When the NT calls Jesus *kyrios*, it places Him within the identity of Israel's God. The confession "Jesus is Lord" is the earliest Christian creed (Rom 10:9; 1 Cor 12:3).

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 10#v9|Rom 10:9]] — "If you confess with your mouth Jesus as Lord (*kyrios*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Philippians/Philippians 2#v11|Phil 2:11]] — "Every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord (*kyrios*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 2#v36|Acts 2:36]] — "God has made Him both Lord (*kyrios*) and Christ"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/1 Corinthians/1 Corinthians 8#v6|1 Cor 8:6]] — "One Lord (*kyrios*), Jesus Christ"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 20#v28|John 20:28]] — "My Lord (*kyrios*) and my God"

## Theological Significance

The confession "Jesus is *kyrios*" was both politically subversive (Caesar claimed the title) and theologically revolutionary (YHWH bore the title). The Philippians 2:11 hymn quotes Isaiah 45:23, where YHWH declares that every knee will bow to Him — and applies it to Jesus. This is not the deification of a human but the identification of Jesus within the divine identity.

Paul's creedal formula in 1 Corinthians 8:6 — "one God (*theos*), the Father… and one Lord (*kyrios*), Jesus Christ" — reformulates the Shema (Deut 6:4) to include Jesus within the confession of monotheism. Christ shares the *kyrios* title with YHWH, not as a rival deity but as the revealed identity of Israel's God in human flesh.
