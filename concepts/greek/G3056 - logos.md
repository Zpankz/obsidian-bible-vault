# λόγος — Logos

One of the most theologically dense words in the NT, carrying freight from both Hebrew (*davar* — word/event) and Greek philosophical (*logos* — reason/ordering principle) traditions. John's Prologue deliberately fuses both backgrounds.

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Christological title | The eternal Word, second person of Trinity | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 1#v1\|John 1:1]] |
| God's spoken message | Prophetic word, Scripture | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Hebrews/Hebrews 4#v12\|Heb 4:12]] |
| Gospel message | The word of God/truth | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Acts/Acts 6#v7\|Acts 6:7]] |
| Human speech | Statement, account | Matt 12:36 |
| Reason/account | Reckoning | Matt 18:23 |

## The Johannine Logos

John 1:1-18 is structured as a theological commentary on Genesis 1:

| John 1 | Genesis 1 | Connection |
|:---|:---|:---|
| "In the beginning was the **Word**" | "In the beginning **God** created" | The Word = the creative agent |
| "All things came into being through Him" | "God **said**… and it was" | Speech → creation |
| "In Him was **life**" | "Let the earth bring forth **living** creatures" | Life's source identified |
| "The **light** shines in the darkness" | "Let there be **light**… God separated light from darkness" | Light/darkness dualism |

The Logos is not an impersonal principle but a person who "became flesh" (1:14) — the Creator entering creation.

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H1697 - davar|דָּבָר]] (*davar*) means both "word" and "event/thing" — in Hebrew thought, to speak is to act. God's word *does* what it *says* (Isa 55:11). This is the conceptual world John inhabits: the Logos is God's self-expression that simultaneously reveals and accomplishes.
