# πιστεύω — Pisteuo

To believe, trust, rely upon — the verbal form of πίστις (faith). John's Gospel uses this verb 98 times (never the noun πίστις), making believing an active, personal response to Christ rather than a static possession. Believing is not intellectual assent but personal entrustment.

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Saving faith | Believing in/on Christ | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/John/John 3#v16\|John 3:16]] |
| Trust in God's promises | Abraham's faith as paradigm | [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Romans/Romans 4#v3\|Rom 4:3]] |
| Intellectual assent | Believing that something is true | James 2:19 |
| Entrusting | Committing something to someone | John 2:24; Luke 16:11 |

## Theological Significance

John 3:16 makes believing the one condition for eternal life: "whoever believes in Him shall not perish." The Johannine construction πιστεύω εἰς ("believe into") is distinctive — it implies movement toward and union with Christ, not merely belief *about* Him.

Paul correlates believing with justification: "Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness" (Rom 4:3, quoting Gen 15:6). Faith is the instrument through which grace is received (Eph 2:8-9).

## OT Background

The Hebrew [[concepts/hebrew/H539 - aman|אָמַן]] (*aman*) means to be firm, to confirm, to trust — the root behind "Amen." Genesis 15:6 is the foundational text: Abraham "believed in YHWH, and He credited it to him as righteousness." Both Paul (Rom 4; Gal 3) and James (Jas 2:23) build their theologies of faith on this verse.
