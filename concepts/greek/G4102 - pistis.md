# πίστις — Pistis

Faith, trust, faithfulness — the noun that stands at the center of Paul's soteriology. "By grace you have been saved through faith (*pistis*)" (Eph 2:8). *Pistis* carries both subjective (human trust in God) and objective (the faith once delivered to the saints, Jude 3) senses. It also means "faithfulness" — the reliability of the one trusted.

## Theological Significance

Habakkuk 2:4 — "the righteous shall live by *emunah/pistis*" — is the OT text that lit the Reformation. Paul cites it three times to establish that right standing before God comes through faith, not through works of Torah. *Pistis* is the empty hand that receives grace — it is not a meritorious work but the instrument of receptivity.

Hebrews 11 defines *pistis* as "the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen" and then illustrates it through the OT saints. Faith is forward-looking, trusting God's promises despite contrary evidence. It is rooted not in human strength but in God's faithfulness (*pistis* in its objective sense) — we can believe because God is believable.
