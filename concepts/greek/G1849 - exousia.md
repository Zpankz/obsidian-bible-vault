# ἐξουσία — Exousia

Authority — the legitimate right to act, contrasted with δύναμις (raw power). Where *dynamis* is the ability to do, *exousia* is the right to do. Jesus teaches "as one having authority" (Matt 7:29), and all authority in heaven and earth is given to Him (Matt 28:18).

## Semantic Range

| Context | Meaning | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Christ's authority | Delegated from the Father | [[bibles/NASB-IL/Matthew/Matthew 28#v18\|Matt 28:18]] |

| Believers' right | Authority to become children of God | [[bibles/NASB-IL/John/John 1#v12\|John 1:12]] |

## Theological Significance

The Great Commission rests on Christ's universal authority: "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore…" (Matt 28:18-19). The church's mission is grounded not in its own power but in Christ's delegated authority.

Colossians 2:15 declares that Christ "disarmed the rulers and authorities" at the cross — the spiritual powers that held humanity captive have been publicly defeated. The *exousiai* that once ruled are now subject to the risen Lord.

## OT Background

YHWH's sovereignty over all creation (Ps 103:19) is the foundation. Daniel 7:14 grants the "Son of Man" dominion (מְמְשַׁל, *memshal*) and authority over all peoples and nations — the text behind Jesus' claim in Matthew 28:18. All earthly and cosmic authority derives from and answers to God.
