# עָשָׂה — Asah

To do, make, produce — the general-purpose Hebrew verb for making or doing. In the creation narrative, *asah* complements [[concepts/hebrew/H1254 - bara|bara]] ("to create from nothing"): while *bara* is reserved for God, *asah* can be used of both divine and human activity. God *makes* the firmament, the luminaries, and the animals.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v7|Gen 1:7]] — "God made (*asah*) the expanse"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v16|Gen 1:16]] — "God made (*asah*) the two great lights"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v26|Gen 1:26]] — "Let Us make (*asah*) man in Our image"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v31|Gen 1:31]] — "God saw all that He had made (*asah*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 31#v17|Exod 31:17]] — "In six days the LORD made (*asah*) heaven and earth"

## Theological Significance

The distinction between *bara* and *asah* in Genesis 1 is theologically significant. *Bara* (vv. 1, 21, 27) marks ontological novelty — the absolute beginning of creation, the first animal life, and the human image-bearer. *Asah* marks the shaping and arranging of created elements within the existing framework. Together they portray God as both transcendent originator and immanent craftsman.

Humans are called to *asah* in imitation of God — to make, build, cultivate, and produce. The mandate to "be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth" is a call to participate in God's creative *asah*. The NT equivalent, [[concepts/greek/G4160 - poieo|poieo]], carries the same range, and Ephesians 2:10 declares believers to be God's "workmanship (*poiema*), created in Christ Jesus for good works."
