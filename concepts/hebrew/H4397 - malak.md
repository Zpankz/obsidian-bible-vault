# מַלְאָךְ — Malak

Messenger or angel — from the root meaning "to send." The word applies to both human messengers (2 Sam 11:19) and divine/heavenly beings sent by God. The "angel of the LORD" (*malak YHWH*) is a special figure who speaks and acts as YHWH Himself, blurring the line between the messenger and the sender.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 16#v7|Gen 16:7]] — "The angel (*malak*) of the LORD found her by a spring"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 3#v2|Exod 3:2]] — "The angel (*malak*) of the LORD appeared to him in a blazing fire"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 22#v11|Gen 22:11]] — "The angel (*malak*) of the LORD called to him from heaven"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Malachi/Malachi 3#v1|Mal 3:1]] — "I am going to send My messenger (*malak*)"

## Theological Significance

The *malak YHWH* ("angel of the LORD") is one of the OT's most intriguing figures. In passages like Genesis 16:7-13 and Exodus 3:2-6, this messenger speaks as God in the first person, and those who encounter him recognize they have seen God. Church fathers identified this figure as a pre-incarnate appearance of Christ (a Christophany).

Whether or not this identification is correct, the *malak YHWH* reveals a theology of mediated divine presence — God comes near without ceasing to be transcendent. This prepares for the ultimate sending: "The Word became flesh" (John 1:14). The book of Malachi itself plays on the word: the prophet's name means "my messenger," and he announces the coming of the ultimate [[concepts/greek/G32 - angelos|messenger]] who will purify the sons of Levi.
