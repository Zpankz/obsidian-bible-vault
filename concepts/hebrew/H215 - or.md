# אוֹר — Or

Light — the first created reality called forth by God's speech (Gen 1:3). Used both literally (physical illumination) and metaphorically (divine presence, truth, salvation, life). The noun *or* and the verb *or* ("to shine, give light") share the same root.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v3|Gen 1:3]] — "Let there be light (*or*); and there was light"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 27#v1|Ps 27:1]] — "The LORD is my light (*or*) and my salvation"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 9#v2|Isa 9:2]] — "The people who walk in darkness have seen a great light (*or*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 119#v105|Ps 119:105]] — "Your word is a lamp… a light (*or*) to my path"

## Theological Significance

Light in Genesis 1:3 precedes the creation of luminaries (1:14-16), suggesting that *or* is fundamentally a divine attribute — the radiance of God's presence — before it is a physical phenomenon. This prepares for the prophetic identification of God himself as Israel's "everlasting light" (Isa 60:19-20).

The light/darkness dualism established in Genesis 1:3-4 through the verb [[concepts/hebrew/H914 - badal|badal]] ("to separate") becomes a controlling metaphor throughout Scripture: light represents holiness, truth, and life, while [[concepts/hebrew/H2822 - choshek|darkness]] represents chaos, evil, and death. John's Gospel imports this directly: "the Light shines in the darkness" (John 1:5).
