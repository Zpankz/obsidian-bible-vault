# נָבִיא — Navi

Prophet — God's authorized spokesman, called to declare the divine *davar* to Israel. The *navi* is not primarily a fortune-teller but a covenant enforcer — one who calls Israel back to Torah faithfulness, announces judgment for disobedience, and proclaims hope for restoration. Moses is the paradigmatic prophet (Deut 18:15).

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 18#v15|Deut 18:15]] — "The LORD your God will raise up for you a prophet (*navi*) like me"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Amos/Amos 3#v7|Amos 3:7]] — "He reveals His secret counsel to His servants the prophets (*neviim*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Numbers/Numbers 12#v6|Num 12:6]] — "If there is a prophet (*navi*) among you, I the LORD make Myself known to him"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 1#v5|Jer 1:5]] — "I have appointed you a prophet (*navi*) to the nations"

## Theological Significance

The *navi* stands in the divine council, hears God's deliberation, and carries the verdict to the people. True prophecy is verified by fulfillment (Deut 18:22) and by conformity to Torah (Deut 13:1-5). The prophetic movement is the conscience of Israel's covenant life.

Deuteronomy 18:15 promises a "prophet like Moses" — one who will mediate a direct encounter with God. The NT identifies Jesus as this ultimate *navi* (Acts 3:22-23), but He is more than a prophet: He does not merely deliver the word but *is* the Word. The prophetic office finds its fulfillment and end in Him.
