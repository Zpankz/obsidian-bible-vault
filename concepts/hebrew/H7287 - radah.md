# רָדָה — Radah

To rule, have dominion, exercise authority — the verb God uses when commissioning humanity to govern creation: "let them rule (*radah*) over the fish… birds… cattle… and over all the earth" (Gen 1:26). This is royal language: the image-bearers are installed as God's vice-regents, delegated to steward the created order.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v26|Gen 1:26]] — "Let them rule (*radah*) over the fish of the sea"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v28|Gen 1:28]] — "Fill the earth, and subdue it; and rule (*radah*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 8#v6|Ps 8:6]] — "You make him to rule over the works of Your hands"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 72#v8|Ps 72:8]] — "May he also rule (*radah*) from sea to sea"

## Theological Significance

The dominion mandate is not a license for exploitation but a commission of stewardship. *Radah* is kingly authority exercised under God's ultimate sovereignty — the image-bearer rules *for* God, not instead of God. Psalm 72 describes the ideal king whose *radah* brings justice, peace, and flourishing — a portrait of rule as service.

The fall distorts *radah* into domination and abuse (Gen 3:16-19), and the rest of Scripture traces the quest for its restoration. Christ is the true image-bearer who exercises perfect dominion (Heb 2:6-9, citing Ps 8), and He restores the vocation to redeemed humanity. The new creation is the recovery of *radah* as it was meant to be — life-giving rule that reflects God's own care for His world.
