# כָּבוֹד — Kavod

Glory — literally "weight, heaviness." God's *kavod* is His manifest presence, the visible radiance of His character and power. It filled the tabernacle as cloud and fire (Exod 40:34), appeared on Sinai in terrifying splendor, and is the goal of all creation: "the earth will be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD" (Hab 2:14).

## Theological Significance

*Kavod* as "weight" suggests that God's glory is the gravity of His being — the undeniable, substantive reality of who He is. Moses asks to see it and is told no one can see God's face and live (Exod 33:20). Yet the trajectory of Scripture is toward ever-greater disclosure: tabernacle, temple, and finally incarnation.

Ezekiel's vision of the *kavod* departing the temple (Ezek 10) is the nadir of Israel's story — God's visible presence leaving His house due to Israel's sin. John 1:14 announces the reversal: "The Word became flesh and dwelt (*tabernacled*) among us, and we beheld His glory (*doxa*)" — the *kavod* has returned, embodied in Jesus, and will ultimately fill all creation (Rev 21:23).
