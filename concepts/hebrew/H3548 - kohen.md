# כֹּהֵן — Kohen

Priest — the authorized mediator between God and the people, responsible for sacrificial worship, teaching Torah, and maintaining the distinction between holy and common. The Aaronic priesthood was established at Sinai (Exod 28-29), but the concept of priesthood precedes it: Melchizedek (Gen 14:18) and Israel's collective calling as "a kingdom of priests" (Exod 19:6).

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 14#v18|Gen 14:18]] — "Melchizedek… was a priest (*kohen*) of God Most High"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 19#v6|Exod 19:6]] — "You shall be to Me a kingdom of priests (*kohanim*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Leviticus/Leviticus 10#v10|Lev 10:10]] — The priest distinguishes holy from common
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 110#v4|Ps 110:4]] — "You are a priest (*kohen*) forever according to the order of Melchizedek"

## Theological Significance

The *kohen* embodies the need for mediation — humanity cannot approach the holy God directly without an authorized representative. The priestly system teaches that access to God is costly (requiring sacrifice) and ordered (requiring holiness). Yet Israel as a whole is called to be a priestly nation — mediating God's presence to the world.

Hebrews 7-10 argues that the Levitical priesthood was always provisional, pointing to the eternal priesthood of Christ "after the order of Melchizedek" (Ps 110:4). Christ is both *kohen* and sacrifice, fulfilling and transcending the entire system. The NT then extends the priestly vocation to all believers (1 Pet 2:9), fulfilling Exodus 19:6.
