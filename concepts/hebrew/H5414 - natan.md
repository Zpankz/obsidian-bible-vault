# נָתַן — Natan

To give — one of the most frequent verbs in the OT, describing the fundamental dynamic of God's relationship with creation: God gives. He gives life, land, law, victory, food, wisdom, and ultimately Himself. The prophet Nathan (same root) embodies the gift of divine speech to the king.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v29|Gen 1:29]] — "I have given (*natan*) you every plant"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 12#v7|Gen 12:7]] — "To your descendants I will give (*natan*) this land"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Leviticus/Leviticus 17#v11|Lev 17:11]] — "I have given (*natan*) it to you on the altar to make atonement"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 8#v18|Deut 8:18]] — "It is He who is giving (*natan*) you power to make wealth"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 36#v26|Ezek 36:26]] — "I will give (*natan*) you a new heart"

## Theological Significance

*Natan* reveals God as the initiating giver in every sphere. Creation is given (Gen 1:29), the land is given (Gen 12:7), the Torah is given (Exod 24:12), atonement is given (Lev 17:11), and the new heart is given (Ezek 36:26). Grace is not a NT innovation — it is the OT's primary verb for God's action.

The climactic divine gift anticipated in the OT is life itself renewed: "I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you." The NT reads this trajectory through to its end: "God so loved the world that He *gave* His only begotten Son" (John 3:16). The entire Bible is the story of *natan* — divine generosity that costs God everything.
