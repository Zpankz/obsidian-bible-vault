# לֵב — Lev

Heart — the center of the human person in Hebrew anthropology, encompassing what modern English divides among "mind," "will," and "emotions." The *lev* thinks, decides, feels, and intends. It is the seat of moral character and the target of God's transforming work.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 6#v5|Deut 6:5]] — "Love the LORD your God with all your heart (*lev*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/1 Samuel/1 Samuel 16#v7|1 Sam 16:7]] — "The LORD looks at the heart (*lev*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 17#v9|Jer 17:9]] — "The heart (*lev*) is more deceitful than all else"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 36#v26|Ezek 36:26]] — "I will give you a new heart (*lev*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Proverbs/Proverbs 4#v23|Prov 4:23]] — "Watch over your heart (*lev*) with all diligence"

## Theological Significance

The Shema commands love for God "with all your heart" — not sentimental feeling but total inner orientation, the alignment of thought, will, and desire toward YHWH. The *lev* is where covenant faithfulness or unfaithfulness originates: outward obedience without heart engagement is empty ritual (Isa 29:13).

The prophets diagnose Israel's core problem as a diseased *lev* (Jer 17:9) and prescribe a radical cure: God Himself will replace the heart of stone with a heart of flesh (Ezek 36:26). This heart-transplant is the essence of the new covenant — not external compliance but internal transformation, the Torah written on the *lev* itself (Jer 31:33).
