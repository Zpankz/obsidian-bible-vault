# אוֹת — Ot

A sign, mark, or token — a visible indicator that points beyond itself to divine reality. Used for covenant signs (rainbow, circumcision, Sabbath), miraculous portents (plagues of Egypt), and prophetic confirmations.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v14|Gen 1:14]] — "Let them be for signs (*otot*) and for seasons"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 9#v12|Gen 9:12]] — "This is the sign (*ot*) of the covenant" (rainbow)
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 17#v11|Gen 17:11]] — Circumcision as "a sign (*ot*) of the covenant"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 31#v13|Exod 31:13]] — The Sabbath as "a sign (*ot*) between Me and you"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 7#v14|Isa 7:14]] — "The Lord Himself will give you a sign (*ot*)"

## Theological Significance

An *ot* is never merely a wonder to dazzle but always a sign that communicates — it has semantic content, pointing to God's faithfulness, power, or promise. The great covenant signs (rainbow, circumcision, Sabbath) are recurring *otot* that make invisible realities visible and bind communities to their covenant Lord.

In the prophets, *ot* takes on predictive force — a sign given in advance to confirm that God's word will come to pass (Isa 7:14). The NT equivalent is [[concepts/greek/G4592 - semeion|semeion]], which John's Gospel uses for Jesus' miracles precisely because they are signs revealing his identity as the sent one of God.
