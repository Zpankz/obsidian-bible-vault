# יהוה — YHWH

The personal, covenant name of the God of Israel — the Tetragrammaton. The most sacred and frequent name for God in the OT (6,828 occurrences). Revealed to Moses at the burning bush (Exod 3:14-15), it is linked to the verb *hayah* ("to be"), expressing God's self-existence, faithfulness, and active presence with His people.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 3#v14|Exod 3:14-15]] — "I AM WHO I AM… YHWH… this is My name forever"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 6#v3|Exod 6:3]] — "By My name, YHWH, I did not make Myself known to them"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 6#v4|Deut 6:4]] — "YHWH is our God, YHWH is one"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 34#v6|Exod 34:6]] — "YHWH, YHWH God, compassionate and gracious"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 42#v8|Isa 42:8]] — "I am YHWH, that is My name"

## Theological Significance

YHWH is not a philosophical label but a relational name — it is the name God gives to Moses in the context of rescue, the name associated with covenant commitment and redemptive action. The Exodus is the definitive revelation of what YHWH means: "I am the one who is there for you, who acts to save."

The early church's application of the LXX title [[concepts/greek/G2962 - kyrios|Kyrios]] (Lord) — the standard translation of YHWH — to Jesus of Nazareth is the most momentous theological move in the NT. When Paul says "every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord (*Kyrios*)" (Phil 2:11), he is citing Isaiah 45:23, where every knee bows to YHWH. The divine name crosses the testamental boundary in the person of Christ.
