# רָקִיעַ — Raqia

Expanse or firmament — the structure God placed on Day Two to separate the waters above from the waters below (Gen 1:6-8). From the verb *raqa* ("to beat out, spread, stamp"), evoking the image of hammered metal spread thin. God calls the *raqia* "heavens" (*shamayim*), making it the visible sky-dome of ancient cosmology.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v6|Gen 1:6]] — "Let there be an expanse (*raqia*) in the midst of the waters"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v8|Gen 1:8]] — "God called the expanse (*raqia*) heaven"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v14|Gen 1:14]] — "Let there be lights in the expanse (*raqia*) of the heavens"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 19#v1|Ps 19:1]] — "The expanse (*raqia*) is declaring the work of His hands"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 1#v22|Ezek 1:22]] — "Over the heads of the living beings there was something like an expanse (*raqia*)"

## Theological Significance

The *raqia* is God's act of imposing structure on chaos — separating the threatening waters to create habitable space. It is a boundary that holds back the destructive potential of the deep, testifying to God's sustaining power. Creation is not self-maintaining; it persists because God upholds the boundaries He set.

Psalm 19:1 transforms the *raqia* from a cosmological structure into a theological witness — the sky itself declares God's glory. Ezekiel's throne-vision (Ezek 1:22-26) places the *raqia* above the cherubim as the floor of God's throne, connecting earthly cosmos to heavenly sovereignty. The physical expanse images the metaphysical reality: God reigns above, and creation is sustained below.
