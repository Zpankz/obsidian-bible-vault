# קֹדֶשׁ — Qodesh

Holiness, the holy, sanctuary — the fundamental attribute of God that defines the gulf between Creator and creature. *Qodesh* means "set apart, other, distinct." God is holy in that He is utterly unique, morally pure, and transcendently different from everything created. Holiness is not merely an attribute among others — it is the attribute that qualifies all others.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 3#v5|Exod 3:5]] — "The place on which you are standing is holy (*qodesh*) ground"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Leviticus/Leviticus 19#v2|Lev 19:2]] — "You shall be holy (*qadosh*), for I the LORD your God am holy"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 6#v3|Isa 6:3]] — "Holy (*qadosh*), holy, holy is the LORD of hosts"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 15#v11|Exod 15:11]] — "Who is like You, majestic in holiness (*qodesh*)?"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 99#v9|Ps 99:9]] — "The LORD our God is holy (*qadosh*)"

## Theological Significance

The threefold "holy, holy, holy" of Isaiah 6:3 — the only divine attribute repeated three times — expresses the superlative degree. Holiness is not one of God's properties but the atmosphere in which all His properties exist. His love is holy love, His justice is holy justice, His power is holy power.

The Levitical code's detailed regulations about clean/unclean, sacred/common are pedagogy: Israel learns through daily life that approaching the holy God requires [[concepts/hebrew/H914 - badal|separation]] from defilement. The NT does not abolish holiness but universalizes it — the church itself becomes the temple, and all believers are called to be [[concepts/greek/G40 - hagios|hagioi]] ("holy ones/saints"), set apart in Christ.
