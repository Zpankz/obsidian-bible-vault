# שָׁמַיִם — Shamayim

Heavens, sky — the dual/plural form expressing the full extent of the sky and the divine realm above. In Genesis 1:1, "the heavens and the earth" (*hashamayim ve'ha'arets*) is a merism for the totality of creation. *Shamayim* refers to the visible sky (where birds fly), the celestial realm (where stars shine), and God's dwelling place.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v1|Gen 1:1]] — "God created the heavens (*shamayim*) and the earth"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 10#v14|Deut 10:14]] — "To the LORD your God belong heaven (*shamayim*) and the highest heavens"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 19#v1|Ps 19:1]] — "The heavens (*shamayim*) are telling of the glory of God"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 66#v1|Isa 66:1]] — "Heaven (*shamayim*) is My throne and the earth is My footstool"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 65#v17|Isa 65:17]] — "I create new heavens (*shamayim*) and a new earth"

## Theological Significance

*Shamayim* establishes the vertical dimension of biblical cosmology: God is "above," not in spatial terms but in terms of authority, transcendence, and glory. The heavens declare God's glory (Ps 19:1), yet even "the highest heavens cannot contain Him" (1 Kgs 8:27). God is associated with *shamayim* without being confined to it.

The prophetic hope for "new heavens and a new earth" (Isa 65:17) is not the destruction of the cosmos but its renovation — the renewal of the entire created order. This pairs *shamayim* with [[concepts/hebrew/H776 - erets|erets]] at both the beginning and end of the biblical narrative: "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth" (Gen 1:1) and "I saw a new heaven and a new earth" (Rev 21:1).
