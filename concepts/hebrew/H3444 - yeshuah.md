# יְשׁוּעָה — Yeshuah

Salvation, deliverance — from the root *yasha* ("to save, deliver, make wide/spacious"). The name Yeshua (Jesus) is derived from this root, making the name itself a theological statement: "YHWH saves." In the OT, *yeshuah* refers to concrete acts of deliverance (military, legal, existential) that reveal God's saving character.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 14#v13|Exod 14:13]] — "Stand still and see the salvation (*yeshuah*) of the LORD"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 3#v8|Ps 3:8]] — "Salvation (*yeshuah*) belongs to the LORD"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 12#v2|Isa 12:2]] — "God is my salvation (*yeshuah*); I will trust and not be afraid"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 49#v6|Isa 49:6]] — "My salvation (*yeshuah*) to the end of the earth"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 118#v14|Ps 118:14]] — "The LORD… has become my salvation (*yeshuah*)"

## Theological Significance

OT salvation is not primarily spiritual escape but holistic rescue — deliverance from enemies, oppression, sickness, and death. God's *yeshuah* is visible, public, and historical, supremely demonstrated at the Red Sea. This concrete, this-worldly salvation is the foundation upon which the NT builds its fuller doctrine of [[concepts/greek/G4991 - soteria|soteria]].

The angel's instruction in Matthew 1:21 — "you shall call His name Jesus (*Yesous*), for He will save His people from their sins" — grounds the name of Christ in this Hebrew root. Jesus *is* the *yeshuah* of God, the saving act of YHWH incarnated in a person. Every OT cry of "salvation belongs to the LORD" finds its ultimate answer in Him.
