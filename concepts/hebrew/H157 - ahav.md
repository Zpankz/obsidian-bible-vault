# אָהַב — Ahav

The primary Hebrew verb for love, encompassing the full range from divine election love to human affection, romantic desire, and friendship. Unlike English, *ahav* is not primarily an emotion but a covenantal commitment expressed in action.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 6#v5|Deut 6:5]] — "You shall love (*ahav*) the LORD your God with all your heart"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 7#v8|Deut 7:8]] — "Because the LORD loved (*ahav*) you"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Leviticus/Leviticus 19#v18|Lev 19:18]] — "You shall love your neighbor as yourself"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Hosea/Hosea 3#v1|Hos 3:1]] — "Love a woman who is loved… even as the LORD loves the sons of Israel"

## Theological Significance

God's *ahav* in Deuteronomy is explicitly elective — He loved Israel not because of their merit but by sovereign choice (Deut 7:7-8). This love is the ground of covenant and the motive for redemption. It precedes command: God first loves, then calls Israel to love in return (Deut 6:5).

The prophets, especially Hosea, develop *ahav* as a metaphor of marital fidelity — God's persistent love for an unfaithful people. This covenantal love-language becomes the background for the NT's [[concepts/greek/G26 - agape|agape]], which the LXX consistently uses to translate *ahav*.
