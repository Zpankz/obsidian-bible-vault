# שַׁדַּי — Shaddai

Almighty or All-Sufficient — the divine name by which God was known to the patriarchs before the revelation of YHWH at the burning bush (Exod 6:3). Typically combined with *El* as *El Shaddai* ("God Almighty"). The etymology is debated: possibly from "mountain" (power), "breast" (nourishment), or "to overpower."

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 17#v1|Gen 17:1]] — "I am God Almighty (*El Shaddai*); walk before Me and be blameless"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 28#v3|Gen 28:3]] — "May God Almighty (*El Shaddai*) bless you"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 49#v25|Gen 49:25]] — "By the Almighty (*Shaddai*) who blesses you"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 6#v3|Exod 6:3]] — "I appeared to Abraham… as God Almighty (*El Shaddai*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Job/Job 5#v17|Job 5:17]] — "Do not despise the discipline of the Almighty (*Shaddai*)"

## Theological Significance

*El Shaddai* is the patriarchal name for God, associated with promises of fertility, protection, and blessing (Gen 17:1; 28:3; 35:11). It reveals a God of overwhelming power who nonetheless commits Himself to a barren family, promising descendants as numerous as the stars. The name speaks to divine sufficiency: God's resources are inexhaustible.

In Job, *Shaddai* appears 31 times — more than in any other book — where it highlights the tension between God's almighty power and human suffering. The LXX renders it as *pantokrator* ("ruler of all"), which Revelation adopts as a title for God (Rev 1:8; 4:8), connecting patriarchal promise to eschatological fulfillment.
