# יָרֵא — Yare

To fear, revere, stand in awe — the word for the foundational human response to the holy God. *Yare* covers the spectrum from terror before divine judgment to reverent worship before divine goodness. "The fear of the LORD" (*yirat YHWH*) is the beginning of wisdom (Prov 1:7), the orienting posture of all true knowledge.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 22#v12|Gen 22:12]] — "Now I know that you fear (*yare*) God"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 14#v31|Exod 14:31]] — "The people feared (*yare*) the LORD"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Proverbs/Proverbs 1#v7|Prov 1:7]] — "The fear (*yirah*) of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 111#v10|Ps 111:10]] — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 6#v5|Isa 6:5]] — "Woe is me! For I am ruined!" (Isaiah's theophanic fear)

## Theological Significance

The fear of the LORD is not cowering dread but the right calibration of the soul before the infinite God. It is the recognition that God is God and we are not — the shattering of autonomy that makes genuine knowledge, worship, and obedience possible. Without *yare*, religion becomes presumption.

Paradoxically, the same God who commands fear also commands "fear not" (*al tira*) — the most frequently repeated command in Scripture. The fear of the LORD drives out all lesser fears. Those who truly revere God need fear nothing else: "Do not fear (*yare*), for I am with you" (Isa 41:10).
