# מִשְׁפָּט — Mishpat

Justice, judgment, ordinance — the act of rendering right decisions and the standard by which they are rendered. *Mishpat* is both procedural (fair trial, due process) and substantive (care for the vulnerable, equity for the oppressed). It is frequently paired with *tsedaqah* (righteousness) as the twin demands of God.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 18#v25|Gen 18:25]] — "Shall not the Judge of all the earth deal justly (*mishpat*)?"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Micah/Micah 6#v8|Mic 6:8]] — "To do justice (*mishpat*), to love kindness"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 1#v17|Isa 1:17]] — "Seek justice (*mishpat*), reprove the ruthless"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 89#v14|Ps 89:14]] — "Righteousness and justice (*mishpat*) are the foundation of Your throne"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Amos/Amos 5#v24|Amos 5:24]] — "Let justice (*mishpat*) roll down like waters"

## Theological Significance

*Mishpat* is not mere legal procedure but the moral order of God's kingdom. When the prophets demand *mishpat*, they are calling for a society that reflects God's character — where the widow, orphan, and stranger receive protection, and where economic and social systems conform to covenant standards.

God Himself is the ultimate source and executor of *mishpat* (Ps 89:14). The messianic hope includes a king who will "establish justice" (Isa 9:7; 42:1-4). Jesus' proclamation of the kingdom is the arrival of God's *mishpat* — not through force but through the cross, where divine justice and mercy meet (Rom 3:25-26).
