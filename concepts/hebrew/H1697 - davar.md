# דָּבָר — Davar

Word, matter, thing, event — a word with extraordinary semantic range. In Hebrew thought, *davar* simultaneously means what is spoken and what happens; speech and event are inseparable. God's *davar* is never mere information; it is performative power that accomplishes what it declares.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 15#v1|Gen 15:1]] — "The word (*davar*) of the LORD came to Abram"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 33#v6|Ps 33:6]] — "By the word (*davar*) of the LORD the heavens were made"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 55#v11|Isa 55:11]] — "My word (*davar*)… will not return to Me empty"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Deuteronomy/Deuteronomy 8#v3|Deut 8:3]] — "Man lives by everything that proceeds out of the mouth of the LORD"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 1#v4|Jer 1:4]] — "The word (*davar*) of the LORD came to me"

## Theological Significance

The prophetic formula "the word of the LORD came to…" occurs hundreds of times, establishing *davar* as the primary mode of divine revelation. God's *davar* creates (Ps 33:6), sustains (Deut 8:3), judges (Hos 6:5), and heals (Ps 107:20). It is the active extension of God's will into the world.

The dual meaning of *davar* as both "word" and "thing/event" is theologically crucial: in the Hebrew worldview, God's speech is itself an event that reshapes reality. This is the conceptual foundation for the Johannine [[concepts/greek/G3056 - logos|Logos]] — the Word who was with God and *was* God, through whom all things came into being.
