# רָאָה — Raah

To see, look, perceive, appear — the primary Hebrew verb of sight, with theological weight far beyond physical vision. When God "sees" (*raah*), He attends with care and acts in response. When humans "see" God, they encounter revelation. The creation refrain "God *saw* that it was good" makes divine seeing the act of evaluation and approval.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v4|Gen 1:4]] — "God saw (*raah*) that the light was good"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 16#v13|Gen 16:13]] — "You are a God who sees (*El Roi*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 22#v14|Gen 22:14]] — "The LORD Will Provide (*YHWH Yireh*)" — lit. "the LORD will see"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 3#v7|Exod 3:7]] — "I have surely seen (*raah*) the affliction of My people"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/1 Samuel/1 Samuel 16#v7|1 Sam 16:7]] — "God sees (*raah*) not as man sees"

## Theological Significance

"God saw" in Genesis 1 is not passive observation but active evaluation — His seeing confirms that creation matches His intention. God's seeing is always purposeful: He sees Hagar's distress (Gen 16:13), Israel's slavery (Exod 3:7), and the heart rather than the appearance (1 Sam 16:7). To be seen by God is to be known and cared for.

Genesis 22:14 reveals the deepest layer: "the LORD will see" (*yireh*) means "the LORD will provide." In Hebrew thought, God's seeing and God's providing are the same act — when God turns His gaze toward a need, the provision follows. This is why *Jehovah Jireh* (the LORD provides) is literally "the LORD sees."
