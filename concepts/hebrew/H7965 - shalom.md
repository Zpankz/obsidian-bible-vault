# שָׁלוֹם — Shalom

Peace, wholeness, completeness, well-being — far richer than mere absence of conflict. From the root *shalam* ("to be complete, whole, sound"), *shalom* describes the state of total flourishing when all relationships — with God, with others, with creation — are rightly ordered. It is the intended condition of creation and the goal of redemption.

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Jeremiah/Jeremiah 29#v11|Jer 29:11]] — "Plans for welfare (*shalom*) and not for calamity"

## Theological Significance

*Shalom* is the OT's comprehensive vision of the good life — material prosperity, physical health, social harmony, and spiritual communion with God, all held together. It is the Aaronic blessing's climactic word (Num 6:26) and the messianic king's defining attribute: "Prince of *Shalom*" (Isa 9:6). Where God rules, *shalom* reigns.

Isaiah 53:5 makes the stunning claim that the Servant's suffering purchases *shalom* for others — "the punishment that brought us *shalom* was upon Him." Peace is not achieved through human effort but received through vicarious atonement. The NT [[concepts/greek/G1515 - eirene|eirene]] carries this forward: "He Himself is our peace" (Eph 2:14), and the gospel is "the good news of peace" (Eph 6:15).
