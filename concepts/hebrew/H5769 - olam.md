# עוֹלָם — Olam

Eternity, forever, ancient time, age — from a root meaning "hidden, concealed," *olam* denotes time whose boundaries are hidden from human view, extending either indefinitely into the past or the future. It does not necessarily mean "timelessness" in the philosophical sense but rather time beyond human horizon.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 21#v33|Gen 21:33]] — "The Everlasting (*olam*) God"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 90#v2|Ps 90:2]] — "From everlasting (*olam*) to everlasting (*olam*), You are God"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ecclesiastes/Ecclesiastes 3#v11|Eccl 3:11]] — "He has set eternity (*olam*) in their heart"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 40#v28|Isa 40:28]] — "The Everlasting (*olam*) God, the LORD"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Daniel/Daniel 12#v2|Dan 12:2]] — "To everlasting (*olam*) life… to everlasting contempt"

## Theological Significance

*Olam* qualifies God's attributes and covenants: His *chesed* is *le'olam* ("forever," Ps 136), His *berith* is an *olam* covenant (Gen 17:7), and He Himself is the *El Olam* ("Everlasting God," Gen 21:33). The word does not merely add duration but signals that God's character and commitments transcend every temporal boundary.

Ecclesiastes 3:11 makes the striking claim that God has placed *olam* in the human heart — an awareness of eternity that makes human beings restless within the confines of temporal existence. This "eternity in the heart" is the anthropological basis for hope, longing for God, and dissatisfaction with a purely material life. The NT concept of "eternal life" (*zoe aionios*) builds directly on *olam*.
