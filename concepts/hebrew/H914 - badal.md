# בָּדַל — Badal

To separate, divide, or make a distinction. A key creation verb: God's creative work in Genesis 1 is fundamentally an act of separation — light from darkness, waters above from waters below, day from night. The same verb later governs Israel's holiness code: to be holy is to be *separated*.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v4|Gen 1:4]] — "God separated (*badal*) the light from the darkness"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v7|Gen 1:7]] — "He separated (*badal*) the waters below from the waters above"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Leviticus/Leviticus 20#v26|Lev 20:26]] — "You are to be holy to Me… I have set you apart (*badal*) from the peoples"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 22#v26|Ezek 22:26]] — "They make no distinction (*badal*) between the holy and the profane"

## Theological Significance

*Badal* reveals that creation is fundamentally about ordering — God imposes distinctions on the formless void, turning chaos into cosmos. Without separation, there is no identity, no meaning, no life. This cosmological principle becomes an ethical one: Israel must maintain the distinctions God has established.

The priestly vocation is defined by *badal* — to teach the people to distinguish holy from common, clean from unclean (Lev 10:10). When Ezekiel condemns the priests for failing to make distinctions, he is accusing them of reversing creation itself, allowing chaos to reenter the ordered world.
