# הָיָה — Hayah

The Hebrew verb "to be" — not static existence but dynamic becoming, happening, and coming to pass. This verb is the etymological root of the divine name YHWH, making God's very name a statement about being and presence. Its first dramatic use is in God's self-revelation: "I AM WHO I AM" (*ehyeh asher ehyeh*, Exod 3:14).

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 3#v14|Exod 3:14]] — "I AM WHO I AM (*ehyeh asher ehyeh*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v3|Gen 1:3]] — "Let there be (*yehi*) light; and there was (*yehi*) light"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 2#v7|Gen 2:7]] — "Man became (*hayah*) a living being"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 3#v12|Exod 3:12]] — "I will be (*ehyeh*) with you"

## Theological Significance

The creation narrative deploys *hayah* in the formula *yehi… vayehi* ("let there be… and there was"), revealing that existence itself is God's gift, called forth by divine speech. Everything that *is* exists because God willed it into being.

Exodus 3:14 transforms the verb into a divine name: God is the one who *is* — not in a Greek philosophical sense of changeless being, but in the Hebrew sense of dynamic, faithful presence. "I will be who I will be" promises ongoing presence: God will be there, actively engaged with His people. This verb thus bridges theology proper (who God is), creation theology (that things exist), and covenant theology (that God is present with His people).
