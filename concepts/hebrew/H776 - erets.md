# אֶרֶץ — Erets

One of the most frequent nouns in the OT, meaning "earth" (the planet), "land" (a territory, especially the Promised Land), or "ground" (the surface). The first verse of Scripture pairs it with [[concepts/hebrew/H8064 - shamayim|shamayim]] to form a merism — "the heavens and the earth" meaning "everything that exists."

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v1|Gen 1:1]] — "God created the heavens and the earth (*erets*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 12#v1|Gen 12:1]] — "Go… to the land (*erets*) which I will show you"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Exodus/Exodus 3#v8|Exod 3:8]] — "A good and spacious land (*erets*), a land flowing with milk and honey"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 65#v17|Isa 65:17]] — "I create new heavens and a new earth (*erets*)"

## Theological Significance

The word *erets* carries two interlocking theological registers. As "earth" it denotes God's created order — the domain given to humanity for stewardship and [[concepts/hebrew/H7287 - radah|dominion]]. As "land" (*ha'arets*) it denotes the specific territory promised to Abraham's descendants, making it a covenant word tied to Israel's identity and hope.

The promise of land is not merely real estate but a sign of God's faithfulness and a type of eschatological rest. The prophetic vision of a "new earth" (Isa 65:17; Rev 21:1) transforms the land promise into a universal hope — the entire *erets* renewed as God's dwelling place with humanity.
