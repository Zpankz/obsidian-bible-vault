# חֹשֶׁךְ — Choshek

Darkness — the primordial condition before God spoke light into being. *Choshek* represents the unordered, the threatening, and the unknown. While not inherently evil in Genesis 1 (God gives it the name "night"), it becomes a metaphor for judgment, death, sin, and the absence of God's revealed presence.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v2|Gen 1:2]] — "Darkness (*choshek*) was over the surface of the deep"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Genesis/Genesis 1#v4|Gen 1:4]] — "God separated the light from the darkness (*choshek*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 10#v22|Exod 10:22]] — "There was thick darkness (*choshek*) in all the land of Egypt"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 9#v2|Isa 9:2]] — "The people who walk in darkness (*choshek*) have seen a great light"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 45#v7|Isa 45:7]] — "I form light and create darkness (*choshek*)"

## Theological Significance

In Genesis 1:2, *choshek* is the pre-creation condition — not a rival power but an absence that yields to God's word. God does not destroy darkness but separates it from light and assigns it a bounded role (night). This establishes God's sovereignty over both realms, a point Isaiah 45:7 makes explicit: God creates both light and darkness.

As a metaphor, *choshek* represents everything opposed to God's revealed order: Egypt's plague of darkness is a judgment that reverses creation, and the prophets describe exile and judgment as walking in darkness. The light/darkness motif runs straight into John's Gospel (1:5; 3:19) and Pauline theology (2 Cor 4:6), where the gospel is a new creation act: light shining in darkness.
