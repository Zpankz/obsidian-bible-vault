# אֱמֶת — Emeth

Truth in the Hebrew sense — not abstract philosophical correspondence but concrete reliability, faithfulness, and trustworthiness. From the root *aman* ("to be firm"), *emeth* describes what is stable, dependable, and proven. Often paired with [[concepts/hebrew/H2617 - chesed|chesed]] as the twin pillars of God's covenant character.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Exodus/Exodus 34#v6|Exod 34:6]] — "Abounding in lovingkindness and truth (*emeth*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 25#v5|Ps 25:5]] — "Lead me in Your truth (*emeth*) and teach me"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Psalms/Psalms 119#v160|Ps 119:160]] — "The sum of Your word is truth (*emeth*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-IL/Isaiah/Isaiah 65#v16|Isa 65:16]] — "The God of truth (*emeth*)" (lit. "the God of Amen")

## Theological Significance

The pairing of *chesed* and *emeth* (lovingkindness and truth/faithfulness) in Exodus 34:6 constitutes God's self-revelation of His covenant character — the most-quoted creedal formula in the OT. God's *emeth* is not merely that He speaks facts but that He keeps promises, that His word is reliable across generations.

When Jesus declares "I am the way, the truth (*aletheia*), and the life" (John 14:6), the underlying concept is deeply Hebraic: He is the ultimate reliability, the one in whom God's promises find their "Amen" (2 Cor 1:20). The LXX rendering [[concepts/greek/G225 - aletheia|aletheia]] carries both the Greek sense (reality/disclosure) and the Hebrew sense (faithfulness/firmness).
