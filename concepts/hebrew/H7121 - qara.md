# קָרָא — Qara

To call, name, proclaim, summon, read aloud — a verb of speech with authority. In Genesis 1, God *names* the elements of creation by calling them (*qara*), exercising sovereign authority through speech. To name is to define, to assign identity and purpose. *Qara* also means "to call upon" God in worship and prayer.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 1#v5|Gen 1:5]] — "God called (*qara*) the light day"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 2#v19|Gen 2:19]] — "Whatever the man called (*qara*) a living creature, that was its name"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 4#v26|Gen 4:26]] — "Men began to call (*qara*) upon the name of the LORD"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 43#v1|Isa 43:1]] — "I have called (*qara*) you by name; you are Mine"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 55#v6|Isa 55:6]] — "Call (*qara*) upon Him while He is near"

## Theological Significance

God's act of naming in Genesis 1 establishes the principle that the one who names has authority over what is named. When Adam names the animals (Gen 2:19), he exercises delegated authority. When God calls Israel by name (Isa 43:1), He claims ownership and relationship — naming is an act of covenant possession.

*Qara* also denotes the human response to God: "to call upon the name of the LORD" is the earliest form of worship (Gen 4:26). Joel 2:32, quoted by Paul in Romans 10:13, promises that "whoever calls on the name of the LORD will be saved" — the verb of divine authority becomes the verb of human trust, and the Creator who names becomes the Savior who answers.
