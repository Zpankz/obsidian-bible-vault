# אֲדֹנָי — Adonai

The reverential title for God meaning "my Lord," used as a surrogate pronunciation for the divine name YHWH. The form is a first-person possessive plural of *adon* ("lord/master"), intensified to express absolute sovereignty.

## Key Passages

- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Genesis/Genesis 15#v2|Gen 15:2]] — "O Lord (*Adonai*) GOD, what will You give me?"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Isaiah/Isaiah 6#v1|Isa 6:1]] — "I saw the Lord (*Adonai*) sitting on a throne"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Psalms/Psalms 110#v1|Ps 110:1]] — "The LORD says to my Lord (*Adonai*)"
- [[bibles/NASB-Interlinear/Ezekiel/Ezekiel 37#v3|Ezek 37:3]] — "O Lord (*Adonai*) GOD, You know"

## Theological Significance

Adonai expresses the relational sovereignty of God — He is not merely a cosmic power but *my* Lord, the one to whom the speaker owes personal allegiance. When combined with YHWH (as *Adonai YHWH*), it creates the fullest expression of divine authority and covenant relationship.

Psalm 110:1 became the most-quoted OT verse in the NT, where Jesus applies it to himself (Matt 22:44), identifying the messianic "Lord" (*Adonai*) as distinct from and yet seated beside YHWH — a text that shaped early Christological confession of Jesus as [[concepts/greek/G2962 - kyrios|Kyrios]].
