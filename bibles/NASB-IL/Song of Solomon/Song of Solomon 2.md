## Song of Solomon 2:3-7

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Like an apple tree among the trees of the forest, So is my beloved among the young men. In his shade I took great delight and sat down, And his fruit was sweet to my taste.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'He has brought me to [his] banquet hall, And his banner over me is love.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Sustain me with raisin cakes, Refresh me with apples, Because I am lovesick.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Let his left hand be under my head And his right hand embrace me.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'I adjure you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the gazelles or by the hinds of the field, That you do not arouse or awaken [my] love Until she pleases.'

## Song of Solomon 2:8-13

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Listen! My beloved! Behold, he is coming, Climbing on the mountains, Leaping on the hills!

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'My beloved is like a gazelle or a young stag. Behold, he is standing behind our wall, He is looking through the windows, He is peering through the lattice.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'My beloved responded and said to me, 'Arise, my darling, my beautiful one, And come along.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'For behold, the winter is past, The rain is over [and] gone.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'The flowers have [already] appeared in the land; The time has arrived for pruning [the vines], And the voice of the turtledove has been heard in our land.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'The fig tree has ripened its figs, And the vines in blossom have given forth [their] fragrance. Arise, my darling, my beautiful one, And come along!''

## Song of Solomon 2:14-17

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'O my dove, in the clefts of the rock, In the secret place of the steep pathway, Let me see your form, Let me hear your voice; For your voice is sweet, And your form is lovely.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Catch the foxes for us, The little foxes that are ruining the vineyards, While our vineyards are in blossom.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'My beloved is mine, and I am his; He pastures [his flock] among the lilies.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Until the cool of the day when the shadows flee away, Turn, my beloved, and be like a gazelle Or a young stag on the mountains of Bether.'
