## Song of Solomon 5:2-8

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'I was asleep but my heart was awake. A voice! My beloved was knocking: 'Open to me, my sister, my darling, My dove, my perfect one! For my head is drenched with dew, My locks with the damp of the night.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'I have taken off my dress, How can I put it on [again]? I have washed my feet, How can I dirty them [again]?

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'My beloved extended his hand through the opening, And my feelings were aroused for him.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'I arose to open to my beloved; And my hands dripped with myrrh, And my fingers with liquid myrrh, On the handles of the bolt.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'I opened to my beloved, But my beloved had turned away [and] had gone! My heart went out [to him] as he spoke. I searched for him but I did not find him; I called him but he did not answer me.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'The watchmen who make the rounds in the city found me, They struck me [and] wounded me; The guardsmen of the walls took away my shawl from me.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'I adjure you, O daughters of Jerusalem, If you find my beloved, As to what you will tell him: For I am lovesick.'

## Song of Solomon 5:9-16

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'What kind of beloved is your beloved, O most beautiful among women? What kind of beloved is your beloved, That thus you adjure us?'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'My beloved is dazzling and ruddy, Outstanding among ten thousand.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'His head is [like] gold, pure gold; His locks are [like] clusters of dates [And] black as a raven.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'His eyes are like doves Beside streams of water, Bathed in milk, [And] reposed in [their] setting.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'His cheeks are like a bed of balsam, Banks of sweet-scented herbs; His lips are lilies Dripping with liquid myrrh.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'His hands are rods of gold Set with beryl; His abdomen is carved ivory Inlaid with sapphires.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'His legs are pillars of alabaster Set on pedestals of pure gold; His appearance is like Lebanon Choice as the cedars.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'His mouth is [full of] sweetness. And he is wholly desirable. This is my beloved and this is my friend, O daughters of Jerusalem.'
