###### v3

## 1 Peter 2:4-12

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> And coming to Him as to a living stone which has been rejected by men, but is choice and precious in the sight of God,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> you also, as living stones, are being built up as a spiritual house for a holy priesthood, to offer up spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God through Jesus Christ.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> For [this] is contained in Scripture: 'BEHOLD, I LAY IN ZION A CHOICE STONE, A PRECIOUS CORNER [stone], AND HE WHO BELIEVES IN HIM WILL NOT BE DISAPPOINTED.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> This precious value, then, is for you who believe; but for those who disbelieve, 'THE STONE WHICH THE BUILDERS REJECTED, THIS BECAME THE VERY CORNER [stone],'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> and, 'A STONE OF STUMBLING AND A ROCK OF OFFENSE'; for they stumble because they are disobedient to the word, and to this [doom] they were also appointed.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> But you are A CHOSEN RACE, A royal PRIESTHOOD, A HOLY NATION, A PEOPLE FOR [God's] OWN POSSESSION, so that you may proclaim the excellencies of Him who has called you out of darkness into His marvelous light;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> for you once were NOT A PEOPLE, but now you are THE PEOPLE OF GOD; you had NOT RECEIVED MERCY, but now you have RECEIVED MERCY.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Beloved, I urge you as aliens and strangers to abstain from fleshly lusts which wage war against the soul.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Keep your behavior excellent among the Gentiles, so that in the thing in which they slander you as evildoers, they may because of your good deeds, as they observe [them], glorify God in the day of visitation.

## 1 Peter 2:13-25

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Submit yourselves for the Lord's sake to every human institution, whether to a king as the one in authority,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> or to governors as sent by him for the punishment of evildoers and the praise of those who do right.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> For such is the will of God that by doing right you may silence the ignorance of foolish men.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> [Act] as free men, and do not use your freedom as a covering for evil, but [use it] as bondslaves of God.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Honor all people, love the brotherhood, fear God, honor the king.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> Servants, be submissive to your masters with all respect, not only to those who are good and gentle, but also to those who are unreasonable.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> For this [finds] favor, if for the sake of conscience toward God a person bears up under sorrows when suffering unjustly.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> For what credit is there if, when you sin and are harshly treated, you endure it with patience? But if when you do what is right and suffer [for it] you patiently endure it, this [finds] favor with God.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> For you have been called for this purpose, since Christ also suffered for you, leaving you an example for you to follow in His steps,

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> WHO COMMITTED NO SIN, NOR WAS ANY DECEIT FOUND IN HIS MOUTH;

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> and while being reviled, He did not revile in return; while suffering, He uttered no threats, but kept entrusting [Himself] to Him who judges righteously;

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> and He Himself bore our sins in His body on the cross, so that we might die to sin and live to righteousness; for by His wounds you were healed.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> For you were continually straying like sheep, but now you have returned to the Shepherd and Guardian of your souls.
