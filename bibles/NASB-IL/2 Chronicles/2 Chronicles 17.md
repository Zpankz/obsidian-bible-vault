# 2 Chronicles 17

## 2 Chronicles 17:1-9

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Jehoshaphat his son then became king in his place, and made his position over Israel firm.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> He placed troops in all the fortified cities of Judah, and set garrisons in the land of Judah and in the cities of Ephraim which Asa his father had captured.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> The LORD was with Jehoshaphat because he followed the example of his father David's earlier days and did not seek the Baals,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> but sought the God of his father, followed His commandments, and did not act as Israel did.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> So the LORD established the kingdom in his control, and all Judah brought tribute to Jehoshaphat, and he had great riches and honor.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> He took great pride in the ways of the LORD and again removed the high places and the Asherim from Judah.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Then in the third year of his reign he sent his officials, Ben-hail, Obadiah, Zechariah, Nethanel and Micaiah, to teach in the cities of Judah;

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> and with them the Levites, Shemaiah, Nethaniah, Zebadiah, Asahel, Shemiramoth, Jehonathan, Adonijah, Tobijah and Tobadonijah, the Levites; and with them Elishama and Jehoram, the priests.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> They taught in Judah, [having] the book of the law of the LORD with them; and they went throughout all the cities of Judah and taught among the people.

## 2 Chronicles 17:10-19

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Now the dread of the LORD was on all the kingdoms of the lands which [were] around Judah, so that they did not make war against Jehoshaphat.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Some of the Philistines brought gifts and silver as tribute to Jehoshaphat; the Arabians also brought him flocks, 7,700 rams and 7,700 male goats.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> So Jehoshaphat grew greater and greater, and he built fortresses and store cities in Judah.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> He had large supplies in the cities of Judah, and warriors, valiant men, in Jerusalem.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> This was their muster according to their fathers' households: of Judah, commanders of thousands, Adnah [was] the commander, and with him 300,000 valiant warriors;

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> and next to him [was] Johanan the commander, and with him 280,000;

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> and next to him Amasiah the son of Zichri, who volunteered for the LORD, and with him 200,000 valiant warriors;

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> and of Benjamin, Eliada a valiant warrior, and with him 200,000 armed with bow and shield;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> and next to him Jehozabad, and with him 180,000 equipped for war.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> These are they who served the king, apart from those whom the king put in the fortified cities through all Judah.
