# Hebrews 4

## Hebrews 4:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Therefore, let us fear if, while a promise remains of entering His rest, any one of you may seem to have come short of it.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For indeed we have had good news preached to us, just as they also; but the word they heard did not profit them, because it was not united by faith in those who heard.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> For we who have believed enter that rest, just as He has said, 'AS I SWORE IN MY WRATH, THEY SHALL NOT ENTER MY REST,' although His works were finished from the foundation of the world.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> For He has said somewhere concerning the seventh [day]: 'AND GOD RESTED ON THE SEVENTH DAY FROM ALL HIS WORKS';

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> and again in this [passage], 'THEY SHALL NOT ENTER MY REST.'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Therefore, since it remains for some to enter it, and those who formerly had good news preached to them failed to enter because of disobedience,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> He again fixes a certain day, 'Today,' saying through David after so long a time just as has been said before, 'TODAY IF YOU HEAR HIS VOICE, DO NOT HARDEN YOUR HEARTS.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> For if Joshua had given them rest, He would not have spoken of another day after that.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> So there remains a Sabbath rest for the people of God.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For the one who has entered His rest has himself also rested from his works, as God did from His.

## Hebrews 4:11-16

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Therefore let us be diligent to enter that rest, so that no one will fall, through [following] the same example of disobedience.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> For the word of God is living and active and sharper than any two-edged sword, and piercing as far as the division of soul and spirit, of both joints and marrow, and able to judge the thoughts and intentions of the heart.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> And there is no creature hidden from His sight, but all things are open and laid bare to the eyes of Him with whom we have to do.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Therefore, since we have a great high priest who has passed through the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our confession.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> For we do not have a high priest who cannot sympathize with our weaknesses, but One who has been tempted in all things as [we are, yet] without sin.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Therefore let us draw near with confidence to the throne of grace, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need.
