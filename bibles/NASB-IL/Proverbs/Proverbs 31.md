# Proverbs 31

## Proverbs 31:1-9

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> The words of King Lemuel, the oracle which his mother taught him:

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> What, O my son? And what, O son of my womb? And what, O son of my vows?

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Do not give your strength to women, Or your ways to that which destroys kings.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> It is not for kings, O Lemuel, It is not for kings to drink wine, Or for rulers to desire strong drink,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> For they will drink and forget what is decreed, And pervert the rights of all the afflicted.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Give strong drink to him who is perishing, And wine to him whose life is bitter.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Let him drink and forget his poverty And remember his trouble no more.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Open your mouth for the mute, For the rights of all the unfortunate.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Open your mouth, judge righteously, And defend the rights of the afflicted and needy.

## Proverbs 31:10-31

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> An excellent wife, who can find? For her worth is far above jewels.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> The heart of her husband trusts in her, And he will have no lack of gain.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> She does him good and not evil All the days of her life.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> She looks for wool and flax And works with her hands in delight.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> She is like merchant ships; She brings her food from afar.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> She rises also while it is still night And gives food to her household And portions to her maidens.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> She considers a field and buys it; From her earnings she plants a vineyard.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> She girds herself with strength And makes her arms strong.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> She senses that her gain is good; Her lamp does not go out at night.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> She stretches out her hands to the distaff, And her hands grasp the spindle.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> She extends her hand to the poor, And she stretches out her hands to the needy.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> She is not afraid of the snow for her household, For all her household are clothed with scarlet.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> She makes coverings for herself; Her clothing is fine linen and purple.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> Her husband is known in the gates, When he sits among the elders of the land.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> She makes linen garments and sells [them], And supplies belts to the tradesmen.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> Strength and dignity are her clothing, And she smiles at the future.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> She opens her mouth in wisdom, And the teaching of kindness is on her tongue.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> She looks well to the ways of her household, And does not eat the bread of idleness.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> Her children rise up and bless her; Her husband [also], and he praises her, [saying]:

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'Many daughters have done nobly, But you excel them all.'

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> Charm is deceitful and beauty is vain, [But] a woman who fears the LORD, she shall be praised.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> Give her the product of her hands, And let her works praise her in the gates.
