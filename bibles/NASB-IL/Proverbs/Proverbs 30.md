## Proverbs 30:1-6

<sup>1</sup> The words of Agur the son of Jakeh, the oracle. The man declares to Ithiel, to Ithiel and Ucal:

<sup>2</sup> Surely I am more stupid than any man, And I do not have the understanding of a man.

<sup>3</sup> Neither have I learned wisdom, Nor do I have the knowledge of the Holy One.

<sup>4</sup> Who has ascended into heaven and descended? Who has gathered the wind in His fists? Who has wrapped the waters in His garment? Who has established all the ends of the earth? What is His name or His son's name? Surely you know![^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7]

<sup>5</sup> Every word of God is tested; He is a shield to those who take refuge in Him.[^8][^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15]

<sup>6</sup> Do not add to His words Or He will reprove you, and you will be proved a liar.[^16][^17][^18][^19]

## Proverbs 30:18-23

<sup>18</sup> There are three things which are too wonderful for me, Four which I do not understand:

<sup>19</sup> The way of an eagle in the sky, The way of a serpent on a rock, The way of a ship in the middle of the sea, And the way of a man with a maid.

<sup>20</sup> This is the way of an adulterous woman: She eats and wipes her mouth, And says, 'I have done no wrong.'

<sup>21</sup> Under three things the earth quakes, And under four, it cannot bear up:

<sup>22</sup> Under a slave when he becomes king, And a fool when he is satisfied with food,

<sup>23</sup> Under an unloved woman when she gets a husband, And a maidservant when she supplants her mistress.

## Proverbs 30:24-28

<sup>24</sup> Four things are small on the earth, But they are exceedingly wise:

<sup>25</sup> The ants are not a strong people, But they prepare their food in the summer;[^36]

<sup>26</sup> The shephanim are not mighty people, Yet they make their houses in the rocks;

<sup>27</sup> The locusts have no king, Yet all of them go out in ranks;

<sup>28</sup> The lizard you may grasp with the hands, Yet it is in kings' palaces.

## Proverbs 30:29-33

<sup>29</sup> There are three things which are stately in [their] march, Even four which are stately when they walk:

<sup>30</sup> The lion [which] is mighty among beasts And does not retreat before any,

<sup>31</sup> The strutting rooster, the male goat also, And a king [when his] army is with him.

<sup>32</sup> If you have been foolish in exalting yourself Or if you have plotted [evil], [put your] hand on your mouth.

<sup>33</sup> For the churning of milk produces butter, And pressing the nose brings forth blood; So the churning of anger produces strife.
