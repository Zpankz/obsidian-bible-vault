## Proverbs 9:1-12

<sup>1</sup> Wisdom has built her house, She has hewn out her seven pillars;

<sup>2</sup> She has prepared her food, she has mixed her wine; She has also set her table;

<sup>3</sup> She has sent out her maidens, she calls From the tops of the heights of the city:

<sup>4</sup> 'Whoever is naive, let him turn in here!' To him who lacks understanding she says,

<sup>5</sup> 'Come, eat of my food And drink of the wine I have mixed.

<sup>6</sup> 'Forsake [your] folly and live, And proceed in the way of understanding.'[^1]

<sup>7</sup> He who corrects a scoffer gets dishonor for himself, And he who reproves a wicked man [gets] insults for himself.[^2]

<sup>8</sup> Do not reprove a scoffer, or he will hate you, Reprove a wise man and he will love you.[^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

<sup>9</sup> Give [instruction] to a wise man and he will be still wiser, Teach a righteous man and he will increase [his] learning.[^9]

<sup>10</sup> The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom, And the knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.[^10][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15][^16][^17]

<sup>11</sup> For by me your days will be multiplied, And years of life will be added to you.[^18][^19][^20][^21]

<sup>12</sup> If you are wise, you are wise for yourself, And if you scoff, you alone will bear it.

## Proverbs 9:13-18

<sup>13</sup> The woman of folly is boisterous, [She is] naive and knows nothing.[^22]

<sup>14</sup> She sits at the doorway of her house, On a seat by the high places of the city,

<sup>15</sup> Calling to those who pass by, Who are making their paths straight:

<sup>16</sup> 'Whoever is naive, let him turn in here,' And to him who lacks understanding she says,

<sup>17</sup> 'Stolen water is sweet; And bread [eaten] in secret is pleasant.'[^23]

<sup>18</sup> But he does not know that the dead are there, [That] her guests are in the depths of Sheol.
