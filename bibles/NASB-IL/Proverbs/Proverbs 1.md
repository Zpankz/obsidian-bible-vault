# Proverbs 1

## Proverbs 1:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> The proverbs of Solomon the son of David, king of Israel:

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> To know wisdom and instruction, To discern the sayings of understanding,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> To receive instruction in wise behavior, Righteousness, justice and equity;

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> To give prudence to the naive, To the youth knowledge and discretion,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> A wise man will hear and increase in learning, And a man of understanding will acquire wise counsel,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> To understand a proverb and a figure, The words of the wise and their riddles.

## Proverbs 1:10-19

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> My son, if sinners entice you, Do not consent.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> If they say, 'Come with us, Let us lie in wait for blood, Let us ambush the innocent without cause;

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Let us swallow them alive like Sheol, Even whole, as those who go down to the pit;

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> We will find all [kinds] of precious wealth, We will fill our houses with spoil;

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Throw in your lot with us, We shall all have one purse,'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> My son, do not walk in the way with them. Keep your feet from their path,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> For their feet run to evil And they hasten to shed blood.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Indeed, it is useless to spread the [baited] net In the sight of any bird;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> But they lie in wait for their own blood; They ambush their own lives.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> So are the ways of everyone who gains by violence; It takes away the life of its possessors.

## Proverbs 1:20-33

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Wisdom shouts in the street, She lifts her voice in the square;

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> At the head of the noisy [streets] she cries out; At the entrance of the gates in the city she utters her sayings:

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'How long, O naive ones, will you love being simple-minded? And scoffers delight themselves in scoffing And fools hate knowledge?

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'Turn to my reproof, Behold, I will pour out my spirit on you; I will make my words known to you.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Because I called and you refused, I stretched out my hand and no one paid attention;

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> And you neglected all my counsel And did not want my reproof;

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> I will also laugh at your calamity; I will mock when your dread comes,

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> When your dread comes like a storm And your calamity comes like a whirlwind, When distress and anguish come upon you.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'Then they will call on me, but I will not answer; They will seek me diligently but they will not find me,

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> Because they hated knowledge And did not choose the fear of the LORD.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'They would not accept my counsel, They spurned all my reproof.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'So they shall eat of the fruit of their own way And be satiated with their own devices.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'For the waywardness of the naive will kill them, And the complacency of fools will destroy them.

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> 'But he who listens to me shall live securely And will be at ease from the dread of evil.'
