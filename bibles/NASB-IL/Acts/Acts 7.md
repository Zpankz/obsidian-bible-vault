# Acts 7

## Acts 7:1-16

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> The high priest said, 'Are these things so?'

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And he said, 'Hear me, brethren and fathers! The God of glory appeared to our father Abraham when he was in Mesopotamia, before he lived in Haran,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> and said to him, 'LEAVE YOUR COUNTRY AND YOUR RELATIVES, AND COME INTO THE LAND THAT I WILL SHOW YOU.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Then he left the land of the Chaldeans and settled in Haran. From there, after his father died, [God] had him move to this country in which you are now living.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'But He gave him no inheritance in it, not even a foot of ground, and [yet], even when he had no child, He promised that HE WOULD GIVE IT TO HIM AS A POSSESSION, AND TO HIS DESCENDANTS AFTER HIM.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'But God spoke to this effect, that his DESCENDANTS WOULD BE ALIENS IN A FOREIGN LAND, AND THAT THEY WOULD BE ENSLAVED AND MISTREATED FOR FOUR HUNDRED YEARS.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> ''AND WHATEVER NATION TO WHICH THEY WILL BE IN BONDAGE I MYSELF WILL JUDGE,' said God, 'AND AFTER THAT THEY WILL COME OUT AND SERVE ME IN THIS PLACE.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'And He gave him the covenant of circumcision; and so [Abraham] became the father of Isaac, and circumcised him on the eighth day; and Isaac [became the father of] Jacob, and Jacob [of] the twelve patriarchs.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'The patriarchs became jealous of Joseph and sold him into Egypt. [Yet] God was with him,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> and rescued him from all his afflictions, and granted him favor and wisdom in the sight of Pharaoh, king of Egypt, and he made him governor over Egypt and all his household.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Now a famine came over all Egypt and Canaan, and great affliction [with it], and our fathers could find no food.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'But when Jacob heard that there was grain in Egypt, he sent our fathers [there] the first time.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'On the second [visit] Joseph made himself known to his brothers, and Joseph's family was disclosed to Pharaoh.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Then Joseph sent [word] and invited Jacob his father and all his relatives to come to him, seventy-five persons [in all].

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'And Jacob went down to Egypt and [there] he and our fathers died.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> '[From there] they were removed to Shechem and laid in the tomb which Abraham had purchased for a sum of money from the sons of Hamor in Shechem.

## Acts 7:17-29

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'But as the time of the promise was approaching which God had assured to Abraham, the people increased and multiplied in Egypt,

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> until THERE AROSE ANOTHER KING OVER EGYPT WHO KNEW NOTHING ABOUT JOSEPH.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'It was he who took shrewd advantage of our race and mistreated our fathers so that they would expose their infants and they would not survive.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'It was at this time that Moses was born; and he was lovely in the sight of God, and he was nurtured three months in his father's home.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'And after he had been set outside, Pharaoh's daughter took him away and nurtured him as her own son.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'Moses was educated in all the learning of the Egyptians, and he was a man of power in words and deeds.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'But when he was approaching the age of forty, it entered his mind to visit his brethren, the sons of Israel.

###### v24

## Acts 7:30-41

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'After forty years had passed, AN ANGEL APPEARED TO HIM IN THE WILDERNESS OF MOUNT Sinai, IN THE FLAME OF A BURNING THORN BUSH.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'When Moses saw it, he marveled at the sight; and as he approached to look [more] closely, there came the voice of the Lord:

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'I AM THE GOD OF YOUR FATHERS, THE GOD OF ABRAHAM AND ISAAC AND JACOB.' Moses shook with fear and would not venture to look.

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> 'BUT THE LORD SAID TO HIM, 'TAKE OFF THE SANDALS FROM YOUR FEET, FOR THE PLACE ON WHICH YOU ARE STANDING IS HOLY GROUND.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> 'I HAVE CERTAINLY SEEN THE OPPRESSION OF MY PEOPLE IN EGYPT AND HAVE HEARD THEIR GROANS, AND I HAVE COME DOWN TO RESCUE THEM; COME NOW, AND I WILL SEND YOU TO EGYPT.'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> 'This Moses whom they disowned, saying, 'WHO MADE YOU A RULER AND A JUDGE?' is the one whom God sent [to be] both a ruler and a deliverer with the help of the angel who appeared to him in the thorn bush.

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> 'This man led them out, performing wonders and signs in the land of Egypt and in the Red Sea and in the wilderness for forty years.

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> 'This is the Moses who said to the sons of Israel, 'GOD WILL RAISE UP FOR YOU A PROPHET LIKE ME FROM YOUR BRETHREN.'

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> 'This is the one who was in the congregation in the wilderness together with the angel who was speaking to him on Mount Sinai, and [who was] with our fathers; and he received living oracles to pass on to you.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> 'Our fathers were unwilling to be obedient to him, but repudiated him and in their hearts turned back to Egypt,

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> SAYING TO AARON, 'MAKE FOR US GODS WHO WILL GO BEFORE US; FOR THIS MOSES WHO LED US OUT OF THE LAND OF EGYPT-- WE DO NOT KNOW WHAT HAPPENED TO HIM.'

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> 'At that time they made a calf and brought a sacrifice to the idol, and were rejoicing in the works of their hands.
