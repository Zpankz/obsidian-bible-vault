# Acts 20

## Acts 20:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> After the uproar had ceased, Paul sent for the disciples, and when he had exhorted them and taken his leave of them, he left to go to Macedonia.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> When he had gone through those districts and had given them much exhortation, he came to Greece.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And [there] he spent three months, and when a plot was formed against him by the Jews as he was about to set sail for Syria, he decided to return through Macedonia.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> And he was accompanied by Sopater of Berea, [the son] of Pyrrhus, and by Aristarchus and Secundus of the Thessalonians, and Gaius of Derbe, and Timothy, and Tychicus and Trophimus of Asia.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> But these had gone on ahead and were waiting for us at Troas.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> We sailed from Philippi after the days of Unleavened Bread, and came to them at Troas within five days; and there we stayed seven days.

## Acts 20:7-12

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> On the first day of the week, when we were gathered together to break bread, Paul [began] talking to them, intending to leave the next day, and he prolonged his message until midnight.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> There were many lamps in the upper room where we were gathered together.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> And there was a young man named Eutychus sitting on the window sill, sinking into a deep sleep; and as Paul kept on talking, he was overcome by sleep and fell down from the third floor and was picked up dead.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> But Paul went down and fell upon him, and after embracing him, he said, 'Do not be troubled, for his life is in him.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> When he had gone [back] up and had broken the bread and eaten, he talked with them a long while until daybreak, and then left.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> They took away the boy alive, and were greatly comforted.

<sup>15</sup> Sailing from there, we arrived the following day opposite Chios; and the next day we crossed over to Samos; and the day following we came to Miletus.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> For Paul had decided to sail past Ephesus so that he would not have to spend time in Asia; for he was hurrying to be in Jerusalem, if possible, on the day of Pentecost.

## Acts 20:17-35

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> From Miletus he sent to Ephesus and called to him the elders of the church.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> And when they had come to him, he said to them, 'You yourselves know, from the first day that I set foot in Asia, how I was with you the whole time,

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> serving the Lord with all humility and with tears and with trials which came upon me through the plots of the Jews;

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> how I did not shrink from declaring to you anything that was profitable, and teaching you publicly and from house to house,

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> solemnly testifying to both Jews and Greeks of repentance toward God and faith in our Lord Jesus Christ.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'And now, behold, bound in spirit, I am on my way to Jerusalem, not knowing what will happen to me there,

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> except that the Holy Spirit solemnly testifies to me in every city, saying that bonds and afflictions await me.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'But I do not consider my life of any account as dear to myself, so that I may finish my course and the ministry which I received from the Lord Jesus, to testify solemnly of the gospel of the grace of God.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'And now, behold, I know that all of you, among whom I went about preaching the kingdom, will no longer see my face.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'Therefore, I testify to you this day that I am innocent of the blood of all men.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'For I did not shrink from declaring to you the whole purpose of God.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'Be on guard for yourselves and for all the flock, among which the Holy Spirit has made you overseers, to shepherd the church of God which He purchased with His own blood.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'I know that after my departure savage wolves will come in among you, not sparing the flock;

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> and from among your own selves men will arise, speaking perverse things, to draw away the disciples after them.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'Therefore be on the alert, remembering that night and day for a period of three years I did not cease to admonish each one with tears.
