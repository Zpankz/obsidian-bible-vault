# Acts 24

## Acts 24:1-9

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> After five days the high priest Ananias came down with some elders, with an attorney [named] Tertullus, and they brought charges to the governor against Paul.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> After [Paul] had been summoned, Tertullus began to accuse him, saying [to the governor], 'Since we have through you attained much peace, and since by your providence reforms are being carried out for this nation,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> we acknowledge [this] in every way and everywhere, most excellent Felix, with all thankfulness.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'But, that I may not weary you any further, I beg you to grant us, by your kindness, a brief hearing.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'For we have found this man a real pest and a fellow who stirs up dissension among all the Jews throughout the world, and a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'And he even tried to desecrate the temple; and then we arrested him. [We wanted to judge him according to our own Law.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'But Lysias the commander came along, and with much violence took him out of our hands,

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> ordering his accusers to come before you]. By examining him yourself concerning all these matters you will be able to ascertain the things of which we accuse him.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> The Jews also joined in the attack, asserting that these things were so.

## Acts 24:10-21

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> When the governor had nodded for him to speak, Paul responded: 'Knowing that for many years you have been a judge to this nation, I cheerfully make my defense,

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> since you can take note of the fact that no more than twelve days ago I went up to Jerusalem to worship.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Neither in the temple, nor in the synagogues, nor in the city [itself] did they find me carrying on a discussion with anyone or causing a riot.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Nor can they prove to you [the charges] of which they now accuse me.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'But this I admit to you, that according to the Way which they call a sect I do serve the God of our fathers, believing everything that is in accordance with the Law and that is written in the Prophets;

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> having a hope in God, which these men cherish themselves, that there shall certainly be a resurrection of both the righteous and the wicked.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'In view of this, I also do my best to maintain always a blameless conscience [both] before God and before men.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Now after several years I came to bring alms to my nation and to present offerings;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> in which they found me [occupied] in the temple, having been purified, without [any] crowd or uproar. But [there were] some Jews from Asia--

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> who ought to have been present before you and to make accusation, if they should have anything against me.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Or else let these men themselves tell what misdeed they found when I stood before the Council,

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> other than for this one statement which I shouted out while standing among them, 'For the resurrection of the dead I am on trial before you today.''

## Acts 24:22-27

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But Felix, having a more exact knowledge about the Way, put them off, saying, 'When Lysias the commander comes down, I will decide your case.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> Then he gave orders to the centurion for him to be kept in custody and [yet] have [some] freedom, and not to prevent any of his friends from ministering to him.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> But some days later Felix arrived with Drusilla, his wife who was a Jewess, and sent for Paul and heard him [speak] about faith in Christ Jesus.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> But as he was discussing righteousness, self-control and the judgment to come, Felix became frightened and said, 'Go away for the present, and when I find time I will summon you.'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> At the same time too, he was hoping that money would be given him by Paul; therefore he also used to send for him quite often and converse with him.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> But after two years had passed, Felix was succeeded by Porcius Festus, and wishing to do the Jews a favor, Felix left Paul imprisoned.
