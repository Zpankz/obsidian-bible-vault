# James 2

## James 2:1-7

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> My brethren, do not hold your faith in our glorious Lord Jesus Christ with [an attitude of] personal favoritism.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For if a man comes into your assembly with a gold ring and dressed in fine clothes, and there also comes in a poor man in dirty clothes,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> and you pay special attention to the one who is wearing the fine clothes, and say, 'You sit here in a good place,' and you say to the poor man, 'You stand over there, or sit down by my footstool,'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> have you not made distinctions among yourselves, and become judges with evil motives?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Listen, my beloved brethren: did not God choose the poor of this world [to be] rich in faith and heirs of the kingdom which He promised to those who love Him?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But you have dishonored the poor man. Is it not the rich who oppress you and personally drag you into court?

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Do they not blaspheme the fair name by which you have been called?

## James 2:8-13

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> If, however, you are fulfilling the royal law according to the Scripture, 'YOU SHALL LOVE YOUR NEIGHBOR AS YOURSELF,' you are doing well.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> But if you show partiality, you are committing sin [and] are convicted by the law as transgressors.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For whoever keeps the whole law and yet stumbles in one [point], he has become guilty of all.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For He who said, 'DO NOT COMMIT ADULTERY,' also said, 'DO NOT COMMIT MURDER.' Now if you do not commit adultery, but do commit murder, you have become a transgressor of the law.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> So speak and so act as those who are to be judged by [the] law of liberty.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> For judgment [will be] merciless to one who has shown no mercy; mercy triumphs over judgment.

## James 2:14-26

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> What use is it, my brethren, if someone says he has faith but he has no works? Can that faith save him?

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> If a brother or sister is without clothing and in need of daily food,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> and one of you says to them, 'Go in peace, be warmed and be filled,' and yet you do not give them what is necessary for [their] body, what use is that?

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Even so faith, if it has no works, is dead, [being] by itself.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> But someone may [well] say, 'You have faith and I have works; show me your faith without the works, and I will show you my faith by my works.'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> You believe that God is one. You do well; the demons also believe, and shudder.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> But are you willing to recognize, you foolish fellow, that faith without works is useless?

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> Was not Abraham our father justified by works when he offered up Isaac his son on the altar?

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> You see that faith was working with his works, and as a result of the works, faith was perfected;

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> and the Scripture was fulfilled which says, 'AND ABRAHAM BELIEVED GOD, AND IT WAS RECKONED TO HIM AS RIGHTEOUSNESS,' and he was called the friend of God.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> You see that a man is justified by works and not by faith alone.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> In the same way, was not Rahab the harlot also justified by works when she received the messengers and sent them out by another way?

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> For just as the body without [the] spirit is dead, so also faith without works is dead.
