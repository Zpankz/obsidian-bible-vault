## James 3:1-12

<sup>1</sup> Let not many [of you] become teachers, my brethren, knowing that as such we will incur a stricter judgment.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

<sup>2</sup> For we all stumble in many [ways]. If anyone does not stumble in what he says, he is a perfect man, able to bridle the whole body as well.[^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15][^16]

<sup>3</sup> Now if we put the bits into the horses' mouths so that they will obey us, we direct their entire body as well.[^17]

<sup>4</sup> Look at the ships also, though they are so great and are driven by strong winds, are still directed by a very small rudder wherever the inclination of the pilot desires.

<sup>5</sup> So also the tongue is a small part of the body, and [yet] it boasts of great things. See how great a forest is set aflame by such a small fire![^18][^19][^20][^21][^22][^23][^24][^25]

<sup>6</sup> And the tongue is a fire, the [very] world of iniquity; the tongue is set among our members as that which defiles the entire body, and sets on fire the course of [our] life, and is set on fire by hell.[^26][^27][^28][^29][^30][^31][^32][^33]

<sup>7</sup> For every species of beasts and birds, of reptiles and creatures of the sea, is tamed and has been tamed by the human race.

<sup>8</sup> But no one can tame the tongue; [it is] a restless evil [and] full of deadly poison.[^34][^35][^36][^37][^38][^39][^40][^41]

<sup>9</sup> With it we bless [our] Lord and Father, and with it we curse men, who have been made in the likeness of God;

<sup>10</sup> from the same mouth come [both] blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not to be this way.[^42][^43][^44][^45][^46]

<sup>11</sup> Does a fountain send out from the same opening [both] fresh and bitter [water]?

<sup>12</sup> Can a fig tree, my brethren, produce olives, or a vine produce figs? Nor [can] salt water produce fresh.[^47][^48][^49]

## James 3:13-18

<sup>13</sup> Who among you is wise and understanding? Let him show by his good behavior his deeds in the gentleness of wisdom.[^50][^51][^52][^53][^54][^55][^56][^57]

<sup>14</sup> But if you have bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your heart, do not be arrogant and [so] lie against the truth.[^58][^59][^60][^61]

<sup>15</sup> This wisdom is not that which comes down from above, but is earthly, natural, demonic.[^62][^63][^64]

<sup>16</sup> For where jealousy and selfish ambition exist, there is disorder and every evil thing.[^65][^66][^67][^68]

<sup>17</sup> But the wisdom from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, reasonable, full of mercy and good fruits, unwavering, without hypocrisy.[^69][^70][^71][^72][^73][^74][^75][^76]

<sup>18</sup> And the seed whose fruit is righteousness is sown in peace by those who make peace.[^77][^78][^79][^80][^81][^82][^83][^84]
