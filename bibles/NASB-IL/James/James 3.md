# James 3

## James 3:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Let not many [of you] become teachers, my brethren, knowing that as such we will incur a stricter judgment.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For we all stumble in many [ways]. If anyone does not stumble in what he says, he is a perfect man, able to bridle the whole body as well.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Now if we put the bits into the horses' mouths so that they will obey us, we direct their entire body as well.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Look at the ships also, though they are so great and are driven by strong winds, are still directed by a very small rudder wherever the inclination of the pilot desires.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> So also the tongue is a small part of the body, and [yet] it boasts of great things. See how great a forest is set aflame by such a small fire!

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> And the tongue is a fire, the [very] world of iniquity; the tongue is set among our members as that which defiles the entire body, and sets on fire the course of [our] life, and is set on fire by hell.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> For every species of beasts and birds, of reptiles and creatures of the sea, is tamed and has been tamed by the human race.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But no one can tame the tongue; [it is] a restless evil [and] full of deadly poison.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> With it we bless [our] Lord and Father, and with it we curse men, who have been made in the likeness of God;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> from the same mouth come [both] blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not to be this way.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Does a fountain send out from the same opening [both] fresh and bitter [water]?

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Can a fig tree, my brethren, produce olives, or a vine produce figs? Nor [can] salt water produce fresh.

## James 3:13-18

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Who among you is wise and understanding? Let him show by his good behavior his deeds in the gentleness of wisdom.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> But if you have bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your heart, do not be arrogant and [so] lie against the truth.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> This wisdom is not that which comes down from above, but is earthly, natural, demonic.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> For where jealousy and selfish ambition exist, there is disorder and every evil thing.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> But the wisdom from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, reasonable, full of mercy and good fruits, unwavering, without hypocrisy.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> And the seed whose fruit is righteousness is sown in peace by those who make peace.
