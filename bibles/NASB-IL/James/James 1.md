## James 1:2-12

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Consider it all joy, my brethren, when you encounter various trials,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> knowing that the testing of your faith produces endurance.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> And let endurance have [its] perfect result, so that you may be perfect and complete, lacking in nothing.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> But if any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives to all generously and without reproach, and it will be given to him.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But he must ask in faith without any doubting, for the one who doubts is like the surf of the sea, driven and tossed by the wind.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> For that man ought not to expect that he will receive anything from the Lord,

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> [being] a double-minded man, unstable in all his ways.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> But the brother of humble circumstances is to glory in his high position;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> and the rich man [is to glory] in his humiliation, because like flowering grass he will pass away.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For the sun rises with a scorching wind and withers the grass; and its flower falls off and the beauty of its appearance is destroyed; so too the rich man in the midst of his pursuits will fade away.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Blessed is a man who perseveres under trial; for once he has been approved, he will receive the crown of life which [the Lord] has promised to those who love Him.

## James 1:13-18

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Let no one say when he is tempted, 'I am being tempted by God'; for God cannot be tempted by evil, and He Himself does not tempt anyone.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> But each one is tempted when he is carried away and enticed by his own lust.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Then when lust has conceived, it gives birth to sin; and when sin is accomplished, it brings forth death.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Do not be deceived, my beloved brethren.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Every good thing given and every perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shifting shadow.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> In the exercise of His will He brought us forth by the word of truth, so that we would be a kind of first fruits among His creatures.

## James 1:19-27

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> [This] you know, my beloved brethren. But everyone must be quick to hear, slow to speak [and] slow to anger;

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> for the anger of man does not achieve the righteousness of God.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> Therefore, putting aside all filthiness and [all] that remains of wickedness, in humility receive the word implanted, which is able to save your souls.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But prove yourselves doers of the word, and not merely hearers who delude themselves.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> For if anyone is a hearer of the word and not a doer, he is like a man who looks at his natural face in a mirror;

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> for [once] he has looked at himself and gone away, he has immediately forgotten what kind of person he was.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> But one who looks intently at the perfect law, the [law] of liberty, and abides by it, not having become a forgetful hearer but an effectual doer, this man will be blessed in what he does.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> If anyone thinks himself to be religious, and yet does not bridle his tongue but deceives his [own] heart, this man's religion is worthless.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> Pure and undefiled religion in the sight of [our] God and Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their distress, [and] to keep oneself unstained by the world.
