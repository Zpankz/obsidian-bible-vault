# Zechariah 11

## Zechariah 11:1-3

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Open your doors, O Lebanon, That a fire may feed on your cedars.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Wail, O cypress, for the cedar has fallen, Because the glorious [trees] have been destroyed; Wail, O oaks of Bashan, For the impenetrable forest has come down.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> There is a sound of the shepherds' wail, For their glory is ruined; There is a sound of the young lions' roar, For the pride of the Jordan is ruined.

## Zechariah 11:4-14

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Thus says the LORD my God, 'Pasture the flock [doomed] to slaughter.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Those who buy them slay them and go unpunished, and [each of] those who sell them says, 'Blessed be the LORD, for I have become rich!' And their own shepherds have no pity on them.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'For I will no longer have pity on the inhabitants of the land,' declares the LORD; 'but behold, I will cause the men to fall, each into another's power and into the power of his king; and they will strike the land, and I will not deliver [them] from their power.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> So I pastured the flock [doomed] to slaughter, hence the afflicted of the flock. And I took for myself two staffs: the one I called Favor and the other I called Union; so I pastured the flock.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Then I annihilated the three shepherds in one month, for my soul was impatient with them, and their soul also was weary of me.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Then I said, 'I will not pasture you. What is to die, let it die, and what is to be annihilated, let it be annihilated; and let those who are left eat one another's flesh.'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> I took my staff Favor and cut it in pieces, to break my covenant which I had made with all the peoples.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> So it was broken on that day, and thus the afflicted of the flock who were watching me realized that it was the word of the LORD.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> I said to them, 'If it is good in your sight, give [me] my wages; but if not, never mind!' So they weighed out thirty [shekels] of silver as my wages.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Then the LORD said to me, 'Throw it to the potter, [that] magnificent price at which I was valued by them.' So I took the thirty [shekels] of silver and threw them to the potter in the house of the LORD.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Then I cut in pieces my second staff Union, to break the brotherhood between Judah and Israel.

## Zechariah 11:15-17

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> The LORD said to me, 'Take again for yourself the equipment of a foolish shepherd.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'For behold, I am going to raise up a shepherd in the land who will not care for the perishing, seek the scattered, heal the broken, or sustain the one standing, but will devour the flesh of the fat [sheep] and tear off their hoofs.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Woe to the worthless shepherd Who leaves the flock! A sword will be on his arm And on his right eye! His arm will be totally withered And his right eye will be blind.'
