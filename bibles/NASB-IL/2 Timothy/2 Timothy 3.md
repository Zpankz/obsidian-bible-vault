# 2 Timothy 3

## 2 Timothy 3:1-9

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> But realize this, that in the last days difficult times will come.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For men will be lovers of self, lovers of money, boastful, arrogant, revilers, disobedient to parents, ungrateful, unholy,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> unloving, irreconcilable, malicious gossips, without self-control, brutal, haters of good,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> treacherous, reckless, conceited, lovers of pleasure rather than lovers of God,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> holding to a form of godliness, although they have denied its power; Avoid such men as these.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> For among them are those who enter into households and captivate weak women weighed down with sins, led on by various impulses,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> always learning and never able to come to the knowledge of the truth.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Just as Jannes and Jambres opposed Moses, so these [men] also oppose the truth, men of depraved mind, rejected in regard to the faith.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> But they will not make further progress; for their folly will be obvious to all, just as Jannes's and Jambres's folly was also.

## 2 Timothy 3:10-17

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Now you followed my teaching, conduct, purpose, faith, patience, love, perseverance,

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> persecutions, [and] sufferings, such as happened to me at Antioch, at Iconium [and] at Lystra; what persecutions I endured, and out of them all the Lord rescued me!

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Indeed, all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> But evil men and impostors will proceed [from bad] to worse, deceiving and being deceived.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> You, however, continue in the things you have learned and become convinced of, knowing from whom you have learned [them],

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> and that from childhood you have known the sacred writings which are able to give you the wisdom that leads to salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> All Scripture is inspired by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, for training in righteousness;

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> so that the man of God may be adequate, equipped for every good work.
