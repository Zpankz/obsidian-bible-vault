## 2 Timothy 2:1-7

<sup>1</sup> You therefore, my son, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6]

<sup>2</sup> The things which you have heard from me in the presence of many witnesses, entrust these to faithful men who will be able to teach others also.[^7][^8][^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14]

<sup>3</sup> Suffer hardship with [me], as a good soldier of Christ Jesus.[^15][^16][^17][^18][^19][^20][^21][^22]

<sup>4</sup> No soldier in active service entangles himself in the affairs of everyday life, so that he may please the one who enlisted him as a soldier.[^23][^24][^25][^26][^27][^28][^29][^30]

<sup>5</sup> Also if anyone competes as an athlete, he does not win the prize unless he competes according to the rules.[^31][^32][^33]

<sup>6</sup> The hard-working farmer ought to be the first to receive his share of the crops.[^34][^35]

<sup>7</sup> Consider what I say, for the Lord will give you understanding in everything.

## 2 Timothy 2:8-13

<sup>8</sup> Remember Jesus Christ, risen from the dead, descendant of David, according to my gospel,

<sup>9</sup> for which I suffer hardship even to imprisonment as a criminal; but the word of God is not imprisoned.[^36]

<sup>10</sup> For this reason I endure all things for the sake of those who are chosen, so that they also may obtain the salvation which is in Christ Jesus [and] with [it] eternal glory.[^37][^38]

<sup>11</sup> It is a trustworthy statement: For if we died with Him, we will also live with Him;[^39][^40][^41][^42]

<sup>12</sup> If we endure, we will also reign with Him; If we deny Him, He also will deny us;[^43][^44][^45][^46]

<sup>13</sup> If we are faithless, He remains faithful, for He cannot deny Himself.[^47][^48][^49][^50][^51][^52][^53][^54]
