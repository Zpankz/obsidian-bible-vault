# Hosea 7

## Hosea 7:1-7

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> When I would heal Israel, The iniquity of Ephraim is uncovered, And the evil deeds of Samaria, For they deal falsely; The thief enters in, Bandits raid outside,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And they do not consider in their hearts That I remember all their wickedness. Now their deeds are all around them; They are before My face.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> With their wickedness they make the king glad, And the princes with their lies.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> They are all adulterers, Like an oven heated by the baker Who ceases to stir up [the fire] From the kneading of the dough until it is leavened.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> On the day of our king, the princes became sick with the heat of wine; He stretched out his hand with scoffers,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> For their hearts are like an oven [As] they approach their plotting; Their anger smolders all night, In the morning it burns like a flaming fire.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> All of them are hot like an oven, And they consume their rulers; All their kings have fallen. None of them calls on Me.

## Hosea 7:8-16

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Ephraim mixes himself with the nations; Ephraim has become a cake not turned.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Strangers devour his strength, Yet he does not know [it]; Gray hairs also are sprinkled on him, Yet he does not know [it].

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Though the pride of Israel testifies against him, Yet they have not returned to the LORD their God, Nor have they sought Him, for all this.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> So Ephraim has become like a silly dove, without sense; They call to Egypt, they go to Assyria.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> When they go, I will spread My net over them; I will bring them down like the birds of the sky. I will chastise them in accordance with the proclamation to their assembly.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Woe to them, for they have strayed from Me! Destruction is theirs, for they have rebelled against Me! I would redeem them, but they speak lies against Me.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And they do not cry to Me from their heart When they wail on their beds; For the sake of grain and new wine they assemble themselves, They turn away from Me.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Although I trained [and] strengthened their arms, Yet they devise evil against Me.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> They turn, [but] not upward, They are like a deceitful bow; Their princes will fall by the sword Because of the insolence of their tongue. This [will be] their derision in the land of Egypt.
