# John 19

## John 19:1-15

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Pilate then took Jesus and scourged Him.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And the soldiers twisted together a crown of thorns and put it on His head, and put a purple robe on Him;

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> and they [began] to come up to Him and say, 'Hail, King of the Jews!' and to give Him slaps [in the face].

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Pilate came out again and said to them, 'Behold, I am bringing Him out to you so that you may know that I find no guilt in Him.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Jesus then came out, wearing the crown of thorns and the purple robe. [Pilate] said to them, 'Behold, the Man!'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> So when the chief priests and the officers saw Him, they cried out saying, 'Crucify, crucify!' Pilate said to them, 'Take Him yourselves and crucify Him, for I find no guilt in Him.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> The Jews answered him, 'We have a law, and by that law He ought to die because He made Himself out [to] [be] the Son of God.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Therefore when Pilate heard this statement, he was [even] more afraid;

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> and he entered into the Praetorium again and said to Jesus, 'Where are You from?' But Jesus gave him no answer.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> So Pilate said to Him, 'You do not speak to me? Do You not know that I have authority to release You, and I have authority to crucify You?'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Jesus answered, 'You would have no authority over Me, unless it had been given you from above; for this reason he who delivered Me to you has [the] greater sin.'

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> As a result of this Pilate made efforts to release Him, but the Jews cried out saying, 'If you release this Man, you are no friend of Caesar; everyone who makes himself out [to be] a king opposes Caesar.'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Therefore when Pilate heard these words, he brought Jesus out, and sat down on the judgment seat at a place called The Pavement, but in Hebrew, Gabbatha.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Now it was the day of preparation for the Passover; it was about the sixth hour. And he said to the Jews, 'Behold, your King!'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> So they cried out, 'Away with [Him], away with [Him], crucify Him!' Pilate said to them, 'Shall I crucify your King?' The chief priests answered, 'We have no king but Caesar.'

## John 19:19-30

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> Pilate also wrote an inscription and put it on the cross. It was written, 'JESUS THE NAZARENE, THE KING OF THE JEWS.'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Therefore many of the Jews read this inscription, for the place where Jesus was crucified was near the city; and it was written in Hebrew, Latin [and] in Greek.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> So the chief priests of the Jews were saying to Pilate, 'Do not write, 'The King of the Jews'; but that He said, 'I am King of the Jews.''

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Pilate answered, 'What I have written I have written.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> Then the soldiers, when they had crucified Jesus, took His outer garments and made four parts, a part to every soldier and [also] the tunic; now the tunic was seamless, woven in one piece.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> So they said to one another, 'Let us not tear it, but cast lots for it, [to decide] whose it shall be'; [this was] to fulfill the Scripture: 'THEY DIVIDED MY OUTER GARMENTS AMONG THEM, AND FOR MY CLOTHING THEY CAST LOTS.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> Therefore the soldiers did these things. But standing by the cross of Jesus were His mother, and His mother's sister, Mary the [wife] of Clopas, and Mary Magdalene.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> When Jesus then saw His mother, and the disciple whom He loved standing nearby, He said to His mother, 'Woman, behold, your son!'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> Then He said to the disciple, 'Behold, your mother!' From that hour the disciple took her into his own [household].

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> After this, Jesus, knowing that all things had already been accomplished, to fulfill the Scripture, said, 'I am thirsty.'

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> A jar full of sour wine was standing there; so they put a sponge full of the sour wine upon [a branch] [of] hyssop and brought it up to His mouth.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> Therefore when Jesus had received the sour wine, He said, 'It is finished!' And He bowed His head and gave up His spirit.

## John 19:31-37

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> Then the Jews, because it was the day of preparation, so that the bodies would not remain on the cross on the Sabbath (for that Sabbath was a high day), asked Pilate that their legs might be broken, and [that] they might be taken away.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> So the soldiers came, and broke the legs of the first man and of the other who was crucified with Him;

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> but coming to Jesus, when they saw that He was already dead, they did not break His legs.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> But one of the soldiers pierced His side with a spear, and immediately blood and water came out.

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> And he who has seen has testified, and his testimony is true; and he knows that he is telling the truth, so that you also may believe.

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> For these things came to pass to fulfill the Scripture, 'NOT A BONE OF HIM SHALL BE BROKEN.'

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> And again another Scripture says, 'THEY SHALL LOOK ON HIM WHOM THEY PIERCED.'

## John 19:38-42

###### v38

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> Nicodemus, who had first come to Him by night, also came, bringing a mixture of myrrh and aloes, about a hundred pounds [weight].

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> So they took the body of Jesus and bound it in linen wrappings with the spices, as is the burial custom of the Jews.

###### v41
