# John 12

## John 12:1-11

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Jesus, therefore, six days before the Passover, came to Bethany where Lazarus was, whom Jesus had raised from the dead.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> So they made Him a supper there, and Martha was serving; but Lazarus was one of those reclining [at the] [table] with Him.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Mary then took a pound of very costly perfume of pure nard, and anointed the feet of Jesus and wiped His feet with her hair; and the house was filled with the fragrance of the perfume.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> But Judas Iscariot, one of His disciples, who was intending to betray Him, said,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Why was this perfume not sold for three hundred denarii and given to poor [people]?'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Now he said this, not because he was concerned about the poor, but because he was a thief, and as he had the money box, he used to pilfer what was put into it.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Therefore Jesus said, 'Let her alone, so that she may keep it for the day of My burial.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'For you always have the poor with you, but you do not always have Me.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> The large crowd of the Jews then learned that He was there; and they came, not for Jesus' sake only, but that they might also see Lazarus, whom He raised from the dead.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> But the chief priests planned to put Lazarus to death also;

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> because on account of him many of the Jews were going away and were believing in Jesus.

## John 12:12-19

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> On the next day the large crowd who had come to the feast, when they heard that Jesus was coming to Jerusalem,

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> took the branches of the palm trees and went out to meet Him, and [began] to shout, 'Hosanna! BLESSED IS HE WHO COMES IN THE NAME OF THE LORD, even the King of Israel.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Jesus, finding a young donkey, sat on it; as it is written,

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'FEAR NOT, DAUGHTER OF ZION; BEHOLD, YOUR KING IS COMING, SEATED ON A DONKEY'S COLT.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> These things His disciples did not understand at the first; but when Jesus was glorified, then they remembered that these things were written of Him, and that they had done these things to Him.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> So the people, who were with Him when He called Lazarus out of the tomb and raised him from the dead, continued to testify [about Him].

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> For this reason also the people went and met Him, because they heard that He had performed this sign.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> So the Pharisees said to one another, 'You see that you are not doing any good; look, the world has gone after Him.'

<sup>20</sup> Now there were some Greeks among those who were going up to worship at the feast;

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> these then came to Philip, who was from Bethsaida of Galilee, and [began to] ask him, saying, 'Sir, we wish to see Jesus.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Philip came and told Andrew; Andrew and Philip came and told Jesus.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> And Jesus answered them, saying, 'The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Truly, truly, I say to you, unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it bears much fruit.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'He who loves his life loses it, and he who hates his life in this world will keep it to life eternal.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'If anyone serves Me, he must follow Me; and where I am, there My servant will be also; if anyone serves Me, the Father will honor him.

## John 12:27-36

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'Now My soul has become troubled; and what shall I say, 'Father, save Me from this hour '? But for this purpose I came to this hour.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'Father, glorify Your name.' Then a voice came out of heaven: 'I have both glorified it, and will glorify it again.'

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> So the crowd [of people] who stood by and heard it were saying that it had thundered; others were saying, 'An angel has spoken to Him.'

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> Jesus answered and said, 'This voice has not come for My sake, but for your sakes.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'Now judgment is upon this world; now the ruler of this world will be cast out.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'And I, if I am lifted up from the earth, will draw all men to Myself.'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> But He was saying this to indicate the kind of death by which He was to die.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> The crowd then answered Him, 'We have heard out of the Law that the Christ is to remain forever; and how can You say, 'The Son of Man must be lifted up'? Who is this Son of Man?'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> So Jesus said to them, 'For a little while longer the Light is among you. Walk while you have the Light, so that darkness will not overtake you; he who walks in the darkness does not know where he goes.

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> 'While you have the Light, believe in the Light, so that you may become sons of Light.' These things Jesus spoke, and He went away and hid Himself from them.

## John 12:37-41

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> But though He had performed so many signs before them, [yet] they were not believing in Him.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> For this reason they could not believe, for Isaiah said again,

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> 'HE HAS BLINDED THEIR EYES AND HE HARDENED THEIR HEART, SO THAT THEY WOULD NOT SEE WITH THEIR EYES AND PERCEIVE WITH THEIR HEART, AND BE CONVERTED AND I HEAL THEM.'

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> These things Isaiah said because he saw His glory, and he spoke of Him.
