# John 18

## John 18:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> When Jesus had spoken these words, He went forth with His disciples over the ravine of the Kidron, where there was a garden, in which He entered with His disciples.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Now Judas also, who was betraying Him, knew the place, for Jesus had often met there with His disciples.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Judas then, having received the [Roman] cohort and officers from the chief priests and the Pharisees, came there with lanterns and torches and weapons.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> So Jesus, knowing all the things that were coming upon Him, went forth and said to them, 'Whom do you seek?'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> They answered Him, 'Jesus the Nazarene.' He said to them, 'I am [He].' And Judas also, who was betraying Him, was standing with them.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> So when He said to them, 'I am [He],' they drew back and fell to the ground.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Therefore He again asked them, 'Whom do you seek?' And they said, 'Jesus the Nazarene.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Jesus answered, 'I told you that I am [He]; so if you seek Me, let these go their way,'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> to fulfill the word which He spoke, 'Of those whom You have given Me I lost not one.'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Simon Peter then, having a sword, drew it and struck the high priest's slave, and cut off his right ear; and the slave's name was Malchus.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> So Jesus said to Peter, 'Put the sword into the sheath; the cup which the Father has given Me, shall I not drink it?'

<sup>12</sup> So the [Roman] cohort and the commander and the officers of the Jews, arrested Jesus and bound Him,

## John 18:13-27

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> and led Him to Annas first; for he was father-in-law of Caiaphas, who was high priest that year.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Now Caiaphas was the one who had advised the Jews that it was expedient for one man to die on behalf of the people.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Simon Peter was following Jesus, and [so] [was] another disciple. Now that disciple was known to the high priest, and entered with Jesus into the court of the high priest,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> but Peter was standing at the door outside. So the other disciple, who was known to the high priest, went out and spoke to the doorkeeper, and brought Peter in.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Then the slave-girl who kept the door said to Peter, 'You are not also [one] of this man's disciples, are you?' He said, 'I am not.'

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> Now the slaves and the officers were standing [there], having made a charcoal fire, for it was cold and they were warming themselves; and Peter was also with them, standing and warming himself.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> The high priest then questioned Jesus about His disciples, and about His teaching.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Jesus answered him, 'I have spoken openly to the world; I always taught in synagogues and in the temple, where all the Jews come together; and I spoke nothing in secret.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'Why do you question Me? Question those who have heard what I spoke to them; they know what I said.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> When He had said this, one of the officers standing nearby struck Jesus, saying, 'Is that the way You answer the high priest?'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> Jesus answered him, 'If I have spoken wrongly, testify of the wrong; but if rightly, why do you strike Me?'

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> So Annas sent Him bound to Caiaphas the high priest.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> Now Simon Peter was standing and warming himself. So they said to him, 'You are not also [one] of His disciples, are you?' He denied [it], and said, 'I am not.'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> One of the slaves of the high priest, being a relative of the one whose ear Peter cut off, said, 'Did I not see you in the garden with Him?'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> Peter then denied [it] again, and immediately a rooster crowed.

## John 18:28-40

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> Then they led Jesus from Caiaphas into the Praetorium, and it was early; and they themselves did not enter into the Praetorium so that they would not be defiled, but might eat the Passover.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> Therefore Pilate went out to them and said, 'What accusation do you bring against this Man?'

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> They answered and said to him, 'If this Man were not an evildoer, we would not have delivered Him to you.'

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> So Pilate said to them, 'Take Him yourselves, and judge Him according to your law.' The Jews said to him, 'We are not permitted to put anyone to death,'

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> to fulfill the word of Jesus which He spoke, signifying by what kind of death He was about to die.

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> Therefore Pilate entered again into the Praetorium, and summoned Jesus and said to Him, 'Are You the King of the Jews?'

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> Jesus answered, 'Are you saying this on your own initiative, or did others tell you about Me?'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> Pilate answered, 'I am not a Jew, am I? Your own nation and the chief priests delivered You to me; what have You done?'

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> Jesus answered, 'My kingdom is not of this world. If My kingdom were of this world, then My servants would be fighting so that I would not be handed over to the Jews; but as it is, My kingdom is not of this realm.'

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> Therefore Pilate said to Him, 'So You are a king?' Jesus answered, 'You say [correctly] that I am a king. For this I have been born, and for this I have come into the world, to testify to the truth. Everyone who is of the truth hears My voice.'

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> Pilate said to Him, 'What is truth?' And when he had said this, he went out again to the Jews and said to them, 'I find no guilt in Him.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> 'But you have a custom that I release someone for you at the Passover; do you wish then that I release for you the King of the Jews?'

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> So they cried out again, saying, 'Not this Man, but Barabbas.' Now Barabbas was a robber.
