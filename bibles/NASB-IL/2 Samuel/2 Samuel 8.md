# 2 Samuel 8

## 2 Samuel 8:1-8

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now after this it came about that David defeated the Philistines and subdued them; and David took control of the chief city from the hand of the Philistines.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> He defeated Moab, and measured them with the line, making them lie down on the ground; and he measured two lines to put to death and one full line to keep alive. And the Moabites became servants to David, bringing tribute.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Then David defeated Hadadezer, the son of Rehob king of Zobah, as he went to restore his rule at the River.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> David captured from him 1,700 horsemen and 20,000 foot soldiers; and David hamstrung the chariot horses, but reserved [enough] of them for 100 chariots.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> When the Arameans of Damascus came to help Hadadezer, king of Zobah, David killed 22,000 Arameans.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Then David put garrisons among the Arameans of Damascus, and the Arameans became servants to David, bringing tribute. And the LORD helped David wherever he went.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> David took the shields of gold which were carried by the servants of Hadadezer and brought them to Jerusalem.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> From Betah and from Berothai, cities of Hadadezer, King David took a very large amount of bronze.

## 2 Samuel 8:9-14

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Now when Toi king of Hamath heard that David had defeated all the army of Hadadezer,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Toi sent Joram his son to King David to greet him and bless him, because he had fought against Hadadezer and defeated him; for Hadadezer had been at war with Toi. And [Joram] brought with him articles of silver, of gold and of bronze.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> King David also dedicated these to the LORD, with the silver and gold that he had dedicated from all the nations which he had subdued:

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> from Aram and Moab and the sons of Ammon and the Philistines and Amalek, and from the spoil of Hadadezer, son of Rehob, king of Zobah.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> So David made a name [for himself] when he returned from killing 18,000 Arameans in the Valley of Salt.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> He put garrisons in Edom. In all Edom he put garrisons, and all the Edomites became servants to David. And the LORD helped David wherever he went.
