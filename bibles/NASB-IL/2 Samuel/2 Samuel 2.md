# 2 Samuel 2

## 2 Samuel 2:1-7

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Then it came about afterwards that David inquired of the LORD, saying, 'Shall I go up to one of the cities of Judah?' And the LORD said to him, 'Go up.' So David said, 'Where shall I go up?' And He said, 'To Hebron.'

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> So David went up there, and his two wives also, Ahinoam the Jezreelitess and Abigail the widow of Nabal the Carmelite.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And David brought up his men who [were] with him, each with his household; and they lived in the cities of Hebron.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Then the men of Judah came and there anointed David king over the house of Judah. And they told David, saying, 'It was the men of Jabesh-gilead who buried Saul.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> David sent messengers to the men of Jabesh-gilead, and said to them, 'May you be blessed of the LORD because you have shown this kindness to Saul your lord, and have buried him.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Now may the LORD show lovingkindness and truth to you; and I also will show this goodness to you, because you have done this thing.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'Now therefore, let your hands be strong and be valiant; for Saul your lord is dead, and also the house of Judah has anointed me king over them.'

## 2 Samuel 2:8-17

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But Abner the son of Ner, commander of Saul's army, had taken Ish-bosheth the son of Saul and brought him over to Mahanaim.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> He made him king over Gilead, over the Ashurites, over Jezreel, over Ephraim, and over Benjamin, even over all Israel.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Ish-bosheth, Saul's son, was forty years old when he became king over Israel, and he was king for two years. The house of Judah, however, followed David.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> The time that David was king in Hebron over the house of Judah was seven years and six months.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Now Abner the son of Ner, went out from Mahanaim to Gibeon with the servants of Ish-bosheth the son of Saul.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> And Joab the son of Zeruiah and the servants of David went out and met them by the pool of Gibeon; and they sat down, one on the one side of the pool and the other on the other side of the pool.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Then Abner said to Joab, 'Now let the young men arise and hold a contest before us.' And Joab said, 'Let them arise.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> So they arose and went over by count, twelve for Benjamin and Ish-bosheth the son of Saul, and twelve of the servants of David.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Each one of them seized his opponent by the head and [thrust] his sword in his opponent's side; so they fell down together. Therefore that place was called Helkath-hazzurim, which is in Gibeon.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> That day the battle was very severe, and Abner and the men of Israel were beaten before the servants of David.

## 2 Samuel 2:18-24

###### v18

###### v19

###### v21

###### v24
