# Ecclesiastes 6

## Ecclesiastes 6:1-3

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> There is an evil which I have seen under the sun and it is prevalent among men--

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> a man to whom God has given riches and wealth and honor so that his soul lacks nothing of all that he desires; yet God has not empowered him to eat from them, for a foreigner enjoys them. This is vanity and a severe affliction.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> If a man fathers a hundred [children] and lives many years, however many they be, but his soul is not satisfied with good things and he does not even have a [proper] burial, [then] I say, 'Better the miscarriage than he,

## Ecclesiastes 6:7-10

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> All a man's labor is for his mouth and yet the appetite is not satisfied.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> For what advantage does the wise man have over the fool? What [advantage] does the poor man have, knowing [how] to walk before the living?

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> What the eyes see is better than what the soul desires. This too is futility and a striving after wind.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Whatever exists has already been named, and it is known what man is; for he cannot dispute with him who is stronger than he is.

###### v12
