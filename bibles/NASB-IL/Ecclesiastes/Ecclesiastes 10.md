# Ecclesiastes 10

## Ecclesiastes 10:1-3

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Dead flies make a perfumer's oil stink, so a little foolishness is weightier than wisdom [and] honor.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> A wise man's heart [directs him] toward the right, but the foolish man's heart [directs him] toward the left.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Even when the fool walks along the road, his sense is lacking and he demonstrates to everyone [that] he is a fool.

## Ecclesiastes 10:4-11

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> If the ruler's temper rises against you, do not abandon your position, because composure allays great offenses.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> There is an evil I have seen under the sun, like an error which goes forth from the ruler--

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> folly is set in many exalted places while rich men sit in humble places.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> I have seen slaves [riding] on horses and princes walking like slaves on the land.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> He who digs a pit may fall into it, and a serpent may bite him who breaks through a wall.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> He who quarries stones may be hurt by them, and he who splits logs may be endangered by them.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> If the axe is dull and he does not sharpen [its] edge, then he must exert more strength. Wisdom has the advantage of giving success.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> If the serpent bites before being charmed, there is no profit for the charmer.

## Ecclesiastes 10:12-15

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Words from the mouth of a wise man are gracious, while the lips of a fool consume him;

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> the beginning of his talking is folly and the end of it is wicked madness.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Yet the fool multiplies words. No man knows what will happen, and who can tell him what will come after him?

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> The toil of a fool [so] wearies him that he does not [even] know how to go to a city.

## Ecclesiastes 10:16-20

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Woe to you, O land, whose king is a lad and whose princes feast in the morning.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Blessed are you, O land, whose king is of nobility and whose princes eat at the appropriate time-- for strength and not for drunkenness.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> Through indolence the rafters sag, and through slackness the house leaks.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> [Men] prepare a meal for enjoyment, and wine makes life merry, and money is the answer to everything.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Furthermore, in your bedchamber do not curse a king, and in your sleeping rooms do not curse a rich man, for a bird of the heavens will carry the sound and the winged creature will make the matter known.
