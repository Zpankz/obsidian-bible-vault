# 1 Timothy 4

## 1 Timothy 4:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> But the Spirit explicitly says that in later times some will fall away from the faith, paying attention to deceitful spirits and doctrines of demons,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> by means of the hypocrisy of liars seared in their own conscience as with a branding iron,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> [men] who forbid marriage [and advocate] abstaining from foods which God has created to be gratefully shared in by those who believe and know the truth.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> For everything created by God is good, and nothing is to be rejected if it is received with gratitude;

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> for it is sanctified by means of the word of God and prayer.

## 1 Timothy 4:6-16

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> In pointing out these things to the brethren, you will be a good servant of Christ Jesus, [constantly] nourished on the words of the faith and of the sound doctrine which you have been following.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> But have nothing to do with worldly fables fit only for old women. On the other hand, discipline yourself for the purpose of godliness;

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> for bodily discipline is only of little profit, but godliness is profitable for all things, since it holds promise for the present life and [also] for the [life] to come.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> It is a trustworthy statement deserving full acceptance.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For it is for this we labor and strive, because we have fixed our hope on the living God, who is the Savior of all men, especially of believers.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Prescribe and teach these things.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Let no one look down on your youthfulness, but [rather] in speech, conduct, love, faith [and] purity, show yourself an example of those who believe.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Until I come, give attention to the [public] reading [of Scripture], to exhortation and teaching.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Do not neglect the spiritual gift within you, which was bestowed on you through prophetic utterance with the laying on of hands by the presbytery.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Take pains with these things; be [absorbed] in them, so that your progress will be evident to all.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Pay close attention to yourself and to your teaching; persevere in these things, for as you do this you will ensure salvation both for yourself and for those who hear you.
