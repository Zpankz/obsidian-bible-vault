## 1 Timothy 2:1-8

<sup>1</sup> First of all, then, I urge that entreaties [and] prayers, petitions [and] thanksgivings, be made on behalf of all men,[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

<sup>2</sup> for kings and all who are in authority, so that we may lead a tranquil and quiet life in all godliness and dignity.[^9][^10][^11][^12][^13]

<sup>3</sup> This is good and acceptable in the sight of God our Savior,[^14]

<sup>4</sup> who desires all men to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth.[^15][^16][^17][^18][^19][^20][^21][^22]

<sup>5</sup> For there is one God, [and] one mediator also between God and men, [the] man Christ Jesus,[^23][^24][^25][^26][^27][^28][^29][^30]

<sup>6</sup> who gave Himself as a ransom for all, the testimony [given] at the proper time.[^31][^32]

<sup>7</sup> For this I was appointed a preacher and an apostle (I am telling the truth, I am not lying) as a teacher of the Gentiles in faith and truth.[^33][^34]

<sup>8</sup> Therefore I want the men in every place to pray, lifting up holy hands, without wrath and dissension.[^35][^36]

## 1 Timothy 2:9-15

<sup>9</sup> Likewise, [I want] women to adorn themselves with proper clothing, modestly and discreetly, not with braided hair and gold or pearls or costly garments,[^37][^38][^39][^40][^41][^42]

<sup>10</sup> but rather by means of good works, as is proper for women making a claim to godliness.

<sup>11</sup> A woman must quietly receive instruction with entire submissiveness.[^43][^44]

<sup>12</sup> But I do not allow a woman to teach or exercise authority over a man, but to remain quiet.[^45]

<sup>13</sup> For it was Adam who was first created, [and] then Eve.[^46][^47]

<sup>14</sup> And [it was] not Adam [who] was deceived, but the woman being deceived, fell into transgression.[^48][^49][^50]

<sup>15</sup> But [women] will be preserved through the bearing of children if they continue in faith and love and sanctity with self-restraint.
