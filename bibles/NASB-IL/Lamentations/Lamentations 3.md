## Lamentations 3:1-20

<sup>1</sup> I am the man who has seen affliction Because of the rod of His wrath.

<sup>2</sup> He has driven me and made me walk In darkness and not in light.

<sup>3</sup> Surely against me He has turned His hand Repeatedly all the day.

<sup>4</sup> He has caused my flesh and my skin to waste away, He has broken my bones.

<sup>5</sup> He has besieged and encompassed me with bitterness and hardship.

<sup>6</sup> In dark places He has made me dwell, Like those who have long been dead.

<sup>7</sup> He has walled me in so that I cannot go out; He has made my chain heavy.

<sup>8</sup> Even when I cry out and call for help, He shuts out my prayer.

<sup>9</sup> He has blocked my ways with hewn stone; He has made my paths crooked.

<sup>10</sup> He is to me like a bear lying in wait, [Like] a lion in secret places.

<sup>11</sup> He has turned aside my ways and torn me to pieces; He has made me desolate.

<sup>12</sup> He bent His bow And set me as a target for the arrow.

<sup>13</sup> He made the arrows of His quiver To enter into my inward parts.

<sup>14</sup> I have become a laughingstock to all my people, Their [mocking] song all the day.

<sup>15</sup> He has filled me with bitterness, He has made me drunk with wormwood.

<sup>16</sup> He has broken my teeth with gravel; He has made me cower in the dust.

<sup>17</sup> My soul has been rejected from peace; I have forgotten happiness.

<sup>18</sup> So I say, 'My strength has perished, And [so has] my hope from the LORD.'

<sup>19</sup> Remember my affliction and my wandering, the wormwood and bitterness.

<sup>20</sup> Surely my soul remembers And is bowed down within me.

## Lamentations 3:21-36

<sup>21</sup> This I recall to my mind, Therefore I have hope.

<sup>22</sup> The LORD'S lovingkindnesses indeed never cease, For His compassions never fail.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

<sup>23</sup> [They] are new every morning; Great is Your faithfulness.[^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15][^16]

<sup>24</sup> 'The LORD is my portion,' says my soul, 'Therefore I have hope in Him.'[^17][^18][^19][^20]

<sup>25</sup> The LORD is good to those who wait for Him, To the person who seeks Him.[^21][^22][^23][^24][^25][^26][^27][^28]

<sup>26</sup> [It is] good that he waits silently For the salvation of the LORD.[^29][^30][^31][^32][^33][^34][^35][^36]

<sup>27</sup> [It is] good for a man that he should bear The yoke in his youth.[^37][^38][^39][^40][^41][^42]

<sup>28</sup> Let him sit alone and be silent Since He has laid [it] on him.

<sup>29</sup> Let him put his mouth in the dust, Perhaps there is hope.

<sup>30</sup> Let him give his cheek to the smiter, Let him be filled with reproach.

<sup>31</sup> For the Lord will not reject forever,[^43][^44][^45][^46]

<sup>32</sup> For if He causes grief, Then He will have compassion According to His abundant lovingkindness.[^47][^48]

<sup>33</sup> For He does not afflict willingly Or grieve the sons of men.

<sup>34</sup> To crush under His feet All the prisoners of the land,

<sup>35</sup> To deprive a man of justice In the presence of the Most High,

<sup>36</sup> To defraud a man in his lawsuit-- Of these things the Lord does not approve.

<sup>46</sup> All our enemies have opened their mouths against us.

<sup>50</sup> Until the LORD looks down And sees from heaven.
