# Lamentations 4

## Lamentations 4:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> How dark the gold has become, [How] the pure gold has changed! The sacred stones are poured out At the corner of every street.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> The precious sons of Zion, Weighed against fine gold, How they are regarded as earthen jars, The work of a potter's hands!

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Even jackals offer the breast, They nurse their young; [But] the daughter of my people has become cruel Like ostriches in the wilderness.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> The tongue of the infant cleaves To the roof of its mouth because of thirst; The little ones ask for bread, [But] no one breaks [it] for them.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Those who ate delicacies Are desolate in the streets; Those reared in purple Embrace ash pits.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> For the iniquity of the daughter of my people Is greater than the sin of Sodom, Which was overthrown as in a moment, And no hands were turned toward her.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Her consecrated ones were purer than snow, They were whiter than milk; They were more ruddy [in] body than corals, Their polishing [was like] lapis lazuli.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Their appearance is blacker than soot, They are not recognized in the streets; Their skin is shriveled on their bones, It is withered, it has become like wood.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Better are those slain with the sword Than those slain with hunger; For they pine away, being stricken For lack of the fruits of the field.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> The hands of compassionate women Boiled their own children; They became food for them Because of the destruction of the daughter of my people.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> The LORD has accomplished His wrath, He has poured out His fierce anger; And He has kindled a fire in Zion Which has consumed its foundations.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> The kings of the earth did not believe, Nor [did] any of the inhabitants of the world, That the adversary and the enemy Could enter the gates of Jerusalem.

## Lamentations 4:13-20

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Because of the sins of her prophets [And] the iniquities of her priests, Who have shed in her midst The blood of the righteous;

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> They wandered, blind, in the streets; They were defiled with blood So that no one could touch their garments.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Depart! Unclean!' they cried of themselves. 'Depart, depart, do not touch!' So they fled and wandered; [Men] among the nations said, 'They shall not continue to dwell [with us].'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> The presence of the LORD has scattered them, He will not continue to regard them; They did not honor the priests, They did not favor the elders.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Yet our eyes failed, [Looking] for help was useless; In our watching we have watched For a nation that could not save.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> They hunted our steps So that we could not walk in our streets; Our end drew near, Our days were finished For our end had come.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> Our pursuers were swifter Than the eagles of the sky; They chased us on the mountains, They waited in ambush for us in the wilderness.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> The breath of our nostrils, the LORD'S anointed, Was captured in their pits, Of whom we had said, 'Under his shadow We shall live among the nations.'

<sup>22</sup> [The punishment] of your iniquity has been completed, O daughter of Zion; He will exile you no longer. [But] He will punish your iniquity, O daughter of Edom; He will expose your sins!
