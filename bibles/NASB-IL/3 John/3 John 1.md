## 3 John 1:3-8

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> For I was very glad when brethren came and testified to your truth, [that is], how you are walking in truth.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> I have no greater joy than this, to hear of my children walking in the truth.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Beloved, you are acting faithfully in whatever you accomplish for the brethren, and especially [when they are] strangers;

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> and they have testified to your love before the church. You will do well to send them on their way in a manner worthy of God.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> For they went out for the sake of the Name, accepting nothing from the Gentiles.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Therefore we ought to support such men, so that we may be fellow workers with the truth.

## 3 John 1:9-11

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> I wrote something to the church; but Diotrephes, who loves to be first among them, does not accept what we say.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For this reason, if I come, I will call attention to his deeds which he does, unjustly accusing us with wicked words; and not satisfied with this, he himself does not receive the brethren, either, and he forbids those who desire [to do so] and puts [them] out of the church.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Beloved, do not imitate what is evil, but what is good. The one who does good is of God; the one who does evil has not seen God.
