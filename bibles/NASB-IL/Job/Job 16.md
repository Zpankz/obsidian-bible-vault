## Job 16:1-5

<sup>1</sup> Then Job answered,

<sup>2</sup> 'I have heard many such things; Sorry comforters are you all.

<sup>3</sup> 'Is there [no] limit to windy words? Or what plagues you that you answer?

<sup>4</sup> 'I too could speak like you, If I were in your place. I could compose words against you And shake my head at you.

<sup>5</sup> 'I could strengthen you with my mouth, And the solace of my lips could lessen [your pain].

## Job 16:6-16

<sup>6</sup> 'If I speak, my pain is not lessened, And if I hold back, what has left me?

<sup>7</sup> 'But now He has exhausted me; You have laid waste all my company.

<sup>8</sup> 'You have shriveled me up, It has become a witness; And my leanness rises up against me, It testifies to my face.

<sup>9</sup> 'His anger has torn me and hunted me down, He has gnashed at me with His teeth; My adversary glares at me.

<sup>10</sup> 'They have gaped at me with their mouth, They have slapped me on the cheek with contempt; They have massed themselves against me.

<sup>11</sup> 'God hands me over to ruffians And tosses me into the hands of the wicked.

<sup>12</sup> 'I was at ease, but He shattered me, And He has grasped me by the neck and shaken me to pieces; He has also set me up as His target.

<sup>13</sup> 'His arrows surround me. Without mercy He splits my kidneys open; He pours out my gall on the ground.

<sup>14</sup> 'He breaks through me with breach after breach; He runs at me like a warrior.

<sup>15</sup> 'I have sewed sackcloth over my skin And thrust my horn in the dust.

<sup>16</sup> 'My face is flushed from weeping, And deep darkness is on my eyelids,

## Job 16:17-22

<sup>17</sup> Although there is no violence in my hands, And my prayer is pure.

<sup>18</sup> 'O earth, do not cover my blood, And let there be no [resting] place for my cry.

<sup>19</sup> 'Even now, behold, my witness is in heaven, And my advocate is on high.

<sup>20</sup> 'My friends are my scoffers; My eye weeps to God.

<sup>21</sup> 'O that a man might plead with God As a man with his neighbor!

<sup>22</sup> 'For when a few years are past, I shall go the way of no return.
