# Job 28

## Job 28:1-13

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Surely there is a mine for silver And a place where they refine gold.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Iron is taken from the dust, And copper is smelted from rock.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> '[Man] puts an end to darkness, And to the farthest limit he searches out The rock in gloom and deep shadow.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'He sinks a shaft far from habitation, Forgotten by the foot; They hang and swing to and fro far from men.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'The earth, from it comes food, And underneath it is turned up as fire.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Its rocks are the source of sapphires, And its dust [contains] gold.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'The path no bird of prey knows, Nor has the falcon's eye caught sight of it.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'The proud beasts have not trodden it, Nor has the [fierce] lion passed over it.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'He puts his hand on the flint; He overturns the mountains at the base.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'He hews out channels through the rocks, And his eye sees anything precious.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'He dams up the streams from flowing, And what is hidden he brings out to the light.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'But where can wisdom be found? And where is the place of understanding?

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Man does not know its value, Nor is it found in the land of the living.

## Job 28:14-19

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'The deep says, 'It is not in me'; And the sea says, 'It is not with me.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Pure gold cannot be given in exchange for it, Nor can silver be weighed as its price.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'It cannot be valued in the gold of Ophir, In precious onyx, or sapphire.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Gold or glass cannot equal it, Nor can it be exchanged for articles of fine gold.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'Coral and crystal are not to be mentioned; And the acquisition of wisdom is above [that of] pearls.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'The topaz of Ethiopia cannot equal it, Nor can it be valued in pure gold.

## Job 28:20-28

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Where then does wisdom come from? And where is the place of understanding?

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'Thus it is hidden from the eyes of all living And concealed from the birds of the sky.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'Abaddon and Death say, 'With our ears we have heard a report of it.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'God understands its way, And He knows its place.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'For He looks to the ends of the earth And sees everything under the heavens.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'When He imparted weight to the wind And meted out the waters by measure,

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> When He set a limit for the rain And a course for the thunderbolt,

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> Then He saw it and declared it; He established it and also searched it out.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'And to man He said, 'Behold, the fear of the Lord, that is wisdom; And to depart from evil is understanding.''
