## Job 31:1-8

<sup>1</sup> 'I have made a covenant with my eyes; How then could I gaze at a virgin?[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

<sup>2</sup> 'And what is the portion of God from above Or the heritage of the Almighty from on high?

<sup>3</sup> 'Is it not calamity to the unjust And disaster to those who work iniquity?

<sup>4</sup> 'Does He not see my ways And number all my steps?[^9]

<sup>5</sup> 'If I have walked with falsehood, And my foot has hastened after deceit,

<sup>6</sup> Let Him weigh me with accurate scales, And let God know my integrity.

<sup>7</sup> 'If my step has turned from the way, Or my heart followed my eyes, Or if any spot has stuck to my hands,

<sup>8</sup> Let me sow and another eat, And let my crops be uprooted.

## Job 31:9-15

<sup>9</sup> 'If my heart has been enticed by a woman, Or I have lurked at my neighbor's doorway,

<sup>10</sup> May my wife grind for another, And let others kneel down over her.

<sup>11</sup> 'For that would be a lustful crime; Moreover, it would be an iniquity [punishable by] judges.

<sup>12</sup> 'For it would be fire that consumes to Abaddon, And would uproot all my increase.

<sup>13</sup> 'If I have despised the claim of my male or female slaves When they filed a complaint against me,

<sup>14</sup> What then could I do when God arises? And when He calls me to account, what will I answer Him?

<sup>15</sup> 'Did not He who made me in the womb make him, And the same one fashion us in the womb?

## Job 31:16-23

<sup>16</sup> 'If I have kept the poor from [their] desire, Or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail,

<sup>17</sup> Or have eaten my morsel alone, And the orphan has not shared it

<sup>18</sup> (But from my youth he grew up with me as with a father, And from infancy I guided her),

<sup>19</sup> If I have seen anyone perish for lack of clothing, Or that the needy had no covering,

<sup>20</sup> If his loins have not thanked me, And if he has not been warmed with the fleece of my sheep,

<sup>21</sup> If I have lifted up my hand against the orphan, Because I saw I had support in the gate,

<sup>22</sup> Let my shoulder fall from the socket, And my arm be broken off at the elbow.

<sup>23</sup> 'For calamity from God is a terror to me, And because of His majesty I can do nothing.

## Job 31:24-32

<sup>24</sup> 'If I have put my confidence [in] gold, And called fine gold my trust,

<sup>25</sup> If I have gloated because my wealth was great, And because my hand had secured [so] much;

<sup>26</sup> If I have looked at the sun when it shone Or the moon going in splendor,

<sup>27</sup> And my heart became secretly enticed, And my hand threw a kiss from my mouth,

<sup>28</sup> That too would have been an iniquity [calling for] judgment, For I would have denied God above.

<sup>29</sup> 'Have I rejoiced at the extinction of my enemy, Or exulted when evil befell him?[^10]

<sup>30</sup> 'No, I have not allowed my mouth to sin By asking for his life in a curse.

<sup>31</sup> 'Have the men of my tent not said, 'Who can find one who has not been satisfied with his meat '?

<sup>32</sup> 'The alien has not lodged outside, [For] I have opened my doors to the traveler.

## Job 31:33-40

<sup>33</sup> 'Have I covered my transgressions like Adam, By hiding my iniquity in my bosom,[^11]

<sup>34</sup> Because I feared the great multitude, And the contempt of families terrified me, And kept silent and did not go out of doors?

<sup>35</sup> 'Oh that I had one to hear me! Behold, here is my signature; Let the Almighty answer me! And the indictment which my adversary has written,

<sup>36</sup> Surely I would carry it on my shoulder, I would bind it to myself like a crown.

<sup>37</sup> 'I would declare to Him the number of my steps; Like a prince I would approach Him.

<sup>38</sup> 'If my land cries out against me, And its furrows weep together;

<sup>39</sup> If I have eaten its fruit without money, Or have caused its owners to lose their lives,

<sup>40</sup> Let briars grow instead of wheat, And stinkweed instead of barley.' The words of Job are ended.
