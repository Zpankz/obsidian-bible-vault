# Job 7

## Job 7:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Is not man forced to labor on earth, And [are not] his days like the days of a hired man?

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'As a slave who pants for the shade, And as a hired man who eagerly waits for his wages,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> So am I allotted months of vanity, And nights of trouble are appointed me.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'When I lie down I say, 'When shall I arise?' But the night continues, And I am continually tossing until dawn.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'My flesh is clothed with worms and a crust of dirt, My skin hardens and runs.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'My days are swifter than a weaver's shuttle, And come to an end without hope.

## Job 7:7-16

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'Remember that my life is [but] breath; My eye will not again see good.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'The eye of him who sees me will behold me no longer; Your eyes [will be] on me, but I will not be.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'When a cloud vanishes, it is gone, So he who goes down to Sheol does not come up.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'He will not return again to his house, Nor will his place know him anymore.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Therefore I will not restrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit, I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Am I the sea, or the sea monster, That You set a guard over me?

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'If I say, 'My bed will comfort me, My couch will ease my complaint,'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Then You frighten me with dreams And terrify me by visions;

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> So that my soul would choose suffocation, Death rather than my pains.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'I waste away; I will not live forever. Leave me alone, for my days are [but] a breath.

## Job 7:17-21

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'What is man that You magnify him, And that You are concerned about him,

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> That You examine him every morning And try him every moment?

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Will You never turn Your gaze away from me, Nor let me alone until I swallow my spittle?

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Have I sinned? What have I done to You, O watcher of men? Why have You set me as Your target, So that I am a burden to myself?

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'Why then do You not pardon my transgression And take away my iniquity? For now I will lie down in the dust; And You will seek me, but I will not be.'
