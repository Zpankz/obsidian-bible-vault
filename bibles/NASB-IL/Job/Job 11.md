## Job 11:1-6

<sup>1</sup> Then Zophar the Naamathite answered,

<sup>2</sup> 'Shall a multitude of words go unanswered, And a talkative man be acquitted?

<sup>3</sup> 'Shall your boasts silence men? And shall you scoff and none rebuke?

<sup>4</sup> 'For you have said, 'My teaching is pure, And I am innocent in your eyes.'

<sup>5</sup> 'But would that God might speak, And open His lips against you,

<sup>6</sup> And show you the secrets of wisdom! For sound wisdom has two sides. Know then that God forgets a part of your iniquity.[^1]

## Job 11:7-12

<sup>7</sup> 'Can you discover the depths of God? Can you discover the limits of the Almighty?

<sup>8</sup> '[They are] high as the heavens, what can you do? Deeper than Sheol, what can you know?

<sup>9</sup> 'Its measure is longer than the earth And broader than the sea.

<sup>10</sup> 'If He passes by or shuts up, Or calls an assembly, who can restrain Him?

<sup>11</sup> 'For He knows false men, And He sees iniquity without investigating.[^2][^3]

<sup>12</sup> 'An idiot will become intelligent When the foal of a wild donkey is born a man.

## Job 11:13-20

<sup>13</sup> 'If you would direct your heart right And spread out your hand to Him,

<sup>14</sup> If iniquity is in your hand, put it far away, And do not let wickedness dwell in your tents;

<sup>15</sup> 'Then, indeed, you could lift up your face without [moral] defect, And you would be steadfast and not fear.

<sup>16</sup> 'For you would forget [your] trouble, As waters that have passed by, you would remember [it].

<sup>17</sup> 'Your life would be brighter than noonday; Darkness would be like the morning.[^4][^5]

<sup>18</sup> 'Then you would trust, because there is hope; And you would look around and rest securely.[^6]

<sup>19</sup> 'You would lie down and none would disturb [you], And many would entreat your favor.

<sup>20</sup> 'But the eyes of the wicked will fail, And there will be no escape for them; And their hope is to breathe their last.'
