# Job 20

## Job 20:1-9

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Then Zophar the Naamathite answered,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Therefore my disquieting thoughts make me respond, Even because of my inward agitation.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'I listened to the reproof which insults me, And the spirit of my understanding makes me answer.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Do you know this from of old, From the establishment of man on earth,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> That the triumphing of the wicked is short, And the joy of the godless momentary?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Though his loftiness reaches the heavens, And his head touches the clouds,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> He perishes forever like his refuse; Those who have seen him will say, 'Where is he?'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'He flies away like a dream, and they cannot find him; Even like a vision of the night he is chased away.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'The eye which saw him sees him no longer, And his place no longer beholds him.

## Job 20:10-22

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'His sons favor the poor, And his hands give back his wealth.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'His bones are full of his youthful vigor, But it lies down with him in the dust.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Though evil is sweet in his mouth [And] he hides it under his tongue,

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> [Though] he desires it and will not let it go, But holds it in his mouth,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> [Yet] his food in his stomach is changed To the venom of cobras within him.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'He swallows riches, But will vomit them up; God will expel them from his belly.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'He sucks the poison of cobras; The viper's tongue slays him.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'He does not look at the streams, The rivers flowing with honey and curds.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'He returns what he has attained And cannot swallow [it]; As to the riches of his trading, He cannot even enjoy [them].

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'For he has oppressed [and] forsaken the poor; He has seized a house which he has not built.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Because he knew no quiet within him, He does not retain anything he desires.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'Nothing remains for him to devour, Therefore his prosperity does not endure.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'In the fullness of his plenty he will be cramped; The hand of everyone who suffers will come [against] him.

## Job 20:23-29

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'When he fills his belly, [God] will send His fierce anger on him And will rain [it] on him while he is eating.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'He may flee from the iron weapon, [But] the bronze bow will pierce him.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'It is drawn forth and comes out of his back, Even the glittering point from his gall. Terrors come upon him,

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> Complete darkness is held in reserve for his treasures, And unfanned fire will devour him; It will consume the survivor in his tent.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'The heavens will reveal his iniquity, And the earth will rise up against him.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'The increase of his house will depart; [His possessions] will flow away in the day of His anger.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'This is the wicked man's portion from God, Even the heritage decreed to him by God.'
