# Job 24

## Job 24:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Why are times not stored up by the Almighty, And why do those who know Him not see His days?

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Some remove the landmarks; They seize and devour flocks.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'They drive away the donkeys of the orphans; They take the widow's ox for a pledge.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'They push the needy aside from the road; The poor of the land are made to hide themselves altogether.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Behold, as wild donkeys in the wilderness They go forth seeking food in their activity, As bread for [their] children in the desert.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'They harvest their fodder in the field And glean the vineyard of the wicked.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'They spend the night naked, without clothing, And have no covering against the cold.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'They are wet with the mountain rains And hug the rock for want of a shelter.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Others snatch the orphan from the breast, And against the poor they take a pledge.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'They cause [the poor] to go about naked without clothing, And they take away the sheaves from the hungry.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Within the walls they produce oil; They tread wine presses but thirst.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'From the city men groan, And the souls of the wounded cry out; Yet God does not pay attention to folly.

## Job 24:13-17

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Others have been with those who rebel against the light; They do not want to know its ways Nor abide in its paths.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'The murderer arises at dawn; He kills the poor and the needy, And at night he is as a thief.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'The eye of the adulterer waits for the twilight, Saying, 'No eye will see me.' And he disguises his face.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'In the dark they dig into houses, They shut themselves up by day; They do not know the light.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'For the morning is the same to him as thick darkness, For he is familiar with the terrors of thick darkness.

## Job 24:18-25

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'They are insignificant on the surface of the water; Their portion is cursed on the earth. They do not turn toward the vineyards.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Drought and heat consume the snow waters, [So does] Sheol [those who] have sinned.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'A mother will forget him; The worm feeds sweetly till he is no longer remembered. And wickedness will be broken like a tree.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'He wrongs the barren woman And does no good for the widow.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'But He drags off the valiant by His power; He rises, but no one has assurance of life.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'He provides them with security, and they are supported; And His eyes are on their ways.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'They are exalted a little while, then they are gone; Moreover, they are brought low and like everything gathered up; Even like the heads of grain they are cut off.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'Now if it is not so, who can prove me a liar, And make my speech worthless?'
