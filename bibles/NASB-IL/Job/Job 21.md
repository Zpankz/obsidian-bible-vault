## Job 21:1-6

<sup>1</sup> Then Job answered,

<sup>2</sup> 'Listen carefully to my speech, And let this be your [way of] consolation.

<sup>3</sup> 'Bear with me that I may speak; Then after I have spoken, you may mock.

<sup>4</sup> 'As for me, is my complaint to man? And why should I not be impatient?

<sup>5</sup> 'Look at me, and be astonished, And put [your] hand over [your] mouth.

<sup>6</sup> 'Even when I remember, I am disturbed, And horror takes hold of my flesh.

## Job 21:7-16

<sup>7</sup> 'Why do the wicked [still] live, Continue on, also become very powerful?[^1]

<sup>8</sup> 'Their descendants are established with them in their sight, And their offspring before their eyes,

<sup>9</sup> Their houses are safe from fear, And the rod of God is not on them.

<sup>10</sup> 'His ox mates without fail; His cow calves and does not abort.

<sup>11</sup> 'They send forth their little ones like the flock, And their children skip about.

<sup>12</sup> 'They sing to the timbrel and harp And rejoice at the sound of the flute.

<sup>13</sup> 'They spend their days in prosperity, And suddenly they go down to Sheol.

<sup>14</sup> 'They say to God, 'Depart from us! We do not even desire the knowledge of Your ways.

<sup>15</sup> 'Who is the Almighty, that we should serve Him, And what would we gain if we entreat Him?'[^2]

<sup>16</sup> 'Behold, their prosperity is not in their hand; The counsel of the wicked is far from me.

## Job 21:17-26

<sup>17</sup> 'How often is the lamp of the wicked put out, Or does their calamity fall on them? Does God apportion destruction in His anger?

<sup>18</sup> 'Are they as straw before the wind, And like chaff which the storm carries away?

<sup>19</sup> '[You say], 'God stores away a man's iniquity for his sons.' Let God repay him so that he may know [it].

<sup>20</sup> 'Let his own eyes see his decay, And let him drink of the wrath of the Almighty.

<sup>21</sup> 'For what does he care for his household after him, When the number of his months is cut off?

<sup>22</sup> 'Can anyone teach God knowledge, In that He judges those on high?

<sup>23</sup> 'One dies in his full strength, Being wholly at ease and satisfied;

<sup>24</sup> His sides are filled out with fat, And the marrow of his bones is moist,

<sup>25</sup> While another dies with a bitter soul, Never even tasting [anything] good.

<sup>26</sup> 'Together they lie down in the dust, And worms cover them.

## Job 21:27-34

<sup>27</sup> 'Behold, I know your thoughts, And the plans by which you would wrong me.

<sup>28</sup> 'For you say, 'Where is the house of the nobleman, And where is the tent, the dwelling places of the wicked?'

<sup>29</sup> 'Have you not asked wayfaring men, And do you not recognize their witness?

<sup>30</sup> 'For the wicked is reserved for the day of calamity; They will be led forth at the day of fury.

<sup>31</sup> 'Who will confront him with his actions, And who will repay him for what he has done?

<sup>32</sup> 'While he is carried to the grave, [Men] will keep watch over [his] tomb.

<sup>33</sup> 'The clods of the valley will gently cover him; Moreover, all men will follow after him, While countless ones [go] before him.

<sup>34</sup> 'How then will you vainly comfort me, For your answers remain [full of] falsehood?'
