## Job 3:1-10

<sup>1</sup> Afterward Job opened his mouth and cursed the day of his [birth].

<sup>2</sup> And Job said,

<sup>3</sup> 'Let the day perish on which I was to be born, And the night [which] said, 'A boy is conceived.'

<sup>4</sup> 'May that day be darkness; Let not God above care for it, Nor light shine on it.

<sup>5</sup> 'Let darkness and black gloom claim it; Let a cloud settle on it; Let the blackness of the day terrify it.

<sup>6</sup> '[As for] that night, let darkness seize it; Let it not rejoice among the days of the year; Let it not come into the number of the months.

<sup>7</sup> 'Behold, let that night be barren; Let no joyful shout enter it.

<sup>8</sup> 'Let those curse it who curse the day, Who are prepared to rouse Leviathan.

<sup>9</sup> 'Let the stars of its twilight be darkened; Let it wait for light but have none, And let it not see the breaking dawn;

<sup>10</sup> Because it did not shut the opening of my [mother's] womb, Or hide trouble from my eyes.

## Job 3:11-19

<sup>11</sup> 'Why did I not die at birth, Come forth from the womb and expire?

<sup>12</sup> 'Why did the knees receive me, And why the breasts, that I should suck?

<sup>13</sup> 'For now I would have lain down and been quiet; I would have slept then, I would have been at rest,

<sup>14</sup> With kings and [with] counselors of the earth, Who rebuilt ruins for themselves;

<sup>15</sup> Or with princes who had gold, Who were filling their houses [with] silver.

<sup>16</sup> 'Or like a miscarriage which is discarded, I would not be, As infants that never saw light.

<sup>17</sup> 'There the wicked cease from raging, And there the weary are at rest.

<sup>18</sup> 'The prisoners are at ease together; They do not hear the voice of the taskmaster.

<sup>19</sup> 'The small and the great are there, And the slave is free from his master.

## Job 3:20-26

<sup>20</sup> 'Why is light given to him who suffers, And life to the bitter of soul,

<sup>21</sup> Who long for death, but there is none, And dig for it more than for hidden treasures,

<sup>22</sup> Who rejoice greatly, [And] exult when they find the grave?

<sup>23</sup> '[Why is light given] to a man whose way is hidden, And whom God has hedged in?

<sup>24</sup> 'For my groaning comes at the sight of my food, And my cries pour out like water.

<sup>25</sup> 'For what I fear comes upon me, And what I dread befalls me.[^1][^2]

<sup>26</sup> 'I am not at ease, nor am I quiet, And I am not at rest, but turmoil comes.'
