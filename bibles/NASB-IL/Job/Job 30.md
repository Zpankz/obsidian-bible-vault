# Job 30

## Job 30:1-14

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'But now those younger than I mock me, Whose fathers I disdained to put with the dogs of my flock.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Indeed, what [good was] the strength of their hands to me? Vigor had perished from them.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'From want and famine they are gaunt Who gnaw the dry ground by night in waste and desolation,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Who pluck mallow by the bushes, And whose food is the root of the broom shrub.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'They are driven from the community; They shout against them as [against] a thief,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> So that they dwell in dreadful valleys, In holes of the earth and of the rocks.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'Among the bushes they cry out; Under the nettles they are gathered together.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Fools, even those without a name, They were scourged from the land.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'And now I have become their taunt, I have even become a byword to them.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'They abhor me [and] stand aloof from me, And they do not refrain from spitting at my face.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Because He has loosed His bowstring and afflicted me, They have cast off the bridle before me.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'On the right hand their brood arises; They thrust aside my feet and build up against me their ways of destruction.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'They break up my path, They profit from my destruction; No one restrains them.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'As [through] a wide breach they come, Amid the tempest they roll on.

## Job 30:15-31

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Terrors are turned against me; They pursue my honor as the wind, And my prosperity has passed away like a cloud.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'And now my soul is poured out within me; Days of affliction have seized me.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'At night it pierces my bones within me, And my gnawing [pains] take no rest.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'By a great force my garment is distorted; It binds me about as the collar of my coat.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'He has cast me into the mire, And I have become like dust and ashes.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'I cry out to You for help, but You do not answer me; I stand up, and You turn Your attention against me.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'You have become cruel to me; With the might of Your hand You persecute me.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'You lift me up to the wind [and] cause me to ride; And You dissolve me in a storm.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'For I know that You will bring me to death And to the house of meeting for all living.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Yet does not one in a heap of ruins stretch out [his] hand, Or in his disaster therefore cry out for help?

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'Have I not wept for the one whose life is hard? Was not my soul grieved for the needy?

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'When I expected good, then evil came; When I waited for light, then darkness came.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'I am seething within and cannot relax; Days of affliction confront me.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'I go about mourning without comfort; I stand up in the assembly [and] cry out for help.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'I have become a brother to jackals And a companion of ostriches.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'My skin turns black on me, And my bones burn with fever.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'Therefore my harp is turned to mourning, And my flute to the sound of those who weep.
