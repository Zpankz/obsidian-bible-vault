# Job 32

## Job 32:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Then these three men ceased answering Job, because he was righteous in his own eyes.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> But the anger of Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite, of the family of Ram burned; against Job his anger burned because he justified himself before God.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And his anger burned against his three friends because they had found no answer, and yet had condemned Job.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Now Elihu had waited to speak to Job because they were years older than he.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> And when Elihu saw that there was no answer in the mouth of the three men his anger burned.

## Job 32:6-14

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> So Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite spoke out and said, 'I am young in years and you are old; Therefore I was shy and afraid to tell you what I think.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'I thought age should speak, And increased years should teach wisdom.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'But it is a spirit in man, And the breath of the Almighty gives them understanding.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'The abundant [in years] may not be wise, Nor may elders understand justice.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'So I say, 'Listen to me, I too will tell what I think.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Behold, I waited for your words, I listened to your reasonings, While you pondered what to say.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'I even paid close attention to you; Indeed, there was no one who refuted Job, Not one of you who answered his words.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Do not say, 'We have found wisdom; God will rout him, not man.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'For he has not arranged [his] words against me, Nor will I reply to him with your arguments.

## Job 32:15-22

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'They are dismayed, they no longer answer; Words have failed them.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'Shall I wait, because they do not speak, Because they stop [and] no longer answer?

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'I too will answer my share, I also will tell my opinion.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'For I am full of words; The spirit within me constrains me.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Behold, my belly is like unvented wine, Like new wineskins it is about to burst.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Let me speak that I may get relief; Let me open my lips and answer.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'Let me now be partial to no one, Nor flatter [any] man.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'For I do not know how to flatter, [Else] my Maker would soon take me away.
