## Job 15:1-16

<sup>1</sup> Then Eliphaz the Temanite responded,

<sup>2</sup> 'Should a wise man answer with windy knowledge And fill himself with the east wind?

<sup>3</sup> 'Should he argue with useless talk, Or with words which are not profitable?

<sup>4</sup> 'Indeed, you do away with reverence And hinder meditation before God.

<sup>5</sup> 'For your guilt teaches your mouth, And you choose the language of the crafty.

<sup>6</sup> 'Your own mouth condemns you, and not I; And your own lips testify against you.

<sup>7</sup> 'Were you the first man to be born, Or were you brought forth before the hills?

<sup>8</sup> 'Do you hear the secret counsel of God, And limit wisdom to yourself?

<sup>9</sup> 'What do you know that we do not know? [What] do you understand that we do not?

<sup>10</sup> 'Both the gray-haired and the aged are among us, Older than your father.

<sup>11</sup> 'Are the consolations of God too small for you, Even the word [spoken] gently with you?

<sup>12</sup> 'Why does your heart carry you away? And why do your eyes flash,

<sup>13</sup> That you should turn your spirit against God And allow [such] words to go out of your mouth?

<sup>14</sup> 'What is man, that he should be pure, Or he who is born of a woman, that he should be righteous?[^1]

<sup>15</sup> 'Behold, He puts no trust in His holy ones, And the heavens are not pure in His sight;[^2][^3]

<sup>16</sup> How much less one who is detestable and corrupt, Man, who drinks iniquity like water!

## Job 15:17-35

<sup>17</sup> 'I will tell you, listen to me; And what I have seen I will also declare;

<sup>18</sup> What wise men have told, And have not concealed from their fathers,

<sup>19</sup> To whom alone the land was given, And no alien passed among them.

<sup>20</sup> 'The wicked man writhes in pain all [his] days, And numbered are the years stored up for the ruthless.

<sup>21</sup> 'Sounds of terror are in his ears; While at peace the destroyer comes upon him.

<sup>22</sup> 'He does not believe that he will return from darkness, And he is destined for the sword.

<sup>23</sup> 'He wanders about for food, saying, 'Where is it?' He knows that a day of darkness is at hand.

<sup>24</sup> 'Distress and anguish terrify him, They overpower him like a king ready for the attack,

<sup>25</sup> Because he has stretched out his hand against God And conducts himself arrogantly against the Almighty.

<sup>26</sup> 'He rushes headlong at Him With his massive shield.

<sup>27</sup> 'For he has covered his face with his fat And made his thighs heavy with flesh.

<sup>28</sup> 'He has lived in desolate cities, In houses no one would inhabit, Which are destined to become ruins.

<sup>29</sup> 'He will not become rich, nor will his wealth endure; And his grain will not bend down to the ground.

<sup>30</sup> 'He will not escape from darkness; The flame will wither his shoots, And by the breath of His mouth he will go away.

<sup>31</sup> 'Let him not trust in emptiness, deceiving himself; For emptiness will be his reward.

<sup>32</sup> 'It will be accomplished before his time, And his palm branch will not be green.

<sup>33</sup> 'He will drop off his unripe grape like the vine, And will cast off his flower like the olive tree.

<sup>34</sup> 'For the company of the godless is barren, And fire consumes the tents of the corrupt.

<sup>35</sup> 'They conceive mischief and bring forth iniquity, And their mind prepares deception.'
