# Job 41

## Job 41:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Can you draw out Leviathan with a fishhook? Or press down his tongue with a cord?

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Can you put a rope in his nose Or pierce his jaw with a hook?

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Will he make many supplications to you, Or will he speak to you soft words?

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Will he make a covenant with you? Will you take him for a servant forever?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Will you play with him as with a bird, Or will you bind him for your maidens?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Will the traders bargain over him? Will they divide him among the merchants?

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'Can you fill his skin with harpoons, Or his head with fishing spears?

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Lay your hand on him; Remember the battle; you will not do it again!

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Behold, your expectation is false; Will you be laid low even at the sight of him?

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'No one is so fierce that he dares to arouse him; Who then is he that can stand before Me?

## Job 41:11-34

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Who has given to Me that I should repay [him]? [Whatever] is under the whole heaven is Mine.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'I will not keep silence concerning his limbs, Or his mighty strength, or his orderly frame.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Who can strip off his outer armor? Who can come within his double mail?

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Who can open the doors of his face? Around his teeth there is terror.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> '[His] strong scales are [his] pride, Shut up [as with] a tight seal.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'One is so near to another That no air can come between them.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'They are joined one to another; They clasp each other and cannot be separated.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'His sneezes flash forth light, And his eyes are like the eyelids of the morning.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Out of his mouth go burning torches; Sparks of fire leap forth.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Out of his nostrils smoke goes forth As [from] a boiling pot and [burning] rushes.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'His breath kindles coals, And a flame goes forth from his mouth.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'In his neck lodges strength, And dismay leaps before him.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'The folds of his flesh are joined together, Firm on him and immovable.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'His heart is as hard as a stone, Even as hard as a lower millstone.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'When he raises himself up, the mighty fear; Because of the crashing they are bewildered.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'The sword that reaches him cannot avail, Nor the spear, the dart or the javelin.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'He regards iron as straw, Bronze as rotten wood.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'The arrow cannot make him flee; Slingstones are turned into stubble for him.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'Clubs are regarded as stubble; He laughs at the rattling of the javelin.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'His underparts are [like] sharp potsherds; He spreads out [like] a threshing sledge on the mire.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'He makes the depths boil like a pot; He makes the sea like a jar of ointment.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'Behind him he makes a wake to shine; One would think the deep to be gray-haired.

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> 'Nothing on earth is like him, One made without fear.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> 'He looks on everything that is high; He is king over all the sons of pride.'
