## Job 38:4-11

###### v4

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Who set its measurements? Since you know. Or who stretched the line on it?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'On what were its bases sunk? Or who laid its cornerstone,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> When the morning stars sang together And all the sons of God shouted for joy?

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Or [who] enclosed the sea with doors When, bursting forth, it went out from the womb;

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> When I made a cloud its garment And thick darkness its swaddling band,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> And I placed boundaries on it And set a bolt and doors,

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> And I said, 'Thus far you shall come, but no farther; And here shall your proud waves stop '?

## Job 38:12-24

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Have you ever in your life commanded the morning, [And] caused the dawn to know its place,

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> That it might take hold of the ends of the earth, And the wicked be shaken out of it?

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'It is changed like clay [under] the seal; And they stand forth like a garment.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'From the wicked their light is withheld, And the uplifted arm is broken.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'Have you entered into the springs of the sea Or walked in the recesses of the deep?

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Have the gates of death been revealed to you, Or have you seen the gates of deep darkness?

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'Have you understood the expanse of the earth? Tell [Me], if you know all this.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Where is the way to the dwelling of light? And darkness, where is its place,

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> That you may take it to its territory And that you may discern the paths to its home?

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'You know, for you were born then, And the number of your days is great!

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'Have you entered the storehouses of the snow, Or have you seen the storehouses of the hail,

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> Which I have reserved for the time of distress, For the day of war and battle?

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Where is the way that the light is divided, [Or] the east wind scattered on the earth?

## Job 38:25-41

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'Who has cleft a channel for the flood, Or a way for the thunderbolt,

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> To bring rain on a land without people, [On] a desert without a man in it,

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> To satisfy the waste and desolate land And to make the seeds of grass to sprout?

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'Has the rain a father? Or who has begotten the drops of dew?

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'From whose womb has come the ice? And the frost of heaven, who has given it birth?

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'Water becomes hard like stone, And the surface of the deep is imprisoned.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'Can you bind the chains of the Pleiades, Or loose the cords of Orion?

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'Can you lead forth a constellation in its season, And guide the Bear with her satellites?

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> 'Do you know the ordinances of the heavens, Or fix their rule over the earth?

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> 'Can you lift up your voice to the clouds, So that an abundance of water will cover you?

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> 'Can you send forth lightnings that they may go And say to you, 'Here we are'?

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> 'Who has put wisdom in the innermost being Or given understanding to the mind?

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> 'Who can count the clouds by wisdom, Or tip the water jars of the heavens,

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> When the dust hardens into a mass And the clods stick together?

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> 'Can you hunt the prey for the lion, Or satisfy the appetite of the young lions,

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> When they crouch in [their] dens [And] lie in wait in [their] lair?

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> 'Who prepares for the raven its nourishment When its young cry to God And wander about without food?
