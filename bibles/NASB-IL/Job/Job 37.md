# Job 37

## Job 37:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'At this also my heart trembles, And leaps from its place.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Listen closely to the thunder of His voice, And the rumbling that goes out from His mouth.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Under the whole heaven He lets it loose, And His lightning to the ends of the earth.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'After it, a voice roars; He thunders with His majestic voice, And He does not restrain the lightnings when His voice is heard.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'God thunders with His voice wondrously, Doing great things which we cannot comprehend.

## Job 37:6-13

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'For to the snow He says, 'Fall on the earth,' And to the downpour and the rain, 'Be strong.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'He seals the hand of every man, That all men may know His work.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Then the beast goes into its lair And remains in its den.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Out of the south comes the storm, And out of the north the cold.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'From the breath of God ice is made, And the expanse of the waters is frozen.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Also with moisture He loads the thick cloud; He disperses the cloud of His lightning.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'It changes direction, turning around by His guidance, That it may do whatever He commands it On the face of the inhabited earth.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Whether for correction, or for His world, Or for lovingkindness, He causes it to happen.

## Job 37:14-20

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Listen to this, O Job, Stand and consider the wonders of God.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Do you know how God establishes them, And makes the lightning of His cloud to shine?

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'Do you know about the layers of the thick clouds, The wonders of one perfect in knowledge,

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> You whose garments are hot, When the land is still because of the south wind?

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'Can you, with Him, spread out the skies, Strong as a molten mirror?

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Teach us what we shall say to Him; We cannot arrange [our case] because of darkness.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Shall it be told Him that I would speak? Or should a man say that he would be swallowed up?

###### v21

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'The Almighty-- we cannot find Him; He is exalted in power And He will not do violence to justice and abundant righteousness.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Therefore men fear Him; He does not regard any who are wise of heart.'
