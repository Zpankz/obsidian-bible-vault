## Job 8:1-7

<sup>1</sup> Then Bildad the Shuhite answered,

<sup>2</sup> 'How long will you say these things, And the words of your mouth be a mighty wind?

<sup>3</sup> 'Does God pervert justice? Or does the Almighty pervert what is right?

<sup>4</sup> 'If your sons sinned against Him, Then He delivered them into the power of their transgression.

<sup>5</sup> 'If you would seek God And implore the compassion of the Almighty,

<sup>6</sup> If you are pure and upright, Surely now He would rouse Himself for you And restore your righteous estate.

<sup>7</sup> 'Though your beginning was insignificant, Yet your end will increase greatly.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

## Job 8:8-19

<sup>8</sup> 'Please inquire of past generations, And consider the things searched out by their fathers.

<sup>9</sup> 'For we are [only] of yesterday and know nothing, Because our days on earth are as a shadow.[^9]

<sup>10</sup> 'Will they not teach you [and] tell you, And bring forth words from their minds?

<sup>11</sup> 'Can the papyrus grow up without a marsh? Can the rushes grow without water?

<sup>12</sup> 'While it is still green [and] not cut down, Yet it withers before any [other] plant.

<sup>13</sup> 'So are the paths of all who forget God; And the hope of the godless will perish,

<sup>14</sup> Whose confidence is fragile, And whose trust a spider's web.

<sup>15</sup> 'He trusts in his house, but it does not stand; He holds fast to it, but it does not endure.

<sup>16</sup> 'He thrives before the sun, And his shoots spread out over his garden.

<sup>17</sup> 'His roots wrap around a rock pile, He grasps a house of stones.

<sup>18</sup> 'If he is removed from his place, Then it will deny him, [saying], 'I never saw you.'

<sup>19</sup> 'Behold, this is the joy of His way; And out of the dust others will spring.
