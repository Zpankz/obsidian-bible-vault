# Job 39

## Job 39:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Do you know the time the mountain goats give birth? Do you observe the calving of the deer?

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Can you count the months they fulfill, Or do you know the time they give birth?

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'They kneel down, they bring forth their young, They get rid of their labor pains.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Their offspring become strong, they grow up in the open field; They leave and do not return to them.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Who sent out the wild donkey free? And who loosed the bonds of the swift donkey,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> To whom I gave the wilderness for a home And the salt land for his dwelling place?

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'He scorns the tumult of the city, The shoutings of the driver he does not hear.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'He explores the mountains for his pasture And searches after every green thing.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Will the wild ox consent to serve you, Or will he spend the night at your manger?

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'Can you bind the wild ox in a furrow with ropes, Or will he harrow the valleys after you?

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Will you trust him because his strength is great And leave your labor to him?

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Will you have faith in him that he will return your grain And gather [it from] your threshing floor?

## Job 39:13-18

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'The ostriches' wings flap joyously With the pinion and plumage of love,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> For she abandons her eggs to the earth And warms them in the dust,

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> And she forgets that a foot may crush them, Or that a wild beast may trample them.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'She treats her young cruelly, as if [they] were not hers; Though her labor be in vain, [she] is unconcerned;

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Because God has made her forget wisdom, And has not given her a share of understanding.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'When she lifts herself on high, She laughs at the horse and his rider.

## Job 39:19-25

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'Do you give the horse [his] might? Do you clothe his neck with a mane?

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'Do you make him leap like the locust? His majestic snorting is terrible.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'He paws in the valley, and rejoices in [his] strength; He goes out to meet the weapons.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'He laughs at fear and is not dismayed; And he does not turn back from the sword.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'The quiver rattles against him, The flashing spear and javelin.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'With shaking and rage he races over the ground, And he does not stand still at the voice of the trumpet.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'As often as the trumpet [sounds] he says, 'Aha!' And he scents the battle from afar, And the thunder of the captains and the war cry.

## Job 39:26-30

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'Is it by your understanding that the hawk soars, Stretching his wings toward the south?

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'Is it at your command that the eagle mounts up And makes his nest on high?

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'On the cliff he dwells and lodges, Upon the rocky crag, an inaccessible place.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'From there he spies out food; His eyes see [it] from afar.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'His young ones also suck up blood; And where the slain are, there is he.'
