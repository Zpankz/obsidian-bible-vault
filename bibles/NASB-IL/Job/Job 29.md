# Job 29

## Job 29:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> And Job again took up his discourse and said,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Oh that I were as in months gone by, As in the days when God watched over me;

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> When His lamp shone over my head, [And] by His light I walked through darkness;

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> As I was in the prime of my days, When the friendship of God [was] over my tent;

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> When the Almighty was yet with me, [And] my children were around me;

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> When my steps were bathed in butter, And the rock poured out for me streams of oil!

## Job 29:7-17

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'When I went out to the gate of the city, When I took my seat in the square,

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> The young men saw me and hid themselves, And the old men arose [and] stood.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'The princes stopped talking And put [their] hands on their mouths;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> The voice of the nobles was hushed, And their tongue stuck to their palate.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'For when the ear heard, it called me blessed, And when the eye saw, it gave witness of me,

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Because I delivered the poor who cried for help, And the orphan who had no helper.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'The blessing of the one ready to perish came upon me, And I made the widow's heart sing for joy.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'I put on righteousness, and it clothed me; My justice was like a robe and a turban.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'I was eyes to the blind And feet to the lame.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'I was a father to the needy, And I investigated the case which I did not know.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'I broke the jaws of the wicked And snatched the prey from his teeth.

## Job 29:18-25

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'Then I thought, 'I shall die in my nest, And I shall multiply [my] days as the sand.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'My root is spread out to the waters, And dew lies all night on my branch.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'My glory is [ever] new with me, And my bow is renewed in my hand.'

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'To me they listened and waited, And kept silent for my counsel.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'After my words they did not speak again, And my speech dropped on them.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'They waited for me as for the rain, And opened their mouth as for the spring rain.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'I smiled on them when they did not believe, And the light of my face they did not cast down.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'I chose a way for them and sat as chief, And dwelt as a king among the troops, As one who comforted the mourners.
