# Job 5

## Job 5:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Call now, is there anyone who will answer you? And to which of the holy ones will you turn?

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'For anger slays the foolish man, And jealousy kills the simple.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'I have seen the foolish taking root, And I cursed his abode immediately.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'His sons are far from safety, They are even oppressed in the gate, And there is no deliverer.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'His harvest the hungry devour And take it to a [place of] thorns, And the schemer is eager for their wealth.

## Job 5:6-16

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'For affliction does not come from the dust, Nor does trouble sprout from the ground,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> For man is born for trouble, As sparks fly upward.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'But as for me, I would seek God, And I would place my cause before God;

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Who does great and unsearchable things, Wonders without number.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'He gives rain on the earth And sends water on the fields,

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> So that He sets on high those who are lowly, And those who mourn are lifted to safety.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'He frustrates the plotting of the shrewd, So that their hands cannot attain success.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'He captures the wise by their own shrewdness, And the advice of the cunning is quickly thwarted.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'By day they meet with darkness, And grope at noon as in the night.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'But He saves from the sword of their mouth, And the poor from the hand of the mighty.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'So the helpless has hope, And unrighteousness must shut its mouth.

## Job 5:17-27

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Behold, how happy is the man whom God reproves, So do not despise the discipline of the Almighty.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'For He inflicts pain, and gives relief; He wounds, and His hands [also] heal.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'From six troubles He will deliver you, Even in seven evil will not touch you.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'In famine He will redeem you from death, And in war from the power of the sword.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'You will be hidden from the scourge of the tongue, And you will not be afraid of violence when it comes.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'You will laugh at violence and famine, And you will not be afraid of wild beasts.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'For you will be in league with the stones of the field, And the beasts of the field will be at peace with you.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'You will know that your tent is secure, For you will visit your abode and fear no loss.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'You will know also that your descendants will be many, And your offspring as the grass of the earth.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'You will come to the grave in full vigor, Like the stacking of grain in its season.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'Behold this; we have investigated it, [and] so it is. Hear it, and know for yourself.'
