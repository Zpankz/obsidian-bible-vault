## Job 5:1-5

<sup>1</sup> 'Call now, is there anyone who will answer you? And to which of the holy ones will you turn?[^1]

<sup>2</sup> 'For anger slays the foolish man, And jealousy kills the simple.

<sup>3</sup> 'I have seen the foolish taking root, And I cursed his abode immediately.

<sup>4</sup> 'His sons are far from safety, They are even oppressed in the gate, And there is no deliverer.

<sup>5</sup> 'His harvest the hungry devour And take it to a [place of] thorns, And the schemer is eager for their wealth.

## Job 5:6-16

<sup>6</sup> 'For affliction does not come from the dust, Nor does trouble sprout from the ground,

<sup>7</sup> For man is born for trouble, As sparks fly upward.[^2]

<sup>8</sup> 'But as for me, I would seek God, And I would place my cause before God;[^3][^4][^5][^6][^7]

<sup>9</sup> Who does great and unsearchable things, Wonders without number.[^8][^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15]

<sup>10</sup> 'He gives rain on the earth And sends water on the fields,

<sup>11</sup> So that He sets on high those who are lowly, And those who mourn are lifted to safety.

<sup>12</sup> 'He frustrates the plotting of the shrewd, So that their hands cannot attain success.[^16][^17][^18][^19][^20][^21][^22][^23]

<sup>13</sup> 'He captures the wise by their own shrewdness, And the advice of the cunning is quickly thwarted.[^24][^25]

<sup>14</sup> 'By day they meet with darkness, And grope at noon as in the night.

<sup>15</sup> 'But He saves from the sword of their mouth, And the poor from the hand of the mighty.

<sup>16</sup> 'So the helpless has hope, And unrighteousness must shut its mouth.

## Job 5:17-27

<sup>17</sup> 'Behold, how happy is the man whom God reproves, So do not despise the discipline of the Almighty.[^26][^27][^28][^29][^30][^31][^32]

<sup>18</sup> 'For He inflicts pain, and gives relief; He wounds, and His hands [also] heal.[^33][^34][^35][^36][^37]

<sup>19</sup> 'From six troubles He will deliver you, Even in seven evil will not touch you.[^38][^39][^40][^41][^42][^43]

<sup>20</sup> 'In famine He will redeem you from death, And in war from the power of the sword.[^44]

<sup>21</sup> 'You will be hidden from the scourge of the tongue, And you will not be afraid of violence when it comes.[^45]

<sup>22</sup> 'You will laugh at violence and famine, And you will not be afraid of wild beasts.[^46]

<sup>23</sup> 'For you will be in league with the stones of the field, And the beasts of the field will be at peace with you.[^47]

<sup>24</sup> 'You will know that your tent is secure, For you will visit your abode and fear no loss.[^48][^49]

<sup>25</sup> 'You will know also that your descendants will be many, And your offspring as the grass of the earth.[^50]

<sup>26</sup> 'You will come to the grave in full vigor, Like the stacking of grain in its season.[^51][^52][^53][^54][^55][^56]

<sup>27</sup> 'Behold this; we have investigated it, [and] so it is. Hear it, and know for yourself.'
