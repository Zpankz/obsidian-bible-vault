# Philippians 2

## Philippians 2:1-11

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Therefore if there is any encouragement in Christ, if there is any consolation of love, if there is any fellowship of the Spirit, if any affection and compassion,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> make my joy complete by being of the same mind, maintaining the same love, united in spirit, intent on one purpose.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Do nothing from selfishness or empty conceit, but with humility of mind regard one another as more important than yourselves;

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> do not [merely] look out for your own personal interests, but also for the interests of others.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> who, although He existed in the form of God, did not regard equality with God a thing to be grasped,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> but emptied Himself, taking the form of a bond-servant, [and] being made in the likeness of men.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Being found in appearance as a man, He humbled Himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> For this reason also, God highly exalted Him, and bestowed on Him the name which is above every name,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> so that at the name of Jesus EVERY KNEE WILL BOW, of those who are in heaven and on earth and under the earth,

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> and that every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

## Philippians 2:14-30

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Do all things without grumbling or disputing;

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> so that you will prove yourselves to be blameless and innocent, children of God above reproach in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you appear as lights in the world,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> holding fast the word of life, so that in the day of Christ I will have reason to glory because I did not run in vain nor toil in vain.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> But even if I am being poured out as a drink offering upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice and share my joy with you all.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> You too, [I urge you], rejoice in the same way and share your joy with me.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> But I hope in the Lord Jesus to send Timothy to you shortly, so that I also may be encouraged when I learn of your condition.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> For I have no one [else] of kindred spirit who will genuinely be concerned for your welfare.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> For they all seek after their own interests, not those of Christ Jesus.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But you know of his proven worth, that he served with me in the furtherance of the gospel like a child [serving] his father.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> Therefore I hope to send him immediately, as soon as I see how things [go] with me;

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> and I trust in the Lord that I myself also will be coming shortly.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> But I thought it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother and fellow worker and fellow soldier, who is also your messenger and minister to my need;

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> because he was longing for you all and was distressed because you had heard that he was sick.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> For indeed he was sick to the point of death, but God had mercy on him, and not on him only but also on me, so that I would not have sorrow upon sorrow.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> Therefore I have sent him all the more eagerly so that when you see him again you may rejoice and I may be less concerned [about you].

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> Receive him then in the Lord with all joy, and hold men like him in high regard;

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> because he came close to death for the work of Christ, risking his life to complete what was deficient in your service to me.
