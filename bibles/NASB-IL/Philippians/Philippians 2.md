## Philippians 2:1-11

<sup>1</sup> Therefore if there is any encouragement in Christ, if there is any consolation of love, if there is any fellowship of the Spirit, if any affection and compassion,[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7]

<sup>2</sup> make my joy complete by being of the same mind, maintaining the same love, united in spirit, intent on one purpose.[^8][^9][^10][^11][^12]

<sup>3</sup> Do nothing from selfishness or empty conceit, but with humility of mind regard one another as more important than yourselves;[^13][^14][^15][^16][^17][^18][^19][^20]

<sup>4</sup> do not [merely] look out for your own personal interests, but also for the interests of others.[^21][^22][^23][^24][^25][^26][^27][^28]

<sup>5</sup> Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus,[^29][^30][^31][^32][^33][^34][^35][^36]

<sup>6</sup> who, although He existed in the form of God, did not regard equality with God a thing to be grasped,[^37][^38][^39][^40][^41][^42][^43][^44]

<sup>7</sup> but emptied Himself, taking the form of a bond-servant, [and] being made in the likeness of men.[^45][^46][^47][^48][^49][^50][^51][^52]

<sup>8</sup> Being found in appearance as a man, He humbled Himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross.[^53][^54][^55][^56][^57][^58][^59][^60]

<sup>9</sup> For this reason also, God highly exalted Him, and bestowed on Him the name which is above every name,[^61][^62][^63][^64][^65][^66][^67][^68]

<sup>10</sup> so that at the name of Jesus EVERY KNEE WILL BOW, of those who are in heaven and on earth and under the earth,[^69][^70][^71][^72][^73][^74][^75][^76]

<sup>11</sup> and that every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.[^77][^78][^79][^80][^81][^82][^83][^84]

## Philippians 2:14-30

<sup>14</sup> Do all things without grumbling or disputing;[^101][^102][^103][^104][^105][^106][^107][^108]

<sup>15</sup> so that you will prove yourselves to be blameless and innocent, children of God above reproach in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you appear as lights in the world,[^109][^110][^111][^112][^113][^114][^115]

<sup>16</sup> holding fast the word of life, so that in the day of Christ I will have reason to glory because I did not run in vain nor toil in vain.[^116]

<sup>17</sup> But even if I am being poured out as a drink offering upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice and share my joy with you all.[^117]

<sup>18</sup> You too, [I urge you], rejoice in the same way and share your joy with me.

<sup>19</sup> But I hope in the Lord Jesus to send Timothy to you shortly, so that I also may be encouraged when I learn of your condition.[^118]

<sup>20</sup> For I have no one [else] of kindred spirit who will genuinely be concerned for your welfare.

<sup>21</sup> For they all seek after their own interests, not those of Christ Jesus.[^119][^120][^121][^122]

<sup>22</sup> But you know of his proven worth, that he served with me in the furtherance of the gospel like a child [serving] his father.

<sup>23</sup> Therefore I hope to send him immediately, as soon as I see how things [go] with me;

<sup>24</sup> and I trust in the Lord that I myself also will be coming shortly.

<sup>25</sup> But I thought it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother and fellow worker and fellow soldier, who is also your messenger and minister to my need;[^123]

<sup>26</sup> because he was longing for you all and was distressed because you had heard that he was sick.

<sup>27</sup> For indeed he was sick to the point of death, but God had mercy on him, and not on him only but also on me, so that I would not have sorrow upon sorrow.

<sup>28</sup> Therefore I have sent him all the more eagerly so that when you see him again you may rejoice and I may be less concerned [about you].

<sup>29</sup> Receive him then in the Lord with all joy, and hold men like him in high regard;

<sup>30</sup> because he came close to death for the work of Christ, risking his life to complete what was deficient in your service to me.
