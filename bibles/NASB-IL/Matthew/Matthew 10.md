# Matthew 10

## Matthew 10:1-4

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Jesus summoned His twelve disciples and gave them authority over unclean spirits, to cast them out, and to heal every kind of disease and every kind of sickness.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Now the names of the twelve apostles are these: The first, Simon, who is called Peter, and Andrew his brother; and James the son of Zebedee, and John his brother;

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Philip and Bartholomew; Thomas and Matthew the tax collector; James the son of Alphaeus, and Thaddaeus;

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Simon the Zealot, and Judas Iscariot, the one who betrayed Him.

## Matthew 10:5-15

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> These twelve Jesus sent out after instructing them: 'Do not go in [the] way of [the] Gentiles, and do not enter [any] city of the Samaritans;

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> but rather go to the lost sheep of the house of Israel.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'And as you go, preach, saying, 'The kingdom of heaven is at hand.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Heal [the] sick, raise [the] dead, cleanse [the] lepers, cast out demons. Freely you received, freely give.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Do not acquire gold, or silver, or copper for your money belts,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> or a bag for [your] journey, or even two coats, or sandals, or a staff; for the worker is worthy of his support.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'And whatever city or village you enter, inquire who is worthy in it, and stay at his house until you leave [that city].

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'As you enter the house, give it your greeting.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'If the house is worthy, give it your [blessing of] peace. But if it is not worthy, take back your [blessing of] peace.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Whoever does not receive you, nor heed your words, as you go out of that house or that city, shake the dust off your feet.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Truly I say to you, it will be more tolerable for [the] land of Sodom and Gomorrah in the day of judgment than for that city.

## Matthew 10:16-42

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'Behold, I send you out as sheep in the midst of wolves; so be shrewd as serpents and innocent as doves.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'But beware of men, for they will hand you over to [the] courts and scourge you in their synagogues;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> and you will even be brought before governors and kings for My sake, as a testimony to them and to the Gentiles.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'But when they hand you over, do not worry about how or what you are to say; for it will be given you in that hour what you are to say.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'For it is not you who speak, but [it is] the Spirit of your Father who speaks in you.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'Brother will betray brother to death, and a father [his] child; and children will rise up against parents and cause them to be put to death.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'You will be hated by all because of My name, but it is the one who has endured to the end who will be saved.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'But whenever they persecute you in one city, flee to the next; for truly I say to you, you will not finish [going through] the cities of Israel until the Son of Man comes.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'A disciple is not above his teacher, nor a slave above his master.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'It is enough for the disciple that he become like his teacher, and the slave like his master. If they have called the head of the house Beelzebul, how much more [will they malign] the members of his household!

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'Therefore do not fear them, for there is nothing concealed that will not be revealed, or hidden that will not be known.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'What I tell you in the darkness, speak in the light; and what you hear [whispered] in [your] ear, proclaim upon the housetops.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'Do not fear those who kill the body but are unable to kill the soul; but rather fear Him who is able to destroy both soul and body in hell.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'Are not two sparrows sold for a cent? And [yet] not one of them will fall to the ground apart from your Father.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> 'But the very hairs of your head are all numbered.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'So do not fear; you are more valuable than many sparrows.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'Therefore everyone who confesses Me before men, I will also confess him before My Father who is in heaven.

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> 'But whoever denies Me before men, I will also deny him before My Father who is in heaven.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> 'Do not think that I came to bring peace on the earth; I did not come to bring peace, but a sword.

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> 'For I came to SET A MAN AGAINST HIS FATHER, AND A DAUGHTER AGAINST HER MOTHER, AND A DAUGHTER-IN-LAW AGAINST HER MOTHER-IN-LAW;

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> and A MAN'S ENEMIES WILL BE THE MEMBERS OF HIS HOUSEHOLD.

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> 'He who loves father or mother more than Me is not worthy of Me; and he who loves son or daughter more than Me is not worthy of Me.

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> 'And he who does not take his cross and follow after Me is not worthy of Me.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> 'He who has found his life will lose it, and he who has lost his life for My sake will find it.

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> 'He who receives you receives Me, and he who receives Me receives Him who sent Me.

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> 'He who receives a prophet in [the] name of a prophet shall receive a prophet's reward; and he who receives a righteous man in the name of a righteous man shall receive a righteous man's reward.

###### v42
<sup>42</sup> 'And whoever in the name of a disciple gives to one of these little ones even a cup of cold water to drink, truly I say to you, he shall not lose his reward.'
