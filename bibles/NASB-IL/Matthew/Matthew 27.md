# Matthew 27

## Matthew 27:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now when morning came, all the chief priests and the elders of the people conferred together against Jesus to put Him to death;

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> and they bound Him, and led Him away and delivered Him to Pilate the governor.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Then when Judas, who had betrayed Him, saw that He had been condemned, he felt remorse and returned the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> saying, 'I have sinned by betraying innocent blood.' But they said, 'What is that to us? See [to that] yourself!'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> And he threw the pieces of silver into the temple sanctuary and departed; and he went away and hanged himself.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> The chief priests took the pieces of silver and said, 'It is not lawful to put them into the temple treasury, since it is the price of blood.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> And they conferred together and with the money bought the Potter's Field as a burial place for strangers.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> For this reason that field has been called the Field of Blood to this day.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Then that which was spoken through Jeremiah the prophet was fulfilled: 'AND THEY TOOK THE THIRTY PIECES OF SILVER, THE PRICE OF THE ONE WHOSE PRICE HAD BEEN SET by the sons of Israel;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> AND THEY GAVE THEM FOR THE POTTER'S FIELD, AS THE LORD DIRECTED ME.'

## Matthew 27:11-25

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Now Jesus stood before the governor, and the governor questioned Him, saying, 'Are You the King of the Jews?' And Jesus said to him, '[It is as] you say.'

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> And while He was being accused by the chief priests and elders, He did not answer.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Then Pilate said to Him, 'Do You not hear how many things they testify against You?'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And He did not answer him with regard to even a [single] charge, so the governor was quite amazed.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Now at [the] feast the governor was accustomed to release for the people [any] one prisoner whom they wanted.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> At that time they were holding a notorious prisoner, called Barabbas.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> So when the people gathered together, Pilate said to them, 'Whom do you want me to release for you? Barabbas, or Jesus who is called Christ?'

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> For he knew that because of envy they had handed Him over.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> While he was sitting on the judgment seat, his wife sent him [a message], saying, 'Have nothing to do with that righteous Man; for last night I suffered greatly in a dream because of Him.'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> But the chief priests and the elders persuaded the crowds to ask for Barabbas and to put Jesus to death.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> But the governor said to them, 'Which of the two do you want me to release for you?' And they said, 'Barabbas.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Pilate said to them, 'Then what shall I do with Jesus who is called Christ?' They all said, 'Crucify Him!'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> And he said, 'Why, what evil has He done?' But they kept shouting all the more, saying, 'Crucify Him!'

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> When Pilate saw that he was accomplishing nothing, but rather that a riot was starting, he took water and washed his hands in front of the crowd, saying, 'I am innocent of this Man's blood; see [to that] yourselves.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> And all the people said, 'His blood shall be on us and on our children!'
