# Matthew 9

## Matthew 9:1-8

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Getting into a boat, Jesus crossed over [the sea] and came to His own city.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And they brought to Him a paralytic lying on a bed. Seeing their faith, Jesus said to the paralytic, 'Take courage, son; your sins are forgiven.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And some of the scribes said to themselves, 'This [fellow] blasphemes.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> And Jesus knowing their thoughts said, 'Why are you thinking evil in your hearts?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Which is easier, to say, 'Your sins are forgiven,' or to say, 'Get up, and walk '?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'But so that you may know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins '-- then He said to the paralytic, 'Get up, pick up your bed and go home.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> And he got up and went home.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But when the crowds saw [this], they were awestruck, and glorified God, who had given such authority to men.

###### v11

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> But when Jesus heard [this], He said, '[It is] not those who are healthy who need a physician, but those who are sick.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'But go and learn what this means: 'I DESIRE COMPASSION, AND NOT SACRIFICE,' for I did not come to call the righteous, but sinners.'

###### v18

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> Jesus got up and [began] to follow him, and [so did] His disciples.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> And a woman who had been suffering from a hemorrhage for twelve years, came up behind Him and touched the fringe of His cloak;

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> for she was saying to herself, 'If I only touch His garment, I will get well.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But Jesus turning and seeing her said, 'Daughter, take courage; your faith has made you well.'

###### v23
