# Matthew 12

## Matthew 12:1-13

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> At that time Jesus went through the grainfields on the Sabbath, and His disciples became hungry and began to pick the heads [of grain] and eat.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> But when the Pharisees saw [this], they said to Him, 'Look, Your disciples do what is not lawful to do on a Sabbath.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> But He said to them, 'Have you not read what David did when he became hungry, he and his companions,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> how he entered the house of God, and they ate the consecrated bread, which was not lawful for him to eat nor for those with him, but for the priests alone?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Or have you not read in the Law, that on the Sabbath the priests in the temple break the Sabbath and are innocent?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'But I say to you that something greater than the temple is here.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'But if you had known what this means, 'I DESIRE COMPASSION, AND NOT A SACRIFICE,' you would not have condemned the innocent.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'For the Son of Man is Lord of the Sabbath.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Departing from there, He went into their synagogue.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> And a man [was there] whose hand was withered. And they questioned Jesus, asking, 'Is it lawful to heal on the Sabbath?'-- so that they might accuse Him.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> And He said to them, 'What man is there among you who has a sheep, and if it falls into a pit on the Sabbath, will he not take hold of it and lift it out?

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'How much more valuable then is a man than a sheep! So then, it is lawful to do good on the Sabbath.'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Then He said to the man, 'Stretch out your hand!' He stretched it out, and it was restored to normal, like the other.

## Matthew 12:14-21

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> But the Pharisees went out and conspired against Him, [as to] how they might destroy Him.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> But Jesus, aware of [this], withdrew from there. Many followed Him, and He healed them all,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> and warned them not to tell who He was.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> [This was] to fulfill what was spoken through Isaiah the prophet:

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'BEHOLD, MY SERVANT WHOM I HAVE CHOSEN; MY BELOVED IN WHOM MY SOUL is WELL-PLEASED; I WILL PUT MY SPIRIT UPON HIM, AND HE SHALL PROCLAIM JUSTICE TO THE GENTILES.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'HE WILL NOT QUARREL, NOR CRY OUT; NOR WILL ANYONE HEAR HIS VOICE IN THE STREETS.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'A BATTERED REED HE WILL NOT BREAK OFF, AND A SMOLDERING WICK HE WILL NOT PUT OUT, UNTIL HE LEADS JUSTICE TO VICTORY.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'AND IN HIS NAME THE GENTILES WILL HOPE.'

## Matthew 12:46-50

###### v46
<sup>46</sup> While He was still speaking to the crowds, behold, His mother and brothers were standing outside, seeking to speak to Him.

###### v47
<sup>47</sup> Someone said to Him, 'Behold, Your mother and Your brothers are standing outside seeking to speak to You.'

###### v48
<sup>48</sup> But Jesus answered the one who was telling Him and said, 'Who is My mother and who are My brothers?'

###### v49
<sup>49</sup> And stretching out His hand toward His disciples, He said, 'Behold My mother and My brothers!

###### v50
<sup>50</sup> 'For whoever does the will of My Father who is in heaven, he is My brother and sister and mother.'
