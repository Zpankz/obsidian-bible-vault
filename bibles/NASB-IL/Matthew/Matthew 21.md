# Matthew 21

## Matthew 21:1-11

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> When they had approached Jerusalem and had come to Bethphage, at the Mount of Olives, then Jesus sent two disciples,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> saying to them, 'Go into the village opposite you, and immediately you will find a donkey tied [there] and a colt with her; untie them and bring them to Me.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'If anyone says anything to you, you shall say, 'The Lord has need of them,' and immediately he will send them.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> This took place to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet:

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'SAY TO THE DAUGHTER OF ZION, 'BEHOLD YOUR KING IS COMING TO YOU, GENTLE, AND MOUNTED ON A DONKEY, EVEN ON A COLT, THE FOAL OF A BEAST OF BURDEN.''

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> The disciples went and did just as Jesus had instructed them,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> and brought the donkey and the colt, and laid their coats on them; and He sat on the coats.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Most of the crowd spread their coats in the road, and others were cutting branches from the trees and spreading them in the road.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> The crowds going ahead of Him, and those who followed, were shouting, 'Hosanna to the Son of David; BLESSED IS HE WHO COMES IN THE NAME OF THE LORD; Hosanna in the highest!'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> When He had entered Jerusalem, all the city was stirred, saying, 'Who is this?'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> And the crowds were saying, 'This is the prophet Jesus, from Nazareth in Galilee.'

## Matthew 21:12-17

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> And Jesus entered the temple and drove out all those who were buying and selling in the temple, and overturned the tables of the money changers and the seats of those who were selling doves.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> And He said to them, 'It is written, 'MY HOUSE SHALL BE CALLED A HOUSE OF PRAYER'; but you are making it a ROBBERS' DEN.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And [the] blind and [the] lame came to Him in the temple, and He healed them.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> But when the chief priests and the scribes saw the wonderful things that He had done, and the children who were shouting in the temple, 'Hosanna to the Son of David,' they became indignant

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> and said to Him, 'Do You hear what these [children] are saying?' And Jesus said to them, 'Yes; have you never read, 'OUT OF THE MOUTH OF INFANTS AND NURSING BABIES YOU HAVE PREPARED PRAISE FOR YOURSELF'?'

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> And He left them and went out of the city to Bethany, and spent the night there.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> 'When the harvest time approached, he sent his slaves to the vine-growers to receive his produce.

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> 'The vine-growers took his slaves and beat one, and killed another, and stoned a third.

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> 'Again he sent another group of slaves larger than the first; and they did the same thing to them.

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> 'But afterward he sent his son to them, saying, 'They will respect my son.'

###### v38

###### v41

###### v42

###### v43

###### v44
<sup>44</sup> 'And he who falls on this stone will be broken to pieces; but on whomever it falls, it will scatter him like dust.'

###### v45
<sup>45</sup> When the chief priests and the Pharisees heard His parables, they understood that He was speaking about them.

###### v46
