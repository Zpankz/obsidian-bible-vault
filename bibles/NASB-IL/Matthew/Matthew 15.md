# Matthew 15

## Matthew 15:1-9

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Then some Pharisees and scribes came to Jesus from Jerusalem and said,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Why do Your disciples break the tradition of the elders? For they do not wash their hands when they eat bread.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And He answered and said to them, 'Why do you yourselves transgress the commandment of God for the sake of your tradition?

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'For God said, 'HONOR YOUR FATHER AND MOTHER,' and, 'HE WHO SPEAKS EVIL OF FATHER OR MOTHER IS TO BE PUT TO DEATH.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'But you say, 'Whoever says to [his] father or mother, 'Whatever I have that would help you has been given [to God],'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> he is not to honor his father or his mother.' And [by this] you invalidated the word of God for the sake of your tradition.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'You hypocrites, rightly did Isaiah prophesy of you:

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'THIS PEOPLE HONORS ME WITH THEIR LIPS, BUT THEIR HEART IS FAR AWAY FROM ME.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'BUT IN VAIN DO THEY WORSHIP ME, TEACHING AS DOCTRINES THE PRECEPTS OF MEN.''

## Matthew 15:10-20

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> After Jesus called the crowd to Him, He said to them, 'Hear and understand.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> '[It is] not what enters into the mouth [that] defiles the man, but what proceeds out of the mouth, this defiles the man.'

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Then the disciples came and said to Him, 'Do You know that the Pharisees were offended when they heard this statement?'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> But He answered and said, 'Every plant which My heavenly Father did not plant shall be uprooted.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Let them alone; they are blind guides of the blind. And if a blind man guides a blind man, both will fall into a pit.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Peter said to Him, 'Explain the parable to us.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Jesus said, 'Are you still lacking in understanding also?

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Do you not understand that everything that goes into the mouth passes into the stomach, and is eliminated?

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'But the things that proceed out of the mouth come from the heart, and those defile the man.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'For out of the heart come evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, fornications, thefts, false witness, slanders.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'These are the things which defile the man; but to eat with unwashed hands does not defile the man.'

## Matthew 15:21-28

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> Jesus went away from there, and withdrew into the district of Tyre and Sidon.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> And a Canaanite woman from that region came out and [began] to cry out, saying, 'Have mercy on me, Lord, Son of David; my daughter is cruelly demon-possessed.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> But He did not answer her a word. And His disciples came and implored Him, saying, 'Send her away, because she keeps shouting at us.'

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> But He answered and said, 'I was sent only to the lost sheep of the house of Israel.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> But she came and [began] to bow down before Him, saying, 'Lord, help me!'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> And He answered and said, 'It is not good to take the children's bread and throw it to the dogs.'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> But she said, 'Yes, Lord; but even the dogs feed on the crumbs which fall from their masters' table.'

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> Then Jesus said to her, 'O woman, your faith is great; it shall be done for you as you wish.' And her daughter was healed at once.

## Matthew 15:29-39

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> Departing from there, Jesus went along by the Sea of Galilee, and having gone up on the mountain, He was sitting there.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> And large crowds came to Him, bringing with them [those who were] lame, crippled, blind, mute, and many others, and they laid them down at His feet; and He healed them.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> So the crowd marveled as they saw the mute speaking, the crippled restored, and the lame walking, and the blind seeing; and they glorified the God of Israel.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> And Jesus called His disciples to Him, and said, 'I feel compassion for the people, because they have remained with Me now three days and have nothing to eat; and I do not want to send them away hungry, for they might faint on the way.'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> The disciples said to Him, 'Where would we get so many loaves in [this] desolate place to satisfy such a large crowd?'

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> And Jesus said to them, 'How many loaves do you have?' And they said, 'Seven, and a few small fish.'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> And He directed the people to sit down on the ground;

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> and He took the seven loaves and the fish; and giving thanks, He broke them and started giving them to the disciples, and the disciples [gave them] to the people.

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> And they all ate and were satisfied, and they picked up what was left over of the broken pieces, seven large baskets full.

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> And those who ate were four thousand men, besides women and children.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> And sending away the crowds, Jesus got into the boat and came to the region of Magadan.
