# Romans 16

## Romans 16:1-16

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> I commend to you our sister Phoebe, who is a servant of the church which is at Cenchrea;

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> that you receive her in the Lord in a manner worthy of the saints, and that you help her in whatever matter she may have need of you; for she herself has also been a helper of many, and of myself as well.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Greet Prisca and Aquila, my fellow workers in Christ Jesus,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> who for my life risked their own necks, to whom not only do I give thanks, but also all the churches of the Gentiles;

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> also [greet] the church that is in their house. Greet Epaenetus, my beloved, who is the first convert to Christ from Asia.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Greet Mary, who has worked hard for you.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Greet Andronicus and Junias, my kinsmen and my fellow prisoners, who are outstanding among the apostles, who also were in Christ before me.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Greet Ampliatus, my beloved in the Lord.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Greet Urbanus, our fellow worker in Christ, and Stachys my beloved.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Greet Apelles, the approved in Christ. Greet those who are of the [household] of Aristobulus.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Greet Herodion, my kinsman. Greet those of the [household] of Narcissus, who are in the Lord.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Greet Tryphaena and Tryphosa, workers in the Lord. Greet Persis the beloved, who has worked hard in the Lord.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Greet Rufus, a choice man in the Lord, also his mother and mine.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> Greet Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermes, Patrobas, Hermas and the brethren with them.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Greet Philologus and Julia, Nereus and his sister, and Olympas, and all the saints who are with them.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Greet one another with a holy kiss. All the churches of Christ greet you.

## Romans 16:17-20

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Now I urge you, brethren, keep your eye on those who cause dissensions and hindrances contrary to the teaching which you learned, and turn away from them.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> For such men are slaves, not of our Lord Christ but of their own appetites; and by their smooth and flattering speech they deceive the hearts of the unsuspecting.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> For the report of your obedience has reached to all; therefore I am rejoicing over you, but I want you to be wise in what is good and innocent in what is evil.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> The God of peace will soon crush Satan under your feet. The grace of our Lord Jesus be with you.
