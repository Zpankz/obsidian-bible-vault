## Romans 9:1-5

<sup>1</sup> I am telling the truth in Christ, I am not lying, my conscience testifies with me in the Holy Spirit,

<sup>2</sup> that I have great sorrow and unceasing grief in my heart.

<sup>3</sup> For I could wish that I myself were accursed, [separated] from Christ for the sake of my brethren, my kinsmen according to the flesh,[^1]

<sup>4</sup> who are Israelites, to whom belongs the adoption as sons, and the glory and the covenants and the giving of the Law and the [temple] service and the promises,[^2][^3]

<sup>5</sup> whose are the fathers, and from whom is the Christ according to the flesh, who is over all, God blessed forever. Amen.[^4][^5][^6][^7]

## Romans 9:6-13

<sup>6</sup> But [it is] not as though the word of God has failed. For they are not all Israel who are [descended] from Israel;[^8][^9][^10]

<sup>7</sup> nor are they all children because they are Abraham's descendants, but: 'THROUGH ISAAC YOUR DESCENDANTS WILL BE NAMED.'[^11][^12]

<sup>8</sup> That is, it is not the children of the flesh who are children of God, but the children of the promise are regarded as descendants.[^13][^14][^15]

<sup>9</sup> For this is the word of promise: 'AT THIS TIME I WILL COME, AND SARAH SHALL HAVE A SON.'[^16][^17][^18]

<sup>10</sup> And not only this, but there was Rebekah also, when she had conceived [twins] by one man, our father Isaac;

<sup>11</sup> for though [the twins] were not yet born and had not done anything good or bad, so that God's purpose according to [His] choice would stand, not because of works but because of Him who calls,[^19][^20][^21]

<sup>12</sup> it was said to her, 'THE OLDER WILL SERVE THE YOUNGER.'[^22]

<sup>13</sup> Just as it is written, 'JACOB I LOVED, BUT ESAU I HATED.'[^23]

## Romans 9:14-24

<sup>14</sup> What shall we say then? There is no injustice with God, is there? May it never be![^24][^25]

<sup>15</sup> For He says to Moses, 'I WILL HAVE MERCY ON WHOM I HAVE MERCY, AND I WILL HAVE COMPASSION ON WHOM I HAVE COMPASSION.'[^26][^27][^28][^29][^30]

<sup>16</sup> So then it [does] not [depend] on the man who wills or the man who runs, but on God who has mercy.[^31][^32][^33][^34][^35]

<sup>17</sup> For the Scripture says to Pharaoh, 'FOR THIS VERY PURPOSE I RAISED YOU UP, TO DEMONSTRATE MY POWER IN YOU, AND THAT MY NAME MIGHT BE PROCLAIMED THROUGHOUT THE WHOLE EARTH.'[^36][^37]

<sup>18</sup> So then He has mercy on whom He desires, and He hardens whom He desires.[^38][^39]

<sup>19</sup> You will say to me then, 'Why does He still find fault? For who resists His will?'[^40]

<sup>20</sup> On the contrary, who are you, O man, who answers back to God? The thing molded will not say to the molder, 'Why did you make me like this,' will it?[^41][^42][^43]

<sup>21</sup> Or does not the potter have a right over the clay, to make from the same lump one vessel for honorable use and another for common use?[^44][^45][^46][^47][^48]

<sup>22</sup> What if God, although willing to demonstrate His wrath and to make His power known, endured with much patience vessels of wrath prepared for destruction?[^49][^50][^51][^52][^53]

<sup>23</sup> And [He did so] to make known the riches of His glory upon vessels of mercy, which He prepared beforehand for glory,

<sup>24</sup> [even] us, whom He also called, not from among Jews only, but also from among Gentiles.
