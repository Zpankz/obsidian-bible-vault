# Romans 9

## Romans 9:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> I am telling the truth in Christ, I am not lying, my conscience testifies with me in the Holy Spirit,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> that I have great sorrow and unceasing grief in my heart.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> For I could wish that I myself were accursed, [separated] from Christ for the sake of my brethren, my kinsmen according to the flesh,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> who are Israelites, to whom belongs the adoption as sons, and the glory and the covenants and the giving of the Law and the [temple] service and the promises,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> whose are the fathers, and from whom is the Christ according to the flesh, who is over all, God blessed forever. Amen.

## Romans 9:6-13

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But [it is] not as though the word of God has failed. For they are not all Israel who are [descended] from Israel;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> nor are they all children because they are Abraham's descendants, but: 'THROUGH ISAAC YOUR DESCENDANTS WILL BE NAMED.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> That is, it is not the children of the flesh who are children of God, but the children of the promise are regarded as descendants.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> For this is the word of promise: 'AT THIS TIME I WILL COME, AND SARAH SHALL HAVE A SON.'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> And not only this, but there was Rebekah also, when she had conceived [twins] by one man, our father Isaac;

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> for though [the twins] were not yet born and had not done anything good or bad, so that God's purpose according to [His] choice would stand, not because of works but because of Him who calls,

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> it was said to her, 'THE OLDER WILL SERVE THE YOUNGER.'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Just as it is written, 'JACOB I LOVED, BUT ESAU I HATED.'

## Romans 9:14-24

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> What shall we say then? There is no injustice with God, is there? May it never be!

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> For He says to Moses, 'I WILL HAVE MERCY ON WHOM I HAVE MERCY, AND I WILL HAVE COMPASSION ON WHOM I HAVE COMPASSION.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> So then it [does] not [depend] on the man who wills or the man who runs, but on God who has mercy.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> For the Scripture says to Pharaoh, 'FOR THIS VERY PURPOSE I RAISED YOU UP, TO DEMONSTRATE MY POWER IN YOU, AND THAT MY NAME MIGHT BE PROCLAIMED THROUGHOUT THE WHOLE EARTH.'

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> So then He has mercy on whom He desires, and He hardens whom He desires.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> You will say to me then, 'Why does He still find fault? For who resists His will?'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> On the contrary, who are you, O man, who answers back to God? The thing molded will not say to the molder, 'Why did you make me like this,' will it?

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> Or does not the potter have a right over the clay, to make from the same lump one vessel for honorable use and another for common use?

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> What if God, although willing to demonstrate His wrath and to make His power known, endured with much patience vessels of wrath prepared for destruction?

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> And [He did so] to make known the riches of His glory upon vessels of mercy, which He prepared beforehand for glory,

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> [even] us, whom He also called, not from among Jews only, but also from among Gentiles.
