# Romans 11

## Romans 11:1-32

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> I say then, God has not rejected His people, has He? May it never be! For I too am an Israelite, a descendant of Abraham, of the tribe of Benjamin.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> God has not rejected His people whom He foreknew. Or do you not know what the Scripture says in [the passage about] Elijah, how he pleads with God against Israel?

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Lord, THEY HAVE KILLED YOUR PROPHETS, THEY HAVE TORN DOWN YOUR ALTARS, AND I ALONE AM LEFT, AND THEY ARE SEEKING MY LIFE.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> But what is the divine response to him? 'I HAVE KEPT for Myself SEVEN THOUSAND MEN WHO HAVE NOT BOWED THE KNEE TO BAAL.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> In the same way then, there has also come to be at the present time a remnant according to [God's] gracious choice.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But if it is by grace, it is no longer on the basis of works, otherwise grace is no longer grace.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> What then? What Israel is seeking, it has not obtained, but those who were chosen obtained it, and the rest were hardened;

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> just as it is written, 'GOD GAVE THEM A SPIRIT OF STUPOR, EYES TO SEE NOT AND EARS TO HEAR NOT, DOWN TO THIS VERY DAY.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> And David says, 'LET THEIR TABLE BECOME A SNARE AND A TRAP, AND A STUMBLING BLOCK AND A RETRIBUTION TO THEM.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'LET THEIR EYES BE DARKENED TO SEE NOT, AND BEND THEIR BACKS FOREVER.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> I say then, they did not stumble so as to fall, did they? May it never be! But by their transgression salvation [has come] to the Gentiles, to make them jealous.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Now if their transgression is riches for the world and their failure is riches for the Gentiles, how much more will their fulfillment be!

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> But I am speaking to you who are Gentiles. Inasmuch then as I am an apostle of Gentiles, I magnify my ministry,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> if somehow I might move to jealousy my fellow countrymen and save some of them.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> For if their rejection is the reconciliation of the world, what will [their] acceptance be but life from the dead?

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> If the first piece [of dough] is holy, the lump is also; and if the root is holy, the branches are too.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> But if some of the branches were broken off, and you, being a wild olive, were grafted in among them and became partaker with them of the rich root of the olive tree,

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> do not be arrogant toward the branches; but if you are arrogant, [remember that] it is not you who supports the root, but the root [supports] you.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> You will say then, 'Branches were broken off so that I might be grafted in.'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Quite right, they were broken off for their unbelief, but you stand by your faith. Do not be conceited, but fear;

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> for if God did not spare the natural branches, He will not spare you, either.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Behold then the kindness and severity of God; to those who fell, severity, but to you, God’s kindness, if you continue in His kindness; otherwise you also will be cut off.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> And they also, if they do not continue in their unbelief, will be grafted in, for God is able to graft them in again.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> For if you were cut off from what is by nature a wild olive tree, and were grafted contrary to nature into a cultivated olive tree, how much more will these who are the natural [branches] be grafted into their own olive tree?

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> For I do not want you, brethren, to be uninformed of this mystery-- so that you will not be wise in your own estimation-- that a partial hardening has happened to Israel until the fullness of the Gentiles has come in;

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> and so all Israel will be saved; just as it is written, 'THE DELIVERER WILL COME FROM ZION, HE WILL REMOVE UNGODLINESS FROM JACOB.'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'THIS IS MY COVENANT WITH THEM, WHEN I TAKE AWAY THEIR SINS.'

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> From the standpoint of the gospel they are enemies for your sake, but from the standpoint of [God's] choice they are beloved for the sake of the fathers;

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> for the gifts and the calling of God are irrevocable.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> For just as you once were disobedient to God, but now have been shown mercy because of their disobedience,

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> so these also now have been disobedient, that because of the mercy shown to you they also may now be shown mercy.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> For God has shut up all in disobedience so that He may show mercy to all.
