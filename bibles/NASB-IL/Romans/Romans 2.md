# Romans 2

## Romans 2:1-16

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Therefore you have no excuse, everyone of you who passes judgment, for in that which you judge another, you condemn yourself; for you who judge practice the same things.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And we know that the judgment of God rightly falls upon those who practice such things.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> But do you suppose this, O man, when you pass judgment on those who practice such things and do the same [yourself], that you will escape the judgment of God?

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Or do you think lightly of the riches of His kindness and tolerance and patience, not knowing that the kindness of God leads you to repentance?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> But because of your stubbornness and unrepentant heart you are storing up wrath for yourself in the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> who WILL RENDER TO EACH PERSON ACCORDING TO HIS DEEDS:

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> to those who by perseverance in doing good seek for glory and honor and immortality, eternal life;

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> but to those who are selfishly ambitious and do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, wrath and indignation.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> [There will be] tribulation and distress for every soul of man who does evil, of the Jew first and also of the Greek,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> but glory and honor and peace to everyone who does good, to the Jew first and also to the Greek.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For there is no partiality with God.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> For all who have sinned without the Law will also perish without the Law, and all who have sinned under the Law will be judged by the Law;

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> for [it is] not the hearers of the Law [who] are just before God, but the doers of the Law will be justified.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> For when Gentiles who do not have the Law do instinctively the things of the Law, these, not having the Law, are a law to themselves,

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> in that they show the work of the Law written in their hearts, their conscience bearing witness and their thoughts alternately accusing or else defending them,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> on the day when, according to my gospel, God will judge the secrets of men through Christ Jesus.

## Romans 2:17-29

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> But if you bear the name 'Jew ' and rely upon the Law and boast in God,

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> and know [His] will and approve the things that are essential, being instructed out of the Law,

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> and are confident that you yourself are a guide to the blind, a light to those who are in darkness,

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> a corrector of the foolish, a teacher of the immature, having in the Law the embodiment of knowledge and of the truth,

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> you, therefore, who teach another, do you not teach yourself? You who preach that one shall not steal, do you steal?

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> You who say that one should not commit adultery, do you commit adultery? You who abhor idols, do you rob temples?

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> You who boast in the Law, through your breaking the Law, do you dishonor God?

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> For 'THE NAME OF GOD IS BLASPHEMED AMONG THE GENTILES BECAUSE OF YOU,' just as it is written.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> For indeed circumcision is of value if you practice the Law; but if you are a transgressor of the Law, your circumcision has become uncircumcision.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> So if the uncircumcised man keeps the requirements of the Law, will not his uncircumcision be regarded as circumcision?

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> And he who is physically uncircumcised, if he keeps the Law, will he not judge you who though having the letter [of the Law] and circumcision are a transgressor of the Law?

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> For he is not a Jew who is one outwardly, nor is circumcision that which is outward in the flesh.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> But he is a Jew who is one inwardly; and circumcision is that which is of the heart, by the Spirit, not by the letter; and his praise is not from men, but from God.
