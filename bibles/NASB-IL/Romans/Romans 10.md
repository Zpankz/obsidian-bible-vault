# Romans 10

## Romans 10:1-11

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Brethren, my heart's desire and my prayer to God for them is for [their] salvation.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For I testify about them that they have a zeal for God, but not in accordance with knowledge.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> For not knowing about God's righteousness and seeking to establish their own, they did not subject themselves to the righteousness of God.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> For Christ is the end of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> For Moses writes that the man who practices the righteousness which is based on law shall live by that righteousness.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But the righteousness based on faith speaks as follows: 'DO NOT SAY IN YOUR HEART, 'WHO WILL ASCEND INTO HEAVEN?' (that is, to bring Christ down),

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> or 'WHO WILL DESCEND INTO THE ABYSS?' (that is, to bring Christ up from the dead).'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But what does it say? 'THE WORD IS NEAR YOU, IN YOUR MOUTH AND IN YOUR HEART '-- that is, the word of faith which we are preaching,

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> that if you confess with your mouth Jesus [as] Lord, and believe in your heart that God raised Him from the dead, you will be saved;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> for with the heart a person believes, resulting in righteousness, and with the mouth he confesses, resulting in salvation.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For the Scripture says, 'WHOEVER BELIEVES IN HIM WILL NOT BE DISAPPOINTED.'

## Romans 10:12-21

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> For there is no distinction between Jew and Greek; for the same [Lord] is Lord of all, abounding in riches for all who call on Him;

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> for 'WHOEVER WILL CALL ON THE NAME OF THE LORD WILL BE SAVED.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> How then will they call on Him in whom they have not believed? How will they believe in Him whom they have not heard? And how will they hear without a preacher?

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> How will they preach unless they are sent? Just as it is written, 'HOW BEAUTIFUL ARE THE FEET OF THOSE WHO BRING GOOD NEWS OF GOOD THINGS!'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> However, they did not all heed the good news; for Isaiah says, 'LORD, WHO HAS BELIEVED OUR REPORT?'

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> So faith [comes] from hearing, and hearing by the word of Christ.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> But I say, surely they have never heard, have they? Indeed they have; 'THEIR VOICE HAS GONE OUT INTO ALL THE EARTH, AND THEIR WORDS TO THE ENDS OF THE WORLD.'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> But I say, surely Israel did not know, did they? First Moses says, 'I WILL MAKE YOU JEALOUS BY THAT WHICH IS NOT A NATION, BY A NATION WITHOUT UNDERSTANDING WILL I ANGER YOU.'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> And Isaiah is very bold and says, 'I WAS FOUND BY THOSE WHO DID NOT SEEK ME, I BECAME MANIFEST TO THOSE WHO DID NOT ASK FOR ME.'

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> But as for Israel He says, 'ALL THE DAY LONG I HAVE STRETCHED OUT MY HANDS TO A DISOBEDIENT AND OBSTINATE PEOPLE.'
