# Genesis 25

## Genesis 25:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now Abraham took another wife, whose name was Keturah.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> She bore to him Zimran and Jokshan and Medan and Midian and Ishbak and Shuah.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Jokshan became the father of Sheba and Dedan. And the sons of Dedan were Asshurim and Letushim and Leummim.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> The sons of Midian [were] Ephah and Epher and Hanoch and Abida and Eldaah. All these [were] the sons of Keturah.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Now Abraham gave all that he had to Isaac;

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> but to the sons of his concubines, Abraham gave gifts while he was still living, and sent them away from his son Isaac eastward, to the land of the east.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> These are all the years of Abraham's life that he lived, one hundred and seventy-five years.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Abraham breathed his last and died in a ripe old age, an old man and satisfied [with life]; and he was gathered to his people.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Then his sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him in the cave of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron the son of Zohar the Hittite, facing Mamre,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> the field which Abraham purchased from the sons of Heth; there Abraham was buried with Sarah his wife.

## Genesis 25:11-18

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> It came about after the death of Abraham, that God blessed his son Isaac; and Isaac lived by Beer-lahai-roi.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Now these are [the records of] the generations of Ishmael, Abraham's son, whom Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah's maid, bore to Abraham;

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> and these are the names of the sons of Ishmael, by their names, in the order of their birth: Nebaioth, the firstborn of Ishmael, and Kedar and Adbeel and Mibsam

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> and Mishma and Dumah and Massa,

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Hadad and Tema, Jetur, Naphish and Kedemah.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> These are the sons of Ishmael and these are their names, by their villages, and by their camps; twelve princes according to their tribes.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> These are the years of the life of Ishmael, one hundred and thirty-seven years; and he breathed his last and died, and was gathered to his people.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> They settled from Havilah to Shur which is east of Egypt as one goes toward Assyria; he settled in defiance of all his relatives.

## Genesis 25:19-28

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> Now these are [the records of] the generations of Isaac, Abraham's son: Abraham became the father of Isaac;

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> and Isaac was forty years old when he took Rebekah, the daughter of Bethuel the Aramean of Paddan-aram, the sister of Laban the Aramean, to be his wife.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> Isaac prayed to the LORD on behalf of his wife, because she was barren; and the LORD answered him and Rebekah his wife conceived.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But the children struggled together within her; and she said, 'If it is so, why then am I [this way]?' So she went to inquire of the LORD.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> The LORD said to her, 'Two nations are in your womb; And two peoples will be separated from your body; And one people shall be stronger than the other; And the older shall serve the younger.'

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> When her days to be delivered were fulfilled, behold, there were twins in her womb.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> Now the first came forth red, all over like a hairy garment; and they named him Esau.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> Afterward his brother came forth with his hand holding on to Esau's heel, so his name was called Jacob; and Isaac was sixty years old when she gave birth to them.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> When the boys grew up, Esau became a skillful hunter, a man of the field, but Jacob was a peaceful man, living in tents.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> Now Isaac loved Esau, because he had a taste for game, but Rebekah loved Jacob.

## Genesis 25:29-34

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> When Jacob had cooked stew, Esau came in from the field and he was famished;

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> and Esau said to Jacob, 'Please let me have a swallow of that red stuff there, for I am famished.' Therefore his name was called Edom.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> But Jacob said, 'First sell me your birthright.'

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> Esau said, 'Behold, I am about to die; so of what [use] then is the birthright to me?'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> And Jacob said, 'First swear to me'; so he swore to him, and sold his birthright to Jacob.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> Then Jacob gave Esau bread and lentil stew; and he ate and drank, and rose and went on his way. Thus Esau despised his birthright.
