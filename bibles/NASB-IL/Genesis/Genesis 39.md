# Genesis 39

## Genesis 39:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now Joseph had been taken down to Egypt; and Potiphar, an Egyptian officer of Pharaoh, the captain of the bodyguard, bought him from the Ishmaelites, who had taken him down there.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> The LORD was with Joseph, so he became a successful man. And he was in the house of his master, the Egyptian.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Now his master saw that the LORD was with him and [how] the LORD caused all that he did to prosper in his hand.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> So Joseph found favor in his sight and became his personal servant; and he made him overseer over his house, and all that he owned he put in his charge.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> It came about that from the time he made him overseer in his house and over all that he owned, the LORD blessed the Egyptian's house on account of Joseph; thus the LORD'S blessing was upon all that he owned, in the house and in the field.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> So he left everything he owned in Joseph's charge; and with him [there] he did not concern himself with anything except the food which he ate. Now Joseph was handsome in form and appearance.

## Genesis 39:7-12

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> It came about after these events that his master's wife looked with desire at Joseph, and she said, 'Lie with me.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But he refused and said to his master's wife, 'Behold, with me [here], my master does not concern himself with anything in the house, and he has put all that he owns in my charge.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'There is no one greater in this house than I, and he has withheld nothing from me except you, because you are his wife. How then could I do this great evil and sin against God?'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> As she spoke to Joseph day after day, he did not listen to her to lie beside her [or] be with her.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Now it happened one day that he went into the house to do his work, and none of the men of the household was there inside.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> She caught him by his garment, saying, 'Lie with me!' And he left his garment in her hand and fled, and went outside.

## Genesis 39:13-18

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> When she saw that he had left his garment in her hand and had fled outside,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> she called to the men of her household and said to them, 'See, he has brought in a Hebrew to us to make sport of us; he came in to me to lie with me, and I screamed.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'When he heard that I raised my voice and screamed, he left his garment beside me and fled and went outside.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> So she left his garment beside her until his master came home.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> Then she spoke to him with these words, 'The Hebrew slave, whom you brought to us, came in to me to make sport of me;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> and as I raised my voice and screamed, he left his garment beside me and fled outside.'

## Genesis 39:19-23

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> Now when his master heard the words of his wife, which she spoke to him, saying, 'This is what your slave did to me,' his anger burned.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> So Joseph's master took him and put him into the jail, the place where the king's prisoners were confined; and he was there in the jail.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> But the LORD was with Joseph and extended kindness to him, and gave him favor in the sight of the chief jailer.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> The chief jailer committed to Joseph's charge all the prisoners who were in the jail; so that whatever was done there, he was responsible [for it].

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> The chief jailer did not supervise anything under Joseph's charge because the LORD was with him; and whatever he did, the LORD made to prosper.
