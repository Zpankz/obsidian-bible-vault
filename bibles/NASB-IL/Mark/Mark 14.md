# Mark 14

## Mark 14:1-11

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now the Passover and Unleavened Bread were two days away; and the chief priests and the scribes were seeking how to seize Him by stealth and kill [Him];

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> for they were saying, 'Not during the festival, otherwise there might be a riot of the people.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> While He was in Bethany at the home of Simon the leper, and reclining [at the table], there came a woman with an alabaster vial of very costly perfume of pure nard; [and] she broke the vial and poured it over His head.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> But some were indignantly [remarking] to one another, 'Why has this perfume been wasted?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'For this perfume might have been sold for over three hundred denarii, and [the money] given to the poor.' And they were scolding her.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But Jesus said, 'Let her alone; why do you bother her? She has done a good deed to Me.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'For you always have the poor with you, and whenever you wish you can do good to them; but you do not always have Me.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'She has done what she could; she has anointed My body beforehand for the burial.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Truly I say to you, wherever the gospel is preached in the whole world, what this woman has done will also be spoken of in memory of her.'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Then Judas Iscariot, who was one of the twelve, went off to the chief priests in order to betray Him to them.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> They were glad when they heard [this], and promised to give him money. And he [began] seeking how to betray Him at an opportune time.

## Mark 14:12-31

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> On the first day of Unleavened Bread, when the Passover [lamb] was being sacrificed, His disciples said to Him, 'Where do You want us to go and prepare for You to eat the Passover?'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> And He sent two of His disciples and said to them, 'Go into the city, and a man will meet you carrying a pitcher of water; follow him;

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> and wherever he enters, say to the owner of the house, 'The Teacher says, 'Where is My guest room in which I may eat the Passover with My disciples?''

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'And he himself will show you a large upper room furnished [and] ready; prepare for us there.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> The disciples went out and came to the city, and found [it] just as He had told them; and they prepared the Passover.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> When it was evening He came with the twelve.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> As they were reclining [at the table] and eating, Jesus said, 'Truly I say to you that one of you will betray Me-- one who is eating with Me.'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> They began to be grieved and to say to Him one by one, 'Surely not I?'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> And He said to them, '[It is] one of the twelve, one who dips with Me in the bowl.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'For the Son of Man [is to] go just as it is written of Him; but woe to that man by whom the Son of Man is betrayed! [It would have been] good for that man if he had not been born.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> While they were eating, He took [some] bread, and after a blessing He broke [it], and gave [it] to them, and said, 'Take [it]; this is My body.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> And when He had taken a cup [and] given thanks, He gave [it] to them, and they all drank from it.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> And He said to them, 'This is My blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'Truly I say to you, I will never again drink of the fruit of the vine until that day when I drink it new in the kingdom of God.'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> After singing a hymn, they went out to the Mount of Olives.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> And Jesus said to them, 'You will all fall away, because it is written, 'I WILL STRIKE DOWN THE SHEPHERD, AND THE SHEEP SHALL BE SCATTERED.'

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'But after I have been raised, I will go ahead of you to Galilee.'

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> But Peter said to Him, '[Even] though all may fall away, yet I will not.'

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> And Jesus said to him, 'Truly I say to you, that this very night, before a rooster crows twice, you yourself will deny Me three times.'

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> But [Peter] kept saying insistently, '[Even] if I have to die with You, I will not deny You!' And they all were saying the same thing also.

## Mark 14:32-42

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> They came to a place named Gethsemane; and He said to His disciples, 'Sit here until I have prayed.'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> And He took with Him Peter and James and John, and began to be very distressed and troubled.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> And He said to them, 'My soul is deeply grieved to the point of death; remain here and keep watch.'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> And He went a little beyond [them], and fell to the ground and [began] to pray that if it were possible, the hour might pass Him by.

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> And He was saying, 'Abba! Father! All things are possible for You; remove this cup from Me; yet not what I will, but what You will.'

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> And He came and found them sleeping, and said to Peter, 'Simon, are you asleep? Could you not keep watch for one hour?

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> 'Keep watching and praying that you may not come into temptation; the spirit is willing, but the flesh is weak.'

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> Again He went away and prayed, saying the same words.

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> And again He came and found them sleeping, for their eyes were very heavy; and they did not know what to answer Him.

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> And He came the third time, and said to them, 'Are you still sleeping and resting? It is enough; the hour has come; behold, the Son of Man is being betrayed into the hands of sinners.

###### v42
<sup>42</sup> 'Get up, let us be going; behold, the one who betrays Me is at hand!'

## Mark 14:66-72

###### v66
<sup>66</sup> As Peter was below in the courtyard, one of the servant-girls of the high priest came,

###### v67
<sup>67</sup> and seeing Peter warming himself, she looked at him and said, 'You also were with Jesus the Nazarene.'

###### v68
<sup>68</sup> But he denied [it], saying, 'I neither know nor understand what you are talking about.' And he went out onto the porch, and a rooster crowed.

###### v69
<sup>69</sup> The servant-girl saw him, and began once more to say to the bystanders, 'This is [one] of them!'

###### v70
<sup>70</sup> But again he denied it. And after a little while the bystanders were again saying to Peter, 'Surely you are [one] of them, for you are a Galilean too.'

###### v71
<sup>71</sup> But he began to curse and swear, 'I do not know this man you are talking about!'

###### v72
<sup>72</sup> Immediately a rooster crowed a second time. And Peter remembered how Jesus had made the remark to him, 'Before a rooster crows twice, you will deny Me three times.' And he began to weep.
