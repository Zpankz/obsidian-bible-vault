# Mark 15

## Mark 15:1-14

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Early in the morning the chief priests with the elders and scribes and the whole Council, immediately held a consultation; and binding Jesus, they led Him away and delivered Him to Pilate.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Pilate questioned Him, 'Are You the King of the Jews?' And He answered him, '[It is as] you say.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> The chief priests [began] to accuse Him harshly.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Then Pilate questioned Him again, saying, 'Do You not answer? See how many charges they bring against You!'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> But Jesus made no further answer; so Pilate was amazed.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Now at [the] feast he used to release for them [any] one prisoner whom they requested.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> The man named Barabbas had been imprisoned with the insurrectionists who had committed murder in the insurrection.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> The crowd went up and began asking him [to] [do] as he had been accustomed to do for them.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Pilate answered them, saying, 'Do you want me to release for you the King of the Jews?'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For he was aware that the chief priests had handed Him over because of envy.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> But the chief priests stirred up the crowd [to ask] him to release Barabbas for them instead.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Answering again, Pilate said to them, 'Then what shall I do with Him whom you call the King of the Jews?'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> They shouted back, 'Crucify Him!'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> But Pilate said to them, 'Why, what evil has He done?' But they shouted all the more, 'Crucify Him!'

## Mark 15:15-21

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Wishing to satisfy the crowd, Pilate released Barabbas for them, and after having Jesus scourged, he handed Him over to be crucified.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> The soldiers took Him away into the palace (that is, the Praetorium), and they called together the whole [Roman] cohort.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> They dressed Him up in purple, and after twisting a crown of thorns, they put it on Him;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> and they began to acclaim Him, 'Hail, King of the Jews!'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> They kept beating His head with a reed, and spitting on Him, and kneeling and bowing before Him.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> After they had mocked Him, they took the purple robe off Him and put His [own] garments on Him. And they led Him out to crucify Him.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> They pressed into service a passer-by coming from the country, Simon of Cyrene (the father of Alexander and Rufus), to bear His cross.

## Mark 15:22-32

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Then they brought Him to the place Golgotha, which is translated, Place of a Skull.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> They tried to give Him wine mixed with myrrh; but He did not take it.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> And they crucified Him, and divided up His garments among themselves, casting lots for them [to decide] what each man should take.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> It was the third hour when they crucified Him.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> The inscription of the charge against Him read, 'THE KING OF THE JEWS.'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> They crucified two robbers with Him, one on His right and one on His left.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> [And the Scripture was fulfilled which says, 'And He was numbered with transgressors.]'

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> Those passing by were hurling abuse at Him, wagging their heads, and saying, 'Ha! You who [are] [going to] destroy the temple and rebuild it in three days,

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> save Yourself, and come down from the cross!'

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> In the same way the chief priests also, along with the scribes, were mocking [Him] among themselves and saying, 'He saved others; He cannot save Himself.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'Let [this] Christ, the King of Israel, now come down from the cross, so that we may see and believe!' Those who were crucified with Him were also insulting Him.

## Mark 15:33-41

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> When the sixth hour came, darkness fell over the whole land until the ninth hour.

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> At the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, 'ELOI, ELOI, LAMA SABACHTHANI?' which is translated, 'MY GOD, MY GOD, WHY HAVE YOU FORSAKEN ME?'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> When some of the bystanders heard it, they [began] saying, 'Behold, He is calling for Elijah.'

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> Someone ran and filled a sponge with sour wine, put it on a reed, and gave Him a drink, saying, 'Let us see whether Elijah will come to take Him down.'

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> And Jesus uttered a loud cry, and breathed His last.

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> And the veil of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> When the centurion, who was standing right in front of Him, saw the way He breathed His last, he said, 'Truly this man was the Son of God!'

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> There were also [some] women looking on from a distance, among whom [were] Mary Magdalene, and Mary the mother of James the Less and Joses, and Salome.

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> When He was in Galilee, they used to follow Him and minister to Him; and [there were] many other women who came up with Him to Jerusalem.
