## Ephesians 5:3-20

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> But immorality or any impurity or greed must not even be named among you, as is proper among saints;

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> and [there must be no] filthiness and silly talk, or coarse jesting, which are not fitting, but rather giving of thanks.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> For this you know with certainty, that no immoral or impure person or covetous man, who is an idolater, has an inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and God.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Let no one deceive you with empty words, for because of these things the wrath of God comes upon the sons of disobedience.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Therefore do not be partakers with them;

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> for you were formerly darkness, but now you are Light in the Lord; walk as children of Light

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> (for the fruit of the Light [consists] in all goodness and righteousness and truth),

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> trying to learn what is pleasing to the Lord.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Do not participate in the unfruitful deeds of darkness, but instead even expose them;

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> for it is disgraceful even to speak of the things which are done by them in secret.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> But all things become visible when they are exposed by the light, for everything that becomes visible is light.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> For this reason it says, 'Awake, sleeper, And arise from the dead, And Christ will shine on you.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Therefore be careful how you walk, not as unwise men but as wise,

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> making the most of your time, because the days are evil.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> So then do not be foolish, but understand what the will of the Lord is.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> And do not get drunk with wine, for that is dissipation, but be filled with the Spirit,

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> speaking to one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody with your heart to the Lord;

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> always giving thanks for all things in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ to God, even the Father;

## Ephesians 5:21-33

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> and be subject to one another in the fear of Christ.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Wives, [be subject] to your own husbands, as to the Lord.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> For the husband is the head of the wife, as Christ also is the head of the church, He Himself [being] the Savior of the body.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> But as the church is subject to Christ, so also the wives [ought to be] to their husbands in everything.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ also loved the church and gave Himself up for her,

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> so that He might sanctify her, having cleansed her by the washing of water with the word,

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> that He might present to Himself the church in all her glory, having no spot or wrinkle or any such thing; but that she would be holy and blameless.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> So husbands ought also to love their own wives as their own bodies. He who loves his own wife loves himself;

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> for no one ever hated his own flesh, but nourishes and cherishes it, just as Christ also [does] the church,

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> because we are members of His body.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> FOR THIS REASON A MAN SHALL LEAVE HIS FATHER AND MOTHER AND SHALL BE JOINED TO HIS WIFE, AND THE TWO SHALL BECOME ONE FLESH.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> This mystery is great; but I am speaking with reference to Christ and the church.

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> Nevertheless, each individual among you also is to love his own wife even as himself, and the wife must [see to it] that she respects her husband.
