# Luke 22

## Luke 22:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now the Feast of Unleavened Bread, which is called the Passover, was approaching.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> The chief priests and the scribes were seeking how they might put Him to death; for they were afraid of the people.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And Satan entered into Judas who was called Iscariot, belonging to the number of the twelve.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> And he went away and discussed with the chief priests and officers how he might betray Him to them.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> They were glad and agreed to give him money.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> So he consented, and [began] seeking a good opportunity to betray Him to them apart from the crowd.

## Luke 22:7-20

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Then came the [first] day of Unleavened Bread on which the Passover [lamb] had to be sacrificed.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> And Jesus sent Peter and John, saying, 'Go and prepare the Passover for us, so that we may eat it.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> They said to Him, 'Where do You want us to prepare it?'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> And He said to them, 'When you have entered the city, a man will meet you carrying a pitcher of water; follow him into the house that he enters.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'And you shall say to the owner of the house, 'The Teacher says to you, 'Where is the guest room in which I may eat the Passover with My disciples?''

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'And he will show you a large, furnished upper room; prepare it there.'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> And they left and found [everything] just as He had told them; and they prepared the Passover.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> When the hour had come, He reclined [at the table], and the apostles with Him.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> And He said to them, 'I have earnestly desired to eat this Passover with you before I suffer;

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> for I say to you, I shall never again eat it until it is fulfilled in the kingdom of God.'

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> And when He had taken a cup [and] given thanks, He said, 'Take this and share it among yourselves;

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> for I say to you, I will not drink of the fruit of the vine from now on until the kingdom of God comes.'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> And when He had taken [some] bread [and] given thanks, He broke it and gave it to them, saying, 'This is My body which is given for you; do this in remembrance of Me.'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> And in the same way [He took] the cup after they had eaten, saying, 'This cup which is poured out for you is the new covenant in My blood.

## Luke 22:21-38

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'But behold, the hand of the one betraying Me is with Mine on the table.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'For indeed, the Son of Man is going as it has been determined; but woe to that man by whom He is betrayed!'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> And they began to discuss among themselves which one of them it might be who was going to do this thing.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> And there arose also a dispute among them [as to] which one of them was regarded to be greatest.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> And He said to them, 'The kings of the Gentiles lord it over them; and those who have authority over them are called 'Benefactors.'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'But [it is] not this way with you, but the one who is the greatest among you must become like the youngest, and the leader like the servant.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'For who is greater, the one who reclines [at the table] or the one who serves? Is it not the one who reclines [at the table]? But I am among you as the one who serves.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'You are those who have stood by Me in My trials;

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> and just as My Father has granted Me a kingdom, I grant you

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> that you may eat and drink at My table in My kingdom, and you will sit on thrones judging the twelve tribes of Israel.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'Simon, Simon, behold, Satan has demanded [permission] to sift you like wheat;

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> but I have prayed for you, that your faith may not fail; and you, when once you have turned again, strengthen your brothers.'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> But he said to Him, 'Lord, with You I am ready to go both to prison and to death!'

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> And He said, 'I say to you, Peter, the rooster will not crow today until you have denied three times that you know Me.'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> And He said to them, 'When I sent you out without money belt and bag and sandals, you did not lack anything, did you?' They said, '[No], nothing.'

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> And He said to them, 'But now, whoever has a money belt is to take it along, likewise also a bag, and whoever has no sword is to sell his coat and buy one.

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> 'For I tell you that this which is written must be fulfilled in Me, 'AND HE WAS NUMBERED WITH TRANSGRESSORS'; for that which refers to Me has [its] fulfillment.'

## Luke 22:39-46

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> And He came out and proceeded as was His custom to the Mount of Olives; and the disciples also followed Him.

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> When He arrived at the place, He said to them, 'Pray that you may not enter into temptation.'

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> And He withdrew from them about a stone's throw, and He knelt down and [began] to pray,

###### v42
<sup>42</sup> saying, 'Father, if You are willing, remove this cup from Me; yet not My will, but Yours be done.'

###### v43
<sup>43</sup> Now an angel from heaven appeared to Him, strengthening Him.

###### v44
<sup>44</sup> And being in agony He was praying very fervently; and His sweat became like drops of blood, falling down upon the ground.

###### v45
<sup>45</sup> When He rose from prayer, He came to the disciples and found them sleeping from sorrow,

###### v46
<sup>46</sup> and said to them, 'Why are you sleeping? Get up and pray that you may not enter into temptation.'
