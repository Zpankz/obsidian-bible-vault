# Luke 24

## Luke 24:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> But on the first day of the week, at early dawn, they came to the tomb bringing the spices which they had prepared.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And they found the stone rolled away from the tomb,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> but when they entered, they did not find the body of the Lord Jesus.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> While they were perplexed about this, behold, two men suddenly stood near them in dazzling clothing;

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> and as [the women] were terrified and bowed their faces to the ground, [the men] said to them, 'Why do you seek the living One among the dead?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'He is not here, but He has risen. Remember how He spoke to you while He was still in Galilee,

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> saying that the Son of Man must be delivered into the hands of sinful men, and be crucified, and the third day rise again.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> And they remembered His words,

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> and returned from the tomb and reported all these things to the eleven and to all the rest.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Now they were Mary Magdalene and Joanna and Mary the [mother] of James; also the other women with them were telling these things to the apostles.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> But these words appeared to them as nonsense, and they would not believe them.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> But Peter got up and ran to the tomb; stooping and looking in, he saw the linen wrappings only; and he went away to his home, marveling at what had happened.

## Luke 24:13-35

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> And behold, two of them were going that very day to a village named Emmaus, which was about seven miles from Jerusalem.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And they were talking with each other about all these things which had taken place.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> While they were talking and discussing, Jesus Himself approached and [began] traveling with them.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> But their eyes were prevented from recognizing Him.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> And He said to them, 'What are these words that you are exchanging with one another as you are walking?' And they stood still, looking sad.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> One [of them], named Cleopas, answered and said to Him, 'Are You the only one visiting Jerusalem and unaware of the things which have happened here in these days?'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> And He said to them, 'What things?' And they said to Him, 'The things about Jesus the Nazarene, who was a prophet mighty in deed and word in the sight of God and all the people,

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> and how the chief priests and our rulers delivered Him to the sentence of death, and crucified Him.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'But we were hoping that it was He who was going to redeem Israel. Indeed, besides all this, it is the third day since these things happened.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'But also some women among us amazed us. When they were at the tomb early in the morning,

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> and did not find His body, they came, saying that they had also seen a vision of angels who said that He was alive.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Some of those who were with us went to the tomb and found it just exactly as the women also had said; but Him they did not see.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> And He said to them, 'O foolish men and slow of heart to believe in all that the prophets have spoken!

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'Was it not necessary for the Christ to suffer these things and to enter into His glory?'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> Then beginning with Moses and with all the prophets, He explained to them the things concerning Himself in all the Scriptures.

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> And they approached the village where they were going, and He acted as though He were going farther.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> But they urged Him, saying, 'Stay with us, for it is [getting] toward evening, and the day is now nearly over.' So He went in to stay with them.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> When He had reclined [at the table] with them, He took the bread and blessed [it], and breaking [it], He [began] giving [it] to them.

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> Then their eyes were opened and they recognized Him; and He vanished from their sight.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> They said to one another, 'Were not our hearts burning within us while He was speaking to us on the road, while He was explaining the Scriptures to us?'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> And they got up that very hour and returned to Jerusalem, and found gathered together the eleven and those who were with them,

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> saying, 'The Lord has really risen and has appeared to Simon.'

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> They [began] to relate their experiences on the road and how He was recognized by them in the breaking of the bread.

## Luke 24:36-49

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> While they were telling these things, He Himself stood in their midst and said to them, 'Peace be to you.'

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> But they were startled and frightened and thought that they were seeing a spirit.

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> And He said to them, 'Why are you troubled, and why do doubts arise in your hearts?

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> 'See My hands and My feet, that it is I Myself; touch Me and see, for a spirit does not have flesh and bones as you see that I have.'

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> And when He had said this, He showed them His hands and His feet.

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> While they still could not believe [it] because of their joy and amazement, He said to them, 'Have you anything here to eat?'

###### v42
<sup>42</sup> They gave Him a piece of a broiled fish;

###### v43
<sup>43</sup> and He took it and ate [it] before them.

###### v44
<sup>44</sup> Now He said to them, 'These are My words which I spoke to you while I was still with you, that all things which are written about Me in the Law of Moses and the Prophets and the Psalms must be fulfilled.'

###### v45
<sup>45</sup> Then He opened their minds to understand the Scriptures,

###### v46
<sup>46</sup> and He said to them, 'Thus it is written, that the Christ would suffer and rise again from the dead the third day,

###### v47
<sup>47</sup> and that repentance for forgiveness of sins would be proclaimed in His name to all the nations, beginning from Jerusalem.

###### v48
<sup>48</sup> 'You are witnesses of these things.

###### v49
<sup>49</sup> 'And behold, I am sending forth the promise of My Father upon you; but you are to stay in the city until you are clothed with power from on high.'
