# Luke 5

## Luke 5:1-11

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now it happened that while the crowd was pressing around Him and listening to the word of God, He was standing by the lake of Gennesaret;

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> and He saw two boats lying at the edge of the lake; but the fishermen had gotten out of them and were washing their nets.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And He got into one of the boats, which was Simon's, and asked him to put out a little way from the land. And He sat down and [began] teaching the people from the boat.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> When He had finished speaking, He said to Simon, 'Put out into the deep water and let down your nets for a catch.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Simon answered and said, 'Master, we worked hard all night and caught nothing, but I will do as You say [and] let down the nets.'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> When they had done this, they enclosed a great quantity of fish, and their nets [began] to break;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> so they signaled to their partners in the other boat for them to come and help them. And they came and filled both of the boats, so that they began to sink.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But when Simon Peter saw [that], he fell down at Jesus' feet, saying, 'Go away from me Lord, for I am a sinful man, O Lord!'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> For amazement had seized him and all his companions because of the catch of fish which they had taken;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> and so also [were] James and John, sons of Zebedee, who were partners with Simon. And Jesus said to Simon, 'Do not fear, from now on you will be catching men.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> When they had brought their boats to land, they left everything and followed Him.

## Luke 5:17-26

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> One day He was teaching; and there were [some] Pharisees and teachers of the law sitting [there], who had come from every village of Galilee and Judea and [from] Jerusalem; and the power of the Lord was [present] for Him to perform healing.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> And [some] men [were] carrying on a bed a man who was paralyzed; and they were trying to bring him in and to set him down in front of Him.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> But not finding any [way] to bring him in because of the crowd, they went up on the roof and let him down through the tiles with his stretcher, into the middle [of the crowd], in front of Jesus.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Seeing their faith, He said, 'Friend, your sins are forgiven you.'

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> The scribes and the Pharisees began to reason, saying, 'Who is this [man] who speaks blasphemies? Who can forgive sins, but God alone?'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But Jesus, aware of their reasonings, answered and said to them, 'Why are you reasoning in your hearts?

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'Which is easier, to say, 'Your sins have been forgiven you,' or to say, 'Get up and walk '?

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'But, so that you may know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins,'-- He said to the paralytic-- 'I say to you, get up, and pick up your stretcher and go home.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> Immediately he got up before them, and picked up what he had been lying on, and went home glorifying God.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> They were all struck with astonishment and [began] glorifying God; and they were filled with fear, saying, 'We have seen remarkable things today.'

## Luke 5:27-39

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> After that He went out and noticed a tax collector named Levi sitting in the tax booth, and He said to him, 'Follow Me.'

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> And he left everything behind, and got up and [began] to follow Him.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> And Levi gave a big reception for Him in his house; and there was a great crowd of tax collectors and other [people] who were reclining [at the table] with them.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> The Pharisees and their scribes [began] grumbling at His disciples, saying, 'Why do you eat and drink with the tax collectors and sinners?'

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> And Jesus answered and said to them, '[It is] not those who are well who need a physician, but those who are sick.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'I have not come to call the righteous but sinners to repentance.'

###### v33
<sup>33</sup> And they said to Him, 'The disciples of John often fast and offer prayers, the [disciples] of the Pharisees also do the same, but Yours eat and drink.'

###### v34
<sup>34</sup> And Jesus said to them, 'You cannot make the attendants of the bridegroom fast while the bridegroom is with them, can you?

###### v35
<sup>35</sup> 'But [the] days will come; and when the bridegroom is taken away from them, then they will fast in those days.'

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> And He was also telling them a parable: 'No one tears a piece of cloth from a new garment and puts it on an old garment; otherwise he will both tear the new, and the piece from the new will not match the old.

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> 'And no one puts new wine into old wineskins; otherwise the new wine will burst the skins and it will be spilled out, and the skins will be ruined.

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> 'But new wine must be put into fresh wineskins.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> 'And no one, after drinking old [wine] wishes for new; for he says, 'The old is good [enough].''
