# Luke 7

## Luke 7:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> When He had completed all His discourse in the hearing of the people, He went to Capernaum.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And a centurion's slave, who was highly regarded by him, was sick and about to die.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> When he heard about Jesus, he sent some Jewish elders asking Him to come and save the life of his slave.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> When they came to Jesus, they earnestly implored Him, saying, 'He is worthy for You to grant this to him;

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> for he loves our nation and it was he who built us our synagogue.'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Now Jesus [started] on His way with them; and when He was not far from the house, the centurion sent friends, saying to Him, 'Lord, do not trouble Yourself further, for I am not worthy for You to come under my roof;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> for this reason I did not even consider myself worthy to come to You, but [just] say the word, and my servant will be healed.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'For I also am a man placed under authority, with soldiers under me; and I say to this one, 'Go!' and he goes, and to another, 'Come!' and he comes, and to my slave, 'Do this!' and he does it.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Now when Jesus heard this, He marveled at him, and turned and said to the crowd that was following Him, 'I say to you, not even in Israel have I found such great faith.'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> When those who had been sent returned to the house, they found the slave in good health.

## Luke 7:11-18

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Soon afterwards He went to a city called Nain; and His disciples were going along with Him, accompanied by a large crowd.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Now as He approached the gate of the city, a dead man was being carried out, the only son of his mother, and she was a widow; and a sizeable crowd from the city was with her.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> When the Lord saw her, He felt compassion for her, and said to her, 'Do not weep.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And He came up and touched the coffin; and the bearers came to a halt. And He said, 'Young man, I say to you, arise!'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> The dead man sat up and began to speak. And [Jesus] gave him back to his mother.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Fear gripped them all, and they [began] glorifying God, saying, 'A great prophet has arisen among us!' and, 'God has visited His people!'

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> This report concerning Him went out all over Judea and in all the surrounding district.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> The disciples of John reported to him about all these things.

###### v19

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> When the men came to Him, they said, 'John the Baptist has sent us to You, to ask, 'Are You the Expected One, or do we look for someone else?''

###### v21

## Luke 7:36-50

###### v36
<sup>36</sup> Now one of the Pharisees was requesting Him to dine with him, and He entered the Pharisee's house and reclined [at the table].

###### v37
<sup>37</sup> And there was a woman in the city who was a sinner; and when she learned that He was reclining [at the table] in the Pharisee's house, she brought an alabaster vial of perfume,

###### v38
<sup>38</sup> and standing behind [Him] at His feet, weeping, she began to wet His feet with her tears, and kept wiping them with the hair of her head, and kissing His feet and anointing them with the perfume.

###### v39
<sup>39</sup> Now when the Pharisee who had invited Him saw this, he said to himself, 'If this man were a prophet He would know who and what sort of person this woman is who is touching Him, that she is a sinner.'

###### v40
<sup>40</sup> And Jesus answered him, 'Simon, I have something to say to you.' And he replied, 'Say it, Teacher.'

###### v41
<sup>41</sup> 'A moneylender had two debtors: one owed five hundred denarii, and the other fifty.

###### v42
<sup>42</sup> 'When they were unable to repay, he graciously forgave them both. So which of them will love him more?'

###### v43
<sup>43</sup> Simon answered and said, 'I suppose the one whom he forgave more.' And He said to him, 'You have judged correctly.'

###### v44
<sup>44</sup> Turning toward the woman, He said to Simon, 'Do you see this woman? I entered your house; you gave Me no water for My feet, but she has wet My feet with her tears and wiped them with her hair.

###### v45
<sup>45</sup> 'You gave Me no kiss; but she, since the time I came in, has not ceased to kiss My feet.

###### v46
<sup>46</sup> 'You did not anoint My head with oil, but she anointed My feet with perfume.

###### v47
<sup>47</sup> 'For this reason I say to you, her sins, which are many, have been forgiven, for she loved much; but he who is forgiven little, loves little.'

###### v48
<sup>48</sup> Then He said to her, 'Your sins have been forgiven.'

###### v49
<sup>49</sup> Those who were reclining [at the table] with Him began to say to themselves, 'Who is this [man] who even forgives sins?'

###### v50
<sup>50</sup> And He said to the woman, 'Your faith has saved you; go in peace.'
