# Luke 15

## Luke 15:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now all the tax collectors and the sinners were coming near Him to listen to Him.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Both the Pharisees and the scribes [began] to grumble, saying, 'This man receives sinners and eats with them.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> So He told them this parable, saying,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'What man among you, if he has a hundred sheep and has lost one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the open pasture and go after the one which is lost until he finds it?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'When he has found it, he lays it on his shoulders, rejoicing.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'And when he comes home, he calls together his friends and his neighbors, saying to them, 'Rejoice with me, for I have found my sheep which was lost!'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'I tell you that in the same way, there will be [more] joy in heaven over one sinner who repents than over ninety-nine righteous persons who need no repentance.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Or what woman, if she has ten silver coins and loses one coin, does not light a lamp and sweep the house and search carefully until she finds it?

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'When she has found it, she calls together her friends and neighbors, saying, 'Rejoice with me, for I have found the coin which I had lost!'

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'In the same way, I tell you, there is joy in the presence of the angels of God over one sinner who repents.'

## Luke 15:11-32

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> And He said, 'A man had two sons.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'The younger of them said to his father, 'Father, give me the share of the estate that falls to me.' So he divided his wealth between them.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'And not many days later, the younger son gathered everything together and went on a journey into a distant country, and there he squandered his estate with loose living.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Now when he had spent everything, a severe famine occurred in that country, and he began to be impoverished.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'So he went and hired himself out to one of the citizens of that country, and he sent him into his fields to feed swine.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'And he would have gladly filled his stomach with the pods that the swine were eating, and no one was giving [anything] to him.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'But when he came to his senses, he said, 'How many of my father's hired men have more than enough bread, but I am dying here with hunger!

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'I will get up and go to my father, and will say to him, 'Father, I have sinned against heaven, and in your sight;

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> I am no longer worthy to be called your son; make me as one of your hired men.''

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'So he got up and came to his father. But while he was still a long way off, his father saw him and felt compassion [for him], and ran and embraced him and kissed him.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'And the son said to him, 'Father, I have sinned against heaven and in your sight; I am no longer worthy to be called your son.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'But the father said to his slaves, 'Quickly bring out the best robe and put it on him, and put a ring on his hand and sandals on his feet;

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> and bring the fattened calf, kill it, and let us eat and celebrate;

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> for this son of mine was dead and has come to life again; he was lost and has been found.' And they began to celebrate.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'Now his older son was in the field, and when he came and approached the house, he heard music and dancing.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'And he summoned one of the servants and [began] inquiring what these things could be.

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> 'And he said to him, 'Your brother has come, and your father has killed the fattened calf because he has received him back safe and sound.'

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> 'But he became angry and was not willing to go in; and his father came out and [began] pleading with him.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> 'But he answered and said to his father, 'Look! For so many years I have been serving you and I have never neglected a command of yours; and [yet] you have never given me a young goat, so that I might celebrate with my friends;

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> but when this son of yours came, who has devoured your wealth with prostitutes, you killed the fattened calf for him.'

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> 'And he said to him, 'Son, you have always been with me, and all that is mine is yours.

###### v32
<sup>32</sup> 'But we had to celebrate and rejoice, for this brother of yours was dead and [has begun] to live, and [was] lost and has been found.''
