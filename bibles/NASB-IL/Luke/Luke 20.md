# Luke 20

## Luke 20:1-8

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> On one of the days while He was teaching the people in the temple and preaching the gospel, the chief priests and the scribes with the elders confronted [Him],

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> and they spoke, saying to Him, 'Tell us by what authority You are doing these things, or who is the one who gave You this authority?'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Jesus answered and said to them, 'I will also ask you a question, and you tell Me:

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Was the baptism of John from heaven or from men?'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> They reasoned among themselves, saying, 'If we say, 'From heaven,' He will say, 'Why did you not believe him?'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'But if we say, 'From men,' all the people will stone us to death, for they are convinced that John was a prophet.'

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> So they answered that they did not know where [it came] from.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> And Jesus said to them, 'Nor will I tell you by what authority I do these things.'

## Luke 20:9-19

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> And He began to tell the people this parable: 'A man planted a vineyard and rented it out to vine-growers, and went on a journey for a long time.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'At the [harvest] time he sent a slave to the vine-growers, so that they would give him [some] of the produce of the vineyard; but the vine-growers beat him and sent him away empty-handed.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'And he proceeded to send another slave; and they beat him also and treated him shamefully and sent him away empty-handed.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'And he proceeded to send a third; and this one also they wounded and cast out.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'The owner of the vineyard said, 'What shall I do? I will send my beloved son; perhaps they will respect him.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'But when the vine-growers saw him, they reasoned with one another, saying, 'This is the heir; let us kill him so that the inheritance will be ours.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'So they threw him out of the vineyard and killed him. What, then, will the owner of the vineyard do to them?

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'He will come and destroy these vine-growers and will give the vineyard to others.' When they heard it, they said, 'May it never be!'

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> But Jesus looked at them and said, 'What then is this that is written: 'THE STONE WHICH THE BUILDERS REJECTED, THIS BECAME THE CHIEF CORNER [stone]'?

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'Everyone who falls on that stone will be broken to pieces; but on whomever it falls, it will scatter him like dust.'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> The scribes and the chief priests tried to lay hands on Him that very hour, and they feared the people; for they understood that He spoke this parable against them.

## Luke 20:20-26

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> So they watched Him, and sent spies who pretended to be righteous, in order that they might catch Him in some statement, so that they [could] deliver Him to the rule and the authority of the governor.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> They questioned Him, saying, 'Teacher, we know that You speak and teach correctly, and You are not partial to any, but teach the way of God in truth.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'Is it lawful for us to pay taxes to Caesar, or not?'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> But He detected their trickery and said to them,

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'Show Me a denarius. Whose likeness and inscription does it have?' They said, 'Caesar's.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> And He said to them, 'Then render to Caesar the things that are Caesar's, and to God the things that are God's.'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> And they were unable to catch Him in a saying in the presence of the people; and being amazed at His answer, they became silent.
