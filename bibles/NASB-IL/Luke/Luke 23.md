# Luke 23

## Luke 23:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Then the whole body of them got up and brought Him before Pilate.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And they began to accuse Him, saying, 'We found this man misleading our nation and forbidding to pay taxes to Caesar, and saying that He Himself is Christ, a King.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> So Pilate asked Him, saying, 'Are You the King of the Jews?' And He answered him and said, '[It is as] you say.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Then Pilate said to the chief priests and the crowds, 'I find no guilt in this man.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> But they kept on insisting, saying, 'He stirs up the people, teaching all over Judea, starting from Galilee even as far as this place.'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> When Pilate heard it, he asked whether the man was a Galilean.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> And when he learned that He belonged to Herod's jurisdiction, he sent Him to Herod, who himself also was in Jerusalem at that time.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Now Herod was very glad when he saw Jesus; for he had wanted to see Him for a long time, because he had been hearing about Him and was hoping to see some sign performed by Him.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> And he questioned Him at some length; but He answered him nothing.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> And the chief priests and the scribes were standing there, accusing Him vehemently.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> And Herod with his soldiers, after treating Him with contempt and mocking Him, dressed Him in a gorgeous robe and sent Him back to Pilate.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Now Herod and Pilate became friends with one another that very day; for before they had been enemies with each other.

## Luke 23:13-25

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Pilate summoned the chief priests and the rulers and the people,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> and said to them, 'You brought this man to me as one who incites the people to rebellion, and behold, having examined Him before you, I have found no guilt in this man regarding the charges which you make against Him.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'No, nor has Herod, for he sent Him back to us; and behold, nothing deserving death has been done by Him.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> 'Therefore I will punish Him and release Him.'

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> [Now he was obliged to release to them at the feast one prisoner].

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> But they cried out all together, saying, 'Away with this man, and release for us Barabbas!'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> (He was one who had been thrown into prison for an insurrection made in the city, and for murder.)

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Pilate, wanting to release Jesus, addressed them again,

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> but they kept on calling out, saying, 'Crucify, crucify Him!'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> And he said to them the third time, 'Why, what evil has this man done? I have found in Him no guilt [demanding] death; therefore I will punish Him and release Him.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> But they were insistent, with loud voices asking that He be crucified. And their voices [began] to prevail.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> And Pilate pronounced sentence that their demand be granted.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> And he released the man they were asking for who had been thrown into prison for insurrection and murder, but he delivered Jesus to their will.

## Luke 23:50-56

###### v50
<sup>50</sup> And a man named Joseph, who was a member of the Council, a good and righteous man

###### v51
<sup>51</sup> (he had not consented to their plan and action), [a man] from Arimathea, a city of the Jews, who was waiting for the kingdom of God;

###### v52
<sup>52</sup> this man went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus.

###### v53
<sup>53</sup> And he took it down and wrapped it in a linen cloth, and laid Him in a tomb cut into the rock, where no one had ever lain.

###### v54
<sup>54</sup> It was the preparation day, and the Sabbath was about to begin.

###### v55
<sup>55</sup> Now the women who had come with Him out of Galilee followed, and saw the tomb and how His body was laid.

###### v56
<sup>56</sup> Then they returned and prepared spices and perfumes. And on the Sabbath they rested according to the commandment.
