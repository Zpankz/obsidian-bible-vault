# Luke 18

## Luke 18:1-8

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now He was telling them a parable to show that at all times they ought to pray and not to lose heart,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> saying, 'In a certain city there was a judge who did not fear God and did not respect man.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'There was a widow in that city, and she kept coming to him, saying, 'Give me legal protection from my opponent.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'For a while he was unwilling; but afterward he said to himself, 'Even though I do not fear God nor respect man,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> yet because this widow bothers me, I will give her legal protection, otherwise by continually coming she will wear me out.''

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> And the Lord said, 'Hear what the unrighteous judge said;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> now, will not God bring about justice for His elect who cry to Him day and night, and will He delay long over them?

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'I tell you that He will bring about justice for them quickly. However, when the Son of Man comes, will He find faith on the earth?'

## Luke 18:9-14

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> And He also told this parable to some people who trusted in themselves that they were righteous, and viewed others with contempt:

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'Two men went up into the temple to pray, one a Pharisee and the other a tax collector.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'The Pharisee stood and was praying this to himself: 'God, I thank You that I am not like other people: swindlers, unjust, adulterers, or even like this tax collector.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'I fast twice a week; I pay tithes of all that I get.'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'But the tax collector, standing some distance away, was even unwilling to lift up his eyes to heaven, but was beating his breast, saying, 'God, be merciful to me, the sinner!'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'I tell you, this man went to his house justified rather than the other; for everyone who exalts himself will be humbled, but he who humbles himself will be exalted.'

## Luke 18:18-30

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> A ruler questioned Him, saying, 'Good Teacher, what shall I do to inherit eternal life?'

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> And Jesus said to him, 'Why do you call Me good? No one is good except God alone.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'You know the commandments, 'DO NOT COMMIT ADULTERY, DO NOT MURDER, DO NOT STEAL, DO NOT BEAR FALSE WITNESS, HONOR YOUR FATHER AND MOTHER.''

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> And he said, 'All these things I have kept from [my] youth.'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> When Jesus heard [this], He said to him, 'One thing you still lack; sell all that you possess and distribute it to the poor, and you shall have treasure in heaven; and come, follow Me.'

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> But when he had heard these things, he became very sad, for he was extremely rich.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> And Jesus looked at him and said, 'How hard it is for those who are wealthy to enter the kingdom of God!

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'For it is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich man to enter the kingdom of God.'

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> They who heard it said, 'Then who can be saved?'

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> But He said, 'The things that are impossible with people are possible with God.'

###### v28
