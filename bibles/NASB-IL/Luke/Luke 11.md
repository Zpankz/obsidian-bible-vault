# Luke 11

## Luke 11:1-13

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> It happened that while Jesus was praying in a certain place, after He had finished, one of His disciples said to Him, 'Lord, teach us to pray just as John also taught his disciples.'

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> And He said to them, 'When you pray, say: 'Father, hallowed be Your name. Your kingdom come.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Give us each day our daily bread.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'And forgive us our sins, For we ourselves also forgive everyone who is indebted to us. And lead us not into temptation.''

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Then He said to them, 'Suppose one of you has a friend, and goes to him at midnight and says to him, 'Friend, lend me three loaves;

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> for a friend of mine has come to me from a journey, and I have nothing to set before him';

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> and from inside he answers and says, 'Do not bother me; the door has already been shut and my children and I are in bed; I cannot get up and give you [anything].'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'I tell you, even though he will not get up and give him [anything] because he is his friend, yet because of his persistence he will get up and give him as much as he needs.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'So I say to you, ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and it will be opened to you.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'For everyone who asks, receives; and he who seeks, finds; and to him who knocks, it will be opened.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'Now suppose one of you fathers is asked by his son for a fish; he will not give him a snake instead of a fish, will he?

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Or [if] he is asked for an egg, he will not give him a scorpion, will he?

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'If you then, being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will [your] heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to those who ask Him?'

## Luke 11:14-26

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And He was casting out a demon, and it was mute; when the demon had gone out, the mute man spoke; and the crowds were amazed.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> But some of them said, 'He casts out demons by Beelzebul, the ruler of the demons.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Others, to test [Him], were demanding of Him a sign from heaven.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> But He knew their thoughts and said to them, 'Any kingdom divided against itself is laid waste; and a house [divided] against itself falls.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> 'If Satan also is divided against himself, how will his kingdom stand? For you say that I cast out demons by Beelzebul.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> 'And if I by Beelzebul cast out demons, by whom do your sons cast them out? So they will be your judges.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> 'But if I cast out demons by the finger of God, then the kingdom of God has come upon you.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'When a strong [man], fully armed, guards his own house, his possessions are undisturbed.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> 'But when someone stronger than he attacks him and overpowers him, he takes away from him all his armor on which he had relied and distributes his plunder.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> 'He who is not with Me is against Me; and he who does not gather with Me, scatters.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> 'When the unclean spirit goes out of a man, it passes through waterless places seeking rest, and not finding any, it says, 'I will return to my house from which I came.'

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'And when it comes, it finds it swept and put in order.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> 'Then it goes and takes [along] seven other spirits more evil than itself, and they go in and live there; and the last state of that man becomes worse than the first.'
