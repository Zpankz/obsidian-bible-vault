## Isaiah 32:1-8

<sup>1</sup> Behold, a king will reign righteously And princes will rule justly.

<sup>2</sup> Each will be like a refuge from the wind And a shelter from the storm, Like streams of water in a dry country, Like the shade of a huge rock in a parched land.

<sup>3</sup> Then the eyes of those who see will not be blinded, And the ears of those who hear will listen.

<sup>4</sup> The mind of the hasty will discern the truth, And the tongue of the stammerers will hasten to speak clearly.

<sup>5</sup> No longer will the fool be called noble, Or the rogue be spoken of [as] generous.

<sup>6</sup> For a fool speaks nonsense, And his heart inclines toward wickedness: To practice ungodliness and to speak error against the LORD, To keep the hungry person unsatisfied And to withhold drink from the thirsty.

<sup>7</sup> As for a rogue, his weapons are evil; He devises wicked schemes To destroy [the] afflicted with slander, Even though [the] needy one speaks what is right.

<sup>8</sup> But the noble man devises noble plans; And by noble plans he stands.

## Isaiah 32:9-20

<sup>9</sup> Rise up, you women who are at ease, [And] hear my voice; Give ear to my word, You complacent daughters.

<sup>10</sup> Within a year and [a few] days You will be troubled, O complacent [daughters]; For the vintage is ended, [And] the [fruit] gathering will not come.

<sup>11</sup> Tremble, you [women] who are at ease; Be troubled, you complacent [daughters]; Strip, undress and put [sackcloth] on [your] waist,

<sup>12</sup> Beat your breasts for the pleasant fields, for the fruitful vine,

<sup>13</sup> For the land of my people [in which] thorns [and] briars shall come up; Yea, for all the joyful houses [and for] the jubilant city.

<sup>14</sup> Because the palace has been abandoned, the populated city forsaken. Hill and watch-tower have become caves forever, A delight for wild donkeys, a pasture for flocks;

<sup>15</sup> Until the Spirit is poured out upon us from on high, And the wilderness becomes a fertile field, And the fertile field is considered as a forest.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7]

<sup>16</sup> Then justice will dwell in the wilderness And righteousness will abide in the fertile field.

<sup>17</sup> And the work of righteousness will be peace, And the service of righteousness, quietness and confidence forever.[^8][^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14]

<sup>18</sup> Then my people will live in a peaceful habitation, And in secure dwellings and in undisturbed resting places;[^15]

<sup>19</sup> And it will hail when the forest comes down, And the city will be utterly laid low.

<sup>20</sup> How blessed will you be, you who sow beside all waters, Who let out freely the ox and the donkey.
