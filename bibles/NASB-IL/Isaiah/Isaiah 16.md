# Isaiah 16

## Isaiah 16:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Send the [tribute] lamb to the ruler of the land, From Sela by way of the wilderness to the mountain of the daughter of Zion.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Then, like fleeing birds [or] scattered nestlings, The daughters of Moab will be at the fords of the Arnon.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Give [us] advice, make a decision; Cast your shadow like night at high noon; Hide the outcasts, do not betray the fugitive.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Let the outcasts of Moab stay with you; Be a hiding place to them from the destroyer.' For the extortioner has come to an end, destruction has ceased, Oppressors have completely [disappeared] from the land.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> A throne will even be established in lovingkindness, And a judge will sit on it in faithfulness in the tent of David; Moreover, he will seek justice And be prompt in righteousness.

## Isaiah 16:6-14

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> We have heard of the pride of Moab, an excessive pride; [Even] of his arrogance, pride, and fury; His idle boasts are false.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Therefore Moab will wail; everyone of Moab will wail. You will moan for the raisin cakes of Kir-hareseth As those who are utterly stricken.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> For the fields of Heshbon have withered, the vines of Sibmah [as well]; The lords of the nations have trampled down its choice clusters Which reached as far as Jazer [and] wandered to the deserts; Its tendrils spread themselves out [and] passed over the sea.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Therefore I will weep bitterly for Jazer, for the vine of Sibmah; I will drench you with my tears, O Heshbon and Elealeh; For the shouting over your summer fruits and your harvest has fallen away.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> Gladness and joy are taken away from the fruitful field; In the vineyards also there will be no cries of joy or jubilant shouting, No treader treads out wine in the presses, [For] I have made the shouting to cease.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Therefore my heart intones like a harp for Moab And my inward feelings for Kir-hareseth.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> So it will come about when Moab presents himself, When he wearies himself upon [his] high place And comes to his sanctuary to pray, That he will not prevail.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> This is the word which the LORD spoke earlier concerning Moab.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> But now the LORD speaks, saying, 'Within three years, as a hired man would count them, the glory of Moab will be degraded along with all [his] great population, and [his] remnant will be very small [and] impotent.'
