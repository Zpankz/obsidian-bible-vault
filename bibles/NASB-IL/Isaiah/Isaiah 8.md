# Isaiah 8

## Isaiah 8:1-8

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Then the LORD said to me, 'Take for yourself a large tablet and write on it in ordinary letters: Swift is the booty, speedy is the prey.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'And I will take to Myself faithful witnesses for testimony, Uriah the priest and Zechariah the son of Jeberechiah.'

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> So I approached the prophetess, and she conceived and gave birth to a son. Then the LORD said to me, 'Name him Maher-shalal-hash-baz;

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> for before the boy knows how to cry out 'My father ' or 'My mother,' the wealth of Damascus and the spoil of Samaria will be carried away before the king of Assyria.'

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Again the LORD spoke to me further, saying,

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Inasmuch as these people have rejected the gently flowing waters of Shiloah And rejoice in Rezin and the son of Remaliah;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'Now therefore, behold, the Lord is about to bring on them the strong and abundant waters of the Euphrates, [Even] the king of Assyria and all his glory; And it will rise up over all its channels and go over all its banks.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Then it will sweep on into Judah, it will overflow and pass through, It will reach even to the neck; And the spread of its wings will fill the breadth of your land, O Immanuel.

## Isaiah 8:9-15

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'Be broken, O peoples, and be shattered; And give ear, all remote places of the earth. Gird yourselves, yet be shattered; Gird yourselves, yet be shattered.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'Devise a plan, but it will be thwarted; State a proposal, but it will not stand, For God is with us.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For thus the LORD spoke to me with mighty power and instructed me not to walk in the way of this people, saying,

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'You are not to say, '[It is] a conspiracy!' In regard to all that this people call a conspiracy, And you are not to fear what they fear or be in dread of [it].

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'It is the LORD of hosts whom you should regard as holy. And He shall be your fear, And He shall be your dread.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Then He shall become a sanctuary; But to both the houses of Israel, a stone to strike and a rock to stumble over, [And] a snare and a trap for the inhabitants of Jerusalem.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'Many will stumble over them, Then they will fall and be broken; They will even be snared and caught.'

## Isaiah 8:16-22

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Bind up the testimony, seal the law among my disciples.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> And I will wait for the LORD who is hiding His face from the house of Jacob; I will even look eagerly for Him.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> Behold, I and the children whom the LORD has given me are for signs and wonders in Israel from the LORD of hosts, who dwells on Mount Zion.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> When they say to you, 'Consult the mediums and the spiritists who whisper and mutter,' should not a people consult their God? [Should they] [consult] the dead on behalf of the living?

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> To the law and to the testimony! If they do not speak according to this word, it is because they have no dawn.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> They will pass through the land hard-pressed and famished, and it will turn out that when they are hungry, they will be enraged and curse their king and their God as they face upward.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> Then they will look to the earth, and behold, distress and darkness, the gloom of anguish; and [they will be] driven away into darkness.
