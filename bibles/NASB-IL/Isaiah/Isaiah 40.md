## Isaiah 40:3-8

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> A voice is calling, 'Clear the way for the LORD in the wilderness; Make smooth in the desert a highway for our God.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'Let every valley be lifted up, And every mountain and hill be made low; And let the rough ground become a plain, And the rugged terrain a broad valley;

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Then the glory of the LORD will be revealed, And all flesh will see [it] together; For the mouth of the LORD has spoken.'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> A voice says, 'Call out.' Then he answered, 'What shall I call out?' All flesh is grass, and all its loveliness is like the flower of the field.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> The grass withers, the flower fades, When the breath of the LORD blows upon it; Surely the people are grass.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> The grass withers, the flower fades, But the word of our God stands forever.

<sup>10</sup> Behold, the Lord GOD will come with might, With His arm ruling for Him. Behold, His reward is with Him And His recompense before Him.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> Like a shepherd He will tend His flock, In His arm He will gather the lambs And carry [them] in His bosom; He will gently lead the nursing [ewes].

## Isaiah 40:12-17

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> Who has measured the waters in the hollow of His hand, And marked off the heavens by the span, And calculated the dust of the earth by the measure, And weighed the mountains in a balance And the hills in a pair of scales?

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Who has directed the Spirit of the LORD, Or as His counselor has informed Him?

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> With whom did He consult and [who] gave Him understanding? And [who] taught Him in the path of justice and taught Him knowledge And informed Him of the way of understanding?

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Behold, the nations are like a drop from a bucket, And are regarded as a speck of dust on the scales; Behold, He lifts up the islands like fine dust.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Even Lebanon is not enough to burn, Nor its beasts enough for a burnt offering.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> All the nations are as nothing before Him, They are regarded by Him as less than nothing and meaningless.

## Isaiah 40:18-26

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> To whom then will you liken God? Or what likeness will you compare with Him?

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> [As for] the idol, a craftsman casts it, A goldsmith plates it with gold, And a silversmith [fashions] chains of silver.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> He who is too impoverished for [such] an offering Selects a tree that does not rot; He seeks out for himself a skillful craftsman To prepare an idol that will not totter.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> Do you not know? Have you not heard? Has it not been declared to you from the beginning? Have you not understood from the foundations of the earth?

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> It is He who sits above the circle of the earth, And its inhabitants are like grasshoppers, Who stretches out the heavens like a curtain And spreads them out like a tent to dwell in.

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> He [it is] who reduces rulers to nothing, Who makes the judges of the earth meaningless.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> Scarcely have they been planted, Scarcely have they been sown, Scarcely has their stock taken root in the earth, But He merely blows on them, and they wither, And the storm carries them away like stubble.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> 'To whom then will you liken Me That I would be [his] equal?' says the Holy One.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> Lift up your eyes on high And see who has created these [stars], The One who leads forth their host by number, He calls them all by name; Because of the greatness of His might and the strength of [His] power, Not one [of them] is missing.

## Isaiah 40:27-31

###### v27
<sup>27</sup> Why do you say, O Jacob, and assert, O Israel, 'My way is hidden from the LORD, And the justice due me escapes the notice of my God '?

###### v28
<sup>28</sup> Do you not know? Have you not heard? The Everlasting God, the LORD, the Creator of the ends of the earth Does not become weary or tired. His understanding is inscrutable.

###### v29
<sup>29</sup> He gives strength to the weary, And to [him who] lacks might He increases power.

###### v30
<sup>30</sup> Though youths grow weary and tired, And vigorous young men stumble badly,

###### v31
<sup>31</sup> Yet those who wait for the LORD Will gain new strength; They will mount up [with] wings like eagles, They will run and not get tired, They will walk and not become weary.
