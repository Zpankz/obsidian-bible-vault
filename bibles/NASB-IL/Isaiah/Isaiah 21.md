## Isaiah 21:1-10

<sup>1</sup> The oracle concerning the wilderness of the sea. As windstorms in the Negev sweep on, It comes from the wilderness, from a terrifying land.

<sup>2</sup> A harsh vision has been shown to me; The treacherous one [still] deals treacherously, and the destroyer [still] destroys. Go up, Elam, lay siege, Media; I have made an end of all the groaning she has caused.

<sup>3</sup> For this reason my loins are full of anguish; Pains have seized me like the pains of a woman in labor. I am so bewildered I cannot hear, so terrified I cannot see.

<sup>4</sup> My mind reels, horror overwhelms me; The twilight I longed for has been turned for me into trembling.

<sup>5</sup> They set the table, they spread out the cloth, they eat, they drink; 'Rise up, captains, oil the shields,'

<sup>6</sup> For thus the Lord says to me, 'Go, station the lookout, let him report what he sees.

<sup>7</sup> 'When he sees riders, horsemen in pairs, A train of donkeys, a train of camels, Let him pay close attention, very close attention.'

<sup>8</sup> Then the lookout called, 'O Lord, I stand continually by day on the watchtower, And I am stationed every night at my guard post.

<sup>9</sup> 'Now behold, here comes a troop of riders, horsemen in pairs.' And one said, 'Fallen, fallen is Babylon; And all the images of her gods are shattered on the ground.'

<sup>10</sup> O my threshed [people], and my afflicted of the threshing floor! What I have heard from the LORD of hosts, The God of Israel, I make known to you.

## Isaiah 21:13-17

<sup>13</sup> The oracle about Arabia. In the thickets of Arabia you must spend the night, O caravans of Dedanites.

<sup>14</sup> Bring water for the thirsty, O inhabitants of the land of Tema, Meet the fugitive with bread.

<sup>15</sup> For they have fled from the swords, From the drawn sword, and from the bent bow And from the press of battle.

<sup>16</sup> For thus the Lord said to me, 'In a year, as a hired man would count it, all the splendor of Kedar will terminate;

<sup>17</sup> and the remainder of the number of bowmen, the mighty men of the sons of Kedar, will be few; for the LORD God of Israel has spoken.'
