# Isaiah 47

## Isaiah 47:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> 'Come down and sit in the dust, O virgin daughter of Babylon; Sit on the ground without a throne, O daughter of the Chaldeans! For you shall no longer be called tender and delicate.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> 'Take the millstones and grind meal. Remove your veil, strip off the skirt, Uncover the leg, cross the rivers.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> 'Your nakedness will be uncovered, Your shame also will be exposed; I will take vengeance and will not spare a man.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Our Redeemer, the LORD of hosts is His name, The Holy One of Israel.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Sit silently, and go into darkness, O daughter of the Chaldeans, For you will no longer be called The queen of kingdoms.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'I was angry with My people, I profaned My heritage And gave them into your hand. You did not show mercy to them, On the aged you made your yoke very heavy.

## Isaiah 47:7-15

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'Yet you said, 'I will be a queen forever.' These things you did not consider Nor remember the outcome of them.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Now, then, hear this, you sensual one, Who dwells securely, Who says in your heart, 'I am, and there is no one besides me. I will not sit as a widow, Nor know loss of children.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'But these two things will come on you suddenly in one day: Loss of children and widowhood. They will come on you in full measure In spite of your many sorceries, In spite of the great power of your spells.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'You felt secure in your wickedness and said, 'No one sees me,' Your wisdom and your knowledge, they have deluded you; For you have said in your heart, 'I am, and there is no one besides me.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'But evil will come on you Which you will not know how to charm away; And disaster will fall on you For which you cannot atone; And destruction about which you do not know Will come on you suddenly.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Stand [fast] now in your spells And in your many sorceries With which you have labored from your youth; Perhaps you will be able to profit, Perhaps you may cause trembling.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'You are wearied with your many counsels; Let now the astrologers, Those who prophesy by the stars, Those who predict by the new moons, Stand up and save you from what will come upon you.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> 'Behold, they have become like stubble, Fire burns them; They cannot deliver themselves from the power of the flame; There will be no coal to warm by [Nor] a fire to sit before!

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> 'So have those become to you with whom you have labored, Who have trafficked with you from your youth; Each has wandered in his own way; There is none to save you.
