# Titus 2

## Titus 2:1-10

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> But as for you, speak the things which are fitting for sound doctrine.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Older men are to be temperate, dignified, sensible, sound in faith, in love, in perseverance.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Older women likewise are to be reverent in their behavior, not malicious gossips nor enslaved to much wine, teaching what is good,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> so that they may encourage the young women to love their husbands, to love their children,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> [to be] sensible, pure, workers at home, kind, being subject to their own husbands, so that the word of God will not be dishonored.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Likewise urge the young men to be sensible;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> in all things show yourself to be an example of good deeds, [with] purity in doctrine, dignified,

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> sound [in] speech which is beyond reproach, so that the opponent will be put to shame, having nothing bad to say about us.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> [Urge] bondslaves to be subject to their own masters in everything, to be well-pleasing, not argumentative,

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> not pilfering, but showing all good faith so that they will adorn the doctrine of God our Savior in every respect.

## Titus 2:11-14

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For the grace of God has appeared, bringing salvation to all men,

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> instructing us to deny ungodliness and worldly desires and to live sensibly, righteously and godly in the present age,

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> looking for the blessed hope and the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior, Christ Jesus,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> who gave Himself for us to redeem us from every lawless deed, and to purify for Himself a people for His own possession, zealous for good deeds.
