# Titus 1

## Titus 1:1-4

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Paul, a bond-servant of God and an apostle of Jesus Christ, for the faith of those chosen of God and the knowledge of the truth which is according to godliness,

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> in the hope of eternal life, which God, who cannot lie, promised long ages ago,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> but at the proper time manifested, [even] His word, in the proclamation with which I was entrusted according to the commandment of God our Savior,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> To Titus, my true child in a common faith: Grace and peace from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Savior.

## Titus 1:6-16

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> [namely], if any man is above reproach, the husband of one wife, having children who believe, not accused of dissipation or rebellion.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> For the overseer must be above reproach as God's steward, not self-willed, not quick-tempered, not addicted to wine, not pugnacious, not fond of sordid gain,

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> but hospitable, loving what is good, sensible, just, devout, self-controlled,

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> holding fast the faithful word which is in accordance with the teaching, so that he will be able both to exhort in sound doctrine and to refute those who contradict.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For there are many rebellious men, empty talkers and deceivers, especially those of the circumcision,

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> who must be silenced because they are upsetting whole families, teaching things they should not [teach] for the sake of sordid gain.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> One of themselves, a prophet of their own, said, 'Cretans are always liars, evil beasts, lazy gluttons.'

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> This testimony is true. For this reason reprove them severely so that they may be sound in the faith,

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> not paying attention to Jewish myths and commandments of men who turn away from the truth.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> To the pure, all things are pure; but to those who are defiled and unbelieving, nothing is pure, but both their mind and their conscience are defiled.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> They profess to know God, but by [their] deeds they deny [Him], being detestable and disobedient and worthless for any good deed.
