# 1 Corinthians 13

## 1 Corinthians 13:1-3

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> If I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, but do not have love, I have become a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> If I have [the gift of] prophecy, and know all mysteries and all knowledge; and if I have all faith, so as to remove mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And if I give all my possessions to feed [the poor], and if I surrender my body to be burned, but do not have love, it profits me nothing.

## 1 Corinthians 13:4-7

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Love is patient, love is kind [and] is not jealous; love does not brag [and] is not arrogant,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> does not act unbecomingly; it does not seek its own, is not provoked, does not take into account a wrong [suffered],

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> does not rejoice in unrighteousness, but rejoices with the truth;

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.

## 1 Corinthians 13:8-13

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Love never fails; but if [there are gifts of] prophecy, they will be done away; if [there are] tongues, they will cease; if [there is] knowledge, it will be done away.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> For we know in part and we prophesy in part;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> but when the perfect comes, the partial will be done away.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> When I was a child, I used to speak like a child, think like a child, reason like a child; when I became a man, I did away with childish things.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> For now we see in a mirror dimly, but then face to face; now I know in part, but then I will know fully just as I also have been fully known.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> But now faith, hope, love, abide these three; but the greatest of these is love.
