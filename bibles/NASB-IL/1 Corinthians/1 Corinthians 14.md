# 1 Corinthians 14

## 1 Corinthians 14:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Pursue love, yet desire earnestly spiritual [gifts], but especially that you may prophesy.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For one who speaks in a tongue does not speak to men but to God; for no one understands, but in [his] spirit he speaks mysteries.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> But one who prophesies speaks to men for edification and exhortation and consolation.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> One who speaks in a tongue edifies himself; but one who prophesies edifies the church.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Now I wish that you all spoke in tongues, but [even] more that you would prophesy; and greater is one who prophesies than one who speaks in tongues, unless he interprets, so that the church may receive edifying.

## 1 Corinthians 14:6-14

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> But now, brethren, if I come to you speaking in tongues, what will I profit you unless I speak to you either by way of revelation or of knowledge or of prophecy or of teaching?

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Yet [even] lifeless things, either flute or harp, in producing a sound, if they do not produce a distinction in the tones, how will it be known what is played on the flute or on the harp?

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> For if the bugle produces an indistinct sound, who will prepare himself for battle?

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> So also you, unless you utter by the tongue speech that is clear, how will it be known what is spoken? For you will be speaking into the air.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> There are, perhaps, a great many kinds of languages in the world, and no [kind] is without meaning.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> If then I do not know the meaning of the language, I will be to the one who speaks a barbarian, and the one who speaks will be a barbarian to me.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> So also you, since you are zealous of spiritual [gifts], seek to abound for the edification of the church.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Therefore let one who speaks in a tongue pray that he may interpret.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> For if I pray in a tongue, my spirit prays, but my mind is unfruitful.

## 1 Corinthians 14:15-20

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> What is [the outcome] then? I will pray with the spirit and I will pray with the mind also; I will sing with the spirit and I will sing with the mind also.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> Otherwise if you bless in the spirit [only], how will the one who fills the place of the ungifted say the 'Amen ' at your giving of thanks, since he does not know what you are saying?

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> For you are giving thanks well enough, but the other person is not edified.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> I thank God, I speak in tongues more than you all;

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> however, in the church I desire to speak five words with my mind so that I may instruct others also, rather than ten thousand words in a tongue.

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> Brethren, do not be children in your thinking; yet in evil be infants, but in your thinking be mature.
