# 1 Corinthians 5

## 1 Corinthians 5:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> It is actually reported that there is immorality among you, and immorality of such a kind as does not exist even among the Gentiles, that someone has his father's wife.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> You have become arrogant and have not mourned instead, so that the one who had done this deed would be removed from your midst.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> For I, on my part, though absent in body but present in spirit, have already judged him who has so committed this, as though I were present.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> In the name of our Lord Jesus, when you are assembled, and I with you in spirit, with the power of our Lord Jesus,

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> [I have decided] to deliver such a one to Satan for the destruction of his flesh, so that his spirit may be saved in the day of the Lord Jesus.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Your boasting is not good. Do you not know that a little leaven leavens the whole lump [of dough]?

## 1 Corinthians 5:9-13

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> I wrote you in my letter not to associate with immoral people;

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> I [did] not at all [mean] with the immoral people of this world, or with the covetous and swindlers, or with idolaters, for then you would have to go out of the world.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> But actually, I wrote to you not to associate with any so-called brother if he is an immoral person, or covetous, or an idolater, or a reviler, or a drunkard, or a swindler-- not even to eat with such a one.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> For what have I to do with judging outsiders? Do you not judge those who are within [the church]?

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> But those who are outside, God judges. REMOVE THE WICKED MAN FROM AMONG YOURSELVES.
