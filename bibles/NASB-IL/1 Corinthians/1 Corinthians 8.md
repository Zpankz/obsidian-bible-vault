# 1 Corinthians 8

## 1 Corinthians 8:1-3

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now concerning things sacrificed to idols, we know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge makes arrogant, but love edifies.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> If anyone supposes that he knows anything, he has not yet known as he ought to know;

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> but if anyone loves God, he is known by Him.

## 1 Corinthians 8:7-13

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> However not all men have this knowledge; but some, being accustomed to the idol until now, eat [food] as if it were sacrificed to an idol; and their conscience being weak is defiled.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> But food will not commend us to God; we are neither the worse if we do not eat, nor the better if we do eat.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> But take care that this liberty of yours does not somehow become a stumbling block to the weak.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> For if someone sees you, who have knowledge, dining in an idol's temple, will not his conscience, if he is weak, be strengthened to eat things sacrificed to idols?

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> For through your knowledge he who is weak is ruined, the brother for whose sake Christ died.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> And so, by sinning against the brethren and wounding their conscience when it is weak, you sin against Christ.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Therefore, if food causes my brother to stumble, I will never eat meat again, so that I will not cause my brother to stumble.
