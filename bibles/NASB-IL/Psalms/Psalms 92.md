## Psalms 92:1-6

<sup>1</sup> A Psalm, a Song for the Sabbath day. It is good to give thanks to the LORD And to sing praises to Your name, O Most High;[^1][^2][^3]

<sup>2</sup> To declare Your lovingkindness in the morning And Your faithfulness by night,[^4][^5][^6][^7][^8][^9][^10][^11]

<sup>3</sup> With the ten-stringed lute and with the harp, With resounding music upon the lyre.

<sup>4</sup> For You, O LORD, have made me glad by what You have done, I will sing for joy at the works of Your hands.[^12][^13][^14][^15]

<sup>5</sup> How great are Your works, O LORD! Your thoughts are very deep.[^16][^17][^18][^19][^20][^21][^22][^23]

<sup>6</sup> A senseless man has no knowledge, Nor does a stupid man understand this:[^24][^25][^26]

## Psalms 92:7-15

<sup>7</sup> That when the wicked sprouted up like grass And all who did iniquity flourished, It [was only] that they might be destroyed forevermore.[^27][^28][^29][^30][^31][^32][^33][^34]

<sup>8</sup> But You, O LORD, are on high forever.

<sup>9</sup> For, behold, Your enemies, O LORD, For, behold, Your enemies will perish; All who do iniquity will be scattered.

<sup>10</sup> But You have exalted my horn like [that of] the wild ox; I have been anointed with fresh oil.[^35][^36][^37][^38][^39][^40][^41][^42]

<sup>11</sup> And my eye has looked [exultantly] upon my foes, My ears hear of the evildoers who rise up against me.[^43][^44]

<sup>12</sup> The righteous man will flourish like the palm tree, He will grow like a cedar in Lebanon.[^45][^46][^47][^48][^49][^50][^51][^52]

<sup>13</sup> Planted in the house of the LORD, They will flourish in the courts of our God.[^53][^54][^55]

<sup>14</sup> They will still yield fruit in old age; They shall be full of sap and very green,[^56][^57][^58]

<sup>15</sup> To declare that the LORD is upright; [He is] my rock, and there is no unrighteousness in Him.[^59]
