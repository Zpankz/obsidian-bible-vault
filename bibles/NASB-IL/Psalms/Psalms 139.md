## Psalms 139:1-6

<sup>1</sup> For the choir director. A Psalm of David. O LORD, You have searched me and known [me].[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6][^7][^8]

<sup>2</sup> You know when I sit down and when I rise up; You understand my thought from afar.[^9][^10][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15]

<sup>3</sup> You scrutinize my path and my lying down, And are intimately acquainted with all my ways.[^16][^17]

<sup>4</sup> Even before there is a word on my tongue, Behold, O LORD, You know it all.

<sup>5</sup> You have enclosed me behind and before, And laid Your hand upon me.[^18][^19]

<sup>6</sup> [Such] knowledge is too wonderful for me; It is [too] high, I cannot attain to it.[^20][^21]

## Psalms 139:7-16

<sup>7</sup> Where can I go from Your Spirit? Or where can I flee from Your presence?[^22][^23][^24]

<sup>8</sup> If I ascend to heaven, You are there; If I make my bed in Sheol, behold, You are there.[^25][^26][^27]

<sup>9</sup> If I take the wings of the dawn, If I dwell in the remotest part of the sea,

<sup>10</sup> Even there Your hand will lead me, And Your right hand will lay hold of me.[^28][^29][^30][^31][^32]

<sup>11</sup> If I say, 'Surely the darkness will overwhelm me, And the light around me will be night,'[^33][^34]

<sup>12</sup> Even the darkness is not dark to You, And the night is as bright as the day. Darkness and light are alike [to You].[^35]

<sup>13</sup> For You formed my inward parts; You wove me in my mother's womb.[^36][^37][^38][^39][^40][^41][^42][^43]

<sup>14</sup> I will give thanks to You, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made; Wonderful are Your works, And my soul knows it very well.[^44][^45][^46][^47][^48][^49][^50]

<sup>15</sup> My frame was not hidden from You, When I was made in secret, [And] skillfully wrought in the depths of the earth;[^51][^52][^53]

<sup>16</sup> Your eyes have seen my unformed substance; And in Your book were all written The days that were ordained [for me], When as yet there was not one of them.[^54][^55][^56]

## Psalms 139:17-24

<sup>17</sup> How precious also are Your thoughts to me, O God! How vast is the sum of them![^57][^58][^59][^60][^61][^62][^63]

<sup>18</sup> If I should count them, they would outnumber the sand. When I awake, I am still with You.[^64][^65]

<sup>19</sup> O that You would slay the wicked, O God; Depart from me, therefore, men of bloodshed.

<sup>20</sup> For they speak against You wickedly, And Your enemies take [Your name] in vain.

<sup>21</sup> Do I not hate those who hate You, O LORD? And do I not loathe those who rise up against You?[^66]

<sup>22</sup> I hate them with the utmost hatred; They have become my enemies.

<sup>23</sup> Search me, O God, and know my heart; Try me and know my anxious thoughts;[^67][^68][^69][^70][^71][^72][^73][^74]

<sup>24</sup> And see if there be any hurtful way in me, And lead me in the everlasting way.[^75][^76][^77][^78][^79][^80][^81][^82]
