# Psalms 6

## Psalms 6:1-7

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> For the choir director; with stringed instruments, upon an eight-string lyre. A Psalm of David. O LORD, do not rebuke me in Your anger, Nor chasten me in Your wrath.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Be gracious to me, O LORD, for I [am] pining away; Heal me, O LORD, for my bones are dismayed.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And my soul is greatly dismayed; But You, O LORD-- how long?

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Return, O LORD, rescue my soul; Save me because of Your lovingkindness.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> For there is no mention of You in death; In Sheol who will give You thanks?

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> I am weary with my sighing; Every night I make my bed swim, I dissolve my couch with my tears.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> My eye has wasted away with grief; It has become old because of all my adversaries.

## Psalms 6:8-10

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> Depart from me, all you who do iniquity, For the LORD has heard the voice of my weeping.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> The LORD has heard my supplication, The LORD receives my prayer.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> All my enemies will be ashamed and greatly dismayed; They shall turn back, they will suddenly be ashamed.
