# Psalms 41

## Psalms 41:1-4

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> For the choir director. A Psalm of David. How blessed is he who considers the helpless; The LORD will deliver him in a day of trouble.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> The LORD will protect him and keep him alive, And he shall be called blessed upon the earth; And do not give him over to the desire of his enemies.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> The LORD will sustain him upon his sickbed; In his illness, You restore him to health.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> As for me, I said, 'O LORD, be gracious to me; Heal my soul, for I have sinned against You.'

## Psalms 41:5-13

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> My enemies speak evil against me, 'When will he die, and his name perish?'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> And when he comes to see [me], he speaks falsehood; His heart gathers wickedness to itself; When he goes outside, he tells it.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> All who hate me whisper together against me; Against me they devise my hurt, [saying],

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'A wicked thing is poured out upon him, That when he lies down, he will not rise up again.'

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Even my close friend in whom I trusted, Who ate my bread, Has lifted up his heel against me.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> But You, O LORD, be gracious to me and raise me up, That I may repay them.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> By this I know that You are pleased with me, Because my enemy does not shout in triumph over me.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> As for me, You uphold me in my integrity, And You set me in Your presence forever.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> Blessed be the LORD, the God of Israel, From everlasting to everlasting. Amen and Amen.
