# Psalms 4

## Psalms 4:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> For the choir director; on stringed instruments. A Psalm of David. Answer me when I call, O God of my righteousness! You have relieved me in my distress; Be gracious to me and hear my prayer.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> O sons of men, how long will my honor become a reproach? [How long] will you love what is worthless and aim at deception? Selah.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> But know that the LORD has set apart the godly man for Himself; The LORD hears when I call to Him.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> Tremble, and do not sin; Meditate in your heart upon your bed, and be still. Selah.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Offer the sacrifices of righteousness, And trust in the LORD.

## Psalms 4:6-8

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Many are saying, 'Who will show us [any] good?' Lift up the light of Your countenance upon us, O LORD!

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> You have put gladness in my heart, More than when their grain and new wine abound.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> In peace I will both lie down and sleep, For You alone, O LORD, make me to dwell in safety.
