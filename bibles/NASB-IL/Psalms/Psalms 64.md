# Psalms 64

## Psalms 64:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> For the choir director. A Psalm of David. Hear my voice, O God, in my complaint; Preserve my life from dread of the enemy.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Hide me from the secret counsel of evildoers, From the tumult of those who do iniquity,

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Who have sharpened their tongue like a sword. They aimed bitter speech [as] their arrow,

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> To shoot from concealment at the blameless; Suddenly they shoot at him, and do not fear.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> They hold fast to themselves an evil purpose; They talk of laying snares secretly; They say, 'Who can see them?'

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> They devise injustices, [saying], 'We are ready with a well-conceived plot'; For the inward thought and the heart of a man are deep.

## Psalms 64:7-10

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> But God will shoot at them with an arrow; Suddenly they will be wounded.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> So they will make him stumble; Their own tongue is against them; All who see them will shake the head.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> Then all men will fear, And they will declare the work of God, And will consider what He has done.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> The righteous man will be glad in the LORD and will take refuge in Him; And all the upright in heart will glory.
