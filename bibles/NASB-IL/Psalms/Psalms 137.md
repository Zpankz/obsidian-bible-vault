# Psalms 137

## Psalms 137:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> By the rivers of Babylon, There we sat down and wept, When we remembered Zion.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Upon the willows in the midst of it We hung our harps.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> For there our captors demanded of us songs, And our tormentors mirth, [saying], 'Sing us one of the songs of Zion.'

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> How can we sing the LORD'S song In a foreign land?

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> If I forget you, O Jerusalem, May my right hand forget [her skill].

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> May my tongue cling to the roof of my mouth If I do not remember you, If I do not exalt Jerusalem Above my chief joy.

## Psalms 137:7-9

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> Remember, O LORD, against the sons of Edom The day of Jerusalem, Who said, 'Raze it, raze it To its very foundation.'

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> O daughter of Babylon, you devastated one, How blessed will be the one who repays you With the recompense with which you have repaid us.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> How blessed will be the one who seizes and dashes your little ones Against the rock.
