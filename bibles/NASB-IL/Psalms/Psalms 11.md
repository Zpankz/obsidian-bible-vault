# Psalms 11

## Psalms 11:1-3

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> For the choir director. [A Psalm] of David. In the LORD I take refuge; How can you say to my soul, 'Flee [as] a bird to your mountain;

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> For, behold, the wicked bend the bow, They make ready their arrow upon the string To shoot in darkness at the upright in heart.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> If the foundations are destroyed, What can the righteous do?'

## Psalms 11:4-7

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> The LORD is in His holy temple; the LORD'S throne is in heaven; His eyes behold, His eyelids test the sons of men.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> The LORD tests the righteous and the wicked, And the one who loves violence His soul hates.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Upon the wicked He will rain snares; Fire and brimstone and burning wind will be the portion of their cup.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> For the LORD is righteous, He loves righteousness; The upright will behold His face.
