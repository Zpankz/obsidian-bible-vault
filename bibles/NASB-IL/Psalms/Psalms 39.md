# Psalms 39

## Psalms 39:1-6

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> For the choir director, for Jeduthun. A Psalm of David. I said, 'I will guard my ways That I may not sin with my tongue; I will guard my mouth as with a muzzle While the wicked are in my presence.'

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> I was mute and silent, I refrained [even] from good, And my sorrow grew worse.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> My heart was hot within me, While I was musing the fire burned; [Then] I spoke with my tongue:

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> 'LORD, make me to know my end And what is the extent of my days; Let me know how transient I am.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> 'Behold, You have made my days [as] handbreadths, And my lifetime as nothing in Your sight; Surely every man at his best is a mere breath. Selah.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> 'Surely every man walks about as a phantom; Surely they make an uproar for nothing; He amasses [riches] and does not know who will gather them.

## Psalms 39:7-13

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> 'And now, Lord, for what do I wait? My hope is in You.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> 'Deliver me from all my transgressions; Make me not the reproach of the foolish.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'I have become mute, I do not open my mouth, Because it is You who have done [it].

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> 'Remove Your plague from me; Because of the opposition of Your hand I am perishing.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> 'With reproofs You chasten a man for iniquity; You consume as a moth what is precious to him; Surely every man is a mere breath. Selah.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Hear my prayer, O LORD, and give ear to my cry; Do not be silent at my tears; For I am a stranger with You, A sojourner like all my fathers.

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> 'Turn Your gaze away from me, that I may smile [again] Before I depart and am no more.'
