# Ruth 1

## Ruth 1:1-5

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> Now it came about in the days when the judges governed, that there was a famine in the land. And a certain man of Bethlehem in Judah went to sojourn in the land of Moab with his wife and his two sons.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> The name of the man [was] Elimelech, and the name of his wife, Naomi; and the names of his two sons [were] Mahlon and Chilion, Ephrathites of Bethlehem in Judah. Now they entered the land of Moab and remained there.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> Then Elimelech, Naomi's husband, died; and she was left with her two sons.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> They took for themselves Moabite women [as] wives; the name of the one was Orpah and the name of the other Ruth. And they lived there about ten years.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> Then both Mahlon and Chilion also died, and the woman was bereft of her two children and her husband.

## Ruth 1:6-18

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> Then she arose with her daughters-in-law that she might return from the land of Moab, for she had heard in the land of Moab that the LORD had visited His people in giving them food.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> So she departed from the place where she was, and her two daughters-in-law with her; and they went on the way to return to the land of Judah.

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> And Naomi said to her two daughters-in-law, 'Go, return each of you to her mother's house. May the LORD deal kindly with you as you have dealt with the dead and with me.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> 'May the LORD grant that you may find rest, each in the house of her husband.' Then she kissed them, and they lifted up their voices and wept.

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> And they said to her, '[No], but we will surely return with you to your people.'

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> But Naomi said, 'Return, my daughters. Why should you go with me? Have I yet sons in my womb, that they may be your husbands?

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> 'Return, my daughters! Go, for I am too old to have a husband. If I said I have hope, if I should even have a husband tonight and also bear sons,

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> would you therefore wait until they were grown? Would you therefore refrain from marrying? No, my daughters; for it is harder for me than for you, for the hand of the LORD has gone forth against me.'

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> And they lifted up their voices and wept again; and Orpah kissed her mother-in-law, but Ruth clung to her.

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> Then she said, 'Behold, your sister-in-law has gone back to her people and her gods; return after your sister-in-law.'

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> But Ruth said, 'Do not urge me to leave you [or] turn back from following you; for where you go, I will go, and where you lodge, I will lodge. Your people [shall be] my people, and your God, my God.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> 'Where you die, I will die, and there I will be buried. Thus may the LORD do to me, and worse, if [anything but] death parts you and me.'

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> When she saw that she was determined to go with her, she said no more to her.

## Ruth 1:19-22

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> So they both went until they came to Bethlehem. And when they had come to Bethlehem, all the city was stirred because of them, and the women said, 'Is this Naomi?'

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> She said to them, 'Do not call me Naomi; call me Mara, for the Almighty has dealt very bitterly with me.

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> 'I went out full, but the LORD has brought me back empty. Why do you call me Naomi, since the LORD has witnessed against me and the Almighty has afflicted me?'

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> So Naomi returned, and with her Ruth the Moabitess, her daughter-in-law, who returned from the land of Moab. And they came to Bethlehem at the beginning of barley harvest.
