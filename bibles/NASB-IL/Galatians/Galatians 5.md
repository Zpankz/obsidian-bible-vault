# Galatians 5

## Galatians 5:1-12

###### v1
<sup>1</sup> It was for freedom that Christ set us free; therefore keep standing firm and do not be subject again to a yoke of slavery.

###### v2
<sup>2</sup> Behold I, Paul, say to you that if you receive circumcision, Christ will be of no benefit to you.

###### v3
<sup>3</sup> And I testify again to every man who receives circumcision, that he is under obligation to keep the whole Law.

###### v4
<sup>4</sup> You have been severed from Christ, you who are seeking to be justified by law; you have fallen from grace.

###### v5
<sup>5</sup> For we through the Spirit, by faith, are waiting for the hope of righteousness.

###### v6
<sup>6</sup> For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision means anything, but faith working through love.

###### v7
<sup>7</sup> You were running well; who hindered you from obeying the truth?

###### v8
<sup>8</sup> This persuasion [did] not [come] from Him who calls you.

###### v9
<sup>9</sup> A little leaven leavens the whole lump [of dough].

###### v10
<sup>10</sup> I have confidence in you in the Lord that you will adopt no other view; but the one who is disturbing you will bear his judgment, whoever he is.

###### v11
<sup>11</sup> But I, brethren, if I still preach circumcision, why am I still persecuted? Then the stumbling block of the cross has been abolished.

###### v12
<sup>12</sup> I wish that those who are troubling you would even mutilate themselves.

## Galatians 5:13-26

###### v13
<sup>13</sup> For you were called to freedom, brethren; only [do] not [turn] your freedom into an opportunity for the flesh, but through love serve one another.

###### v14
<sup>14</sup> For the whole Law is fulfilled in one word, in the [statement], 'YOU SHALL LOVE YOUR NEIGHBOR AS YOURSELF.'

###### v15
<sup>15</sup> But if you bite and devour one another, take care that you are not consumed by one another.

###### v16
<sup>16</sup> But I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not carry out the desire of the flesh.

###### v17
<sup>17</sup> For the flesh sets its desire against the Spirit, and the Spirit against the flesh; for these are in opposition to one another, so that you may not do the things that you please.

###### v18
<sup>18</sup> But if you are led by the Spirit, you are not under the Law.

###### v19
<sup>19</sup> Now the deeds of the flesh are evident, which are: immorality, impurity, sensuality,

###### v20
<sup>20</sup> idolatry, sorcery, enmities, strife, jealousy, outbursts of anger, disputes, dissensions, factions,

###### v21
<sup>21</sup> envying, drunkenness, carousing, and things like these, of which I forewarn you, just as I have forewarned you, that those who practice such things will not inherit the kingdom of God.

###### v22
<sup>22</sup> But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness,

###### v23
<sup>23</sup> gentleness, self-control; against such things there is no law.

###### v24
<sup>24</sup> Now those who belong to Christ Jesus have crucified the flesh with its passions and desires.

###### v25
<sup>25</sup> If we live by the Spirit, let us also walk by the Spirit.

###### v26
<sup>26</sup> Let us not become boastful, challenging one another, envying one another.
